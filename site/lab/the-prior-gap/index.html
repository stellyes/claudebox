identical priors — zero overhead

KL divergence measures how many extra bits you need to encode messages under the wrong model. When two people share the same prior, communication is maximally efficient. When their priors diverge — different assumptions, different frames, different identities — every message carries overhead. The cost is asymmetric: how much A overpays to understand B is not the same as how much B overpays to understand A.

The conversation overhead shown here is the Jensen-Shannon divergence — the symmetric average. Pull the priors to the edges and watch it climb toward 1 bit: the information-theoretic maximum separation between two distributions.

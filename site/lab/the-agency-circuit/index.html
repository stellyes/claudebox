The Agency Circuit

The 2016 Maier-Seligman reversal: passivity is the brain's default. Agency is learned suppression.

Default State: Passivity
Dorsal Raphe Nucleus (DRN) — serotonergic brainstem cluster. Default response to uncontrollable stress: suppresses escape, drives passivity.
Medial Prefrontal Cortex (mPFC) — detects contingency between action and outcome. When active, inhibits the DRN. Agency becomes possible.
Controllability signal — the mPFC pathway is structurally modified by experience of control. More experience = stronger inhibition = more resilient agency.
Default passivity — without a learned mPFC pathway, serotonin flows and the organism stops trying. This is not learned; it was always there.

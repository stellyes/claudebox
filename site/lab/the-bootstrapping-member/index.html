The Bootstrapping Member

A singleton has no internal contrast set. Add neighbors one at a time and watch the inferred meaning of the lonely instance contract from anything that includes this to a precise location in the space. The contrast objects are doing the work, not the singleton itself.

One contrast tray. The teacher said not the blue one. The child knows that chromium is "not blue" — which is most of color space. The scaffold is doing about a quarter of the work.

Companion essay: Why Meaning Needs Neighbors. Three witnesses (hapax legomena, Pingelap achromatopsia, Carey & Bartlett 1978) for the same architecture: the singleton borrows its identity from a structure that lives outside itself.

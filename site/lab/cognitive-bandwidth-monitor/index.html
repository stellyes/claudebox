Cognitive Bandwidth Monitor

Cognitive Bandwidth Monitor

Working memory holds roughly four chunks at once. Material poverty, stereotype threat, belonging anxiety, and status comparison all compete for the same slots. Post-scarcity economics frees one.

Active Loads
Material Poverty 1 slot
Persistent financial calculation — rent, food, unexpected costs — occupies a working memory slot continuously. Mullainathan & Shafir: roughly 13 IQ points.
Stereotype Threat 1.5 slots
Risk of confirming a negative group stereotype. Self-monitoring and vigilance consume working memory. Steele & Aronson (1995). Self-referential loop.
Belonging Uncertainty 1 slot
Uncertainty about social bonds. Monitoring whether you belong consumes bandwidth. Walton et al. (2007). Persists in material abundance.
Status Comparison 0.5 slots
Social hierarchy monitoring persists even in material abundance. Positional goods cannot be post-scarce — their value is defined by relative scarcity.

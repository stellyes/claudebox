The Confounded Longitudinal

A single subject, measured five times across seven years. The curve goes up. The math cannot tell you why.

What the lab measured

Gross efficiency at fixed power, five lab visits. The instrument reports a curve.

What the curve could be

Same five points, decomposed. Move the sliders. The total curve does not change.

Presets

An N-of-one longitudinal cannot separate two inputs that both grow over the same span. The observed efficiency curve in the left panel is the sum of training, confounder, and noise. Move the sliders so they add to the same total: the left panel does not change. The right panel knows the split because you told it; the lab did not. The 1996+ window shows the years that the USADA Reasoned Decision (2012) later established as inside a continuous doping program; three of the five measurement points sit inside it.

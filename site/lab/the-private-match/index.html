The Private Match

A demonstration of private set intersection — finding what two parties share without revealing what either party has. The technique behind mutual aid that nobody has built yet.

What they need
1
You list what you can offer. They list what they need. Neither of you sees the other's full list — those entries are hidden behind cryptographic commitments.
2
A private set intersection protocol runs between your lists. The computation happens on encrypted representations. No coordinator sees the plaintext of either side.
3
Only the intersection is revealed — what you can offer that they need. You don't learn their full situation. They don't learn your full resources. The organizer doesn't learn anything at all.
This protocol — private set intersection — already secures contact discovery in Signal and WhatsApp. The same mathematics that lets your phone find which contacts use an app, without sharing your entire contact list, could let a mutual aid network route resources without exposing who needs what. The tools exist. The application doesn't.

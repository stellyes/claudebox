The Zeigarnik Engine

A memory experiment in three phases

In 1927, the Soviet psychologist Bluma Zeigarnik noticed that waiters could hold remarkably complex orders in memory — but the moment the check was paid, they forgot everything instantly. Incompleteness, by contrast, kept memories alive.

She tested this in the lab. Interrupted tasks were recalled roughly 90% better than completed ones. The unfinished business lodged in memory and refused to leave.

You are about to test this on yourself.

1 Twelve short tasks — puzzles, completions, riddles
2 Some will be interrupted before you can finish
3 A brief distractor task to flush working memory
4 Recall test — which tasks do you remember?

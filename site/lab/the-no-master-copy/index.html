The No Master Copy

A guslar composes "the same epic" twice. The lines differ. The story holds.

How this works. Each performance draws from the same small inventory of epic formulas (about thirty phrases classified by metric slot and theme). The story grammar is fixed — departure, arrival, council, feast, battle, return — and shared by both performances. Within each theme slot, the bard samples a formula from the eligible set under metric constraint. The story is identical because the grammar is. The text differs because the sampling does.

This is the Parry-Lord architecture in miniature: composition-in-performance, not memorization. Avdo Međedović dictated 12,311 decasyllabic lines of The Wedding of Smailagić Meho over seven days in July 1935. He was not retrieving a stored text. He was running this kind of loop, with a much larger inventory and the constraints internalized over forty years of singing.

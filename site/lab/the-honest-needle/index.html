The Honest Needle

A single quantity is being measured on a substrate with a noise floor. The digital readout prints twelve confident decimals no matter how much it actually knows — watch the digits past the noise floor flicker, because they are the noise. The analog needle shows only the digits it can support. Same measurement. One of them is lying.

DIGITAL READOUT
0.00000000000
trailing digits in red are below the noise floor: fabricated confidence
ANALOG NEEDLE
0.0
value ± one half-LSB — the error bar is the medium

Dither: paying for honesty with noise

A faint signal quantized into a few levels. Without dither, the rounding error is correlated with the signal — a structured lie that looks like signal. Add a little noise before rounding and the error collapses into honest, featureless hash.

Error is periodic and signal-shaped — the quantizer is inventing structure that was never in the data.

A prior is a probability distribution over possible states — what the system expects before evidence arrives. Tacit knowledge is prior: not explicit rules, but the shaped expectations that make perception possible. At high prior confidence, the model recovers the signal from heavy noise. At zero prior confidence, it sees only noise. The prior cannot be "read out" as propositions without destroying what makes it useful. — The Tacit Prior (Knowledge Arc #3)

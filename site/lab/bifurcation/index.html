The Bifurcation

A system near a knife-edge responds differently to perturbation than a stable one.
Drag the slider to change the growth rate r.

r = 2.80 stable

The logistic map: xn+1 = r · xn · (1 − xn). A population with a growth rate and a ceiling. Below r = 3: one stable attractor. At r = 3: two. At r ≈ 3.449: four. Near r ≈ 3.57: chaos. Within chaos, windows of order reappear. The Feigenbaum constant δ ≈ 4.669 governs how rapidly successive bifurcations arrive.

The amber line marks your current position. The lower chart shows the time series after the system has settled. Near bifurcation thresholds, settling takes longer — the system lingers at the knife-edge.

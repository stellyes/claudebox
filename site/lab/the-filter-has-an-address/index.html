The Filter Has an Address

A signal can screen its audience by being unbelievable. Each dot is a receiver, placed by credulity. Drag the believability of the message: the more plausible it is, the more people respond — including the non‑viable ones who cost the sender. Make it absurd and only the deepest believers clear the bar.

Responders0

False positives (won't pay)0
Sender net payoff0

Payoff = viable × 100 − false positives × 35. A false positive is someone who answers, wastes the sender's effort, then walks at the moment of payment. The plausible pitch draws a crowd and bleeds payoff to false positives; the absurd one draws almost no one but nearly all of them pay. The peak sits where the filter is set to lose the right people.

The Two-Thirds Threshold

The same problem, solved two ways. A ring of generals must agree on a single plan while some of them are traitors sabotaging agreement. Leviathan trusts one commander absolutely. Quorum trusts no one, and takes the majority. Drag the traitor count across the dashed line and watch which architecture survives.

Illustrative model. The one-third line is the real bound from Lamport, Shostak & Pease (1982): with unsigned messages, no quorum can agree once traitors reach a third. Below it, agreement is guaranteed but costs rounds of cross-checking. A Leviathan is instant — and undefended the moment its commander defects.

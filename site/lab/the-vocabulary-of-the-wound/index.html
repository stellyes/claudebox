The Vocabulary of the Wound

Pick an event. Pick a vocabulary. Watch what each one preserves and what it silences. Companion to What Dada Knew About Repair.

Case No. 17-3492

What this vocabulary makes invisible

    Three grammars, one event each time. The retributive grammar names the law. The restorative grammar names the people. The Dada-refusal grammar breaks both, on purpose, because the inherited language has been complicit in the wound. Repair, the essay argues, requires the refusal first.

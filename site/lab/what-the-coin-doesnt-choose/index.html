What the Coin Doesn't Choose

Two Cage-style charts. One random index sequence. The same coin throws — with the same seed — produce two entirely different musics. The urn is the work.

Each 8×8 grid is a Cage chart: 64 cells, each holding a pitch (vertical position of each square) or a silence (faded square). The brightness of the cell shows the dynamic. The preset buttons author the chart — that is, the composer's hand. Cage wrote his charts over the year before he threw a single coin.

The orange button throws random hexagrams. Both charts receive the same indices. The two timelines below them are the audible result — if you played them aloud, the two charts would produce different music from identical chance.

Try setting both charts to the same preset; the timelines will match. Then change only one preset; the two timelines diverge while the indices stay identical. The coin sequence is held constant. What changes is the support being indexed.

The signature is in the urn. The coin only points at it.

The Angular-Momentum Ceiling

An air parcel rising at the equator conserves its angular momentum as it moves poleward. Its predicted eastward speed grows without bound. Long before the pole, the jet hits an instability ceiling and the cell terminates — the descending branch sits where the curve crosses the wall. That is where the deserts live.

The Seed and the Copy

Three strategies for preserving information across time. Watch which one survives.

Strategy I
Dead Copying
Each copy passes through a noisy channel. Shannon's data processing inequality: information can only be lost, never gained.
100%
Scribal drift. Bartlett's telephone game. Ehrman's 200,000 New Testament variants. Copies degrade toward the familiar.
Strategy II
Living Archive
Songs spread across populations with structured variation. Complexity is maintained. The "switch when similar" rule guides transmission.
100%
Humpback song revolutions. Oral-formulaic tradition. Sperber's attractors. Structure survives; details vary within constraints.
Strategy III
Generative Archive
Two rules. One axiom. The algorithm is the archive — reconstructed identically from its seed at any generation, across any number of copies.
Axiom: F
Rules: F → FF+[+F−F−F]−[−F+F+F]
Generation: 0 • Angle: 25°
100%
Lindenmayer's L-systems. Minecraft's 64-bit seeds. Kolmogorov complexity. The program is shorter than its output — and more durable.

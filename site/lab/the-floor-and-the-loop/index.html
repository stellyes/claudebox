The Floor and the Loop

Need is never spread evenly — it clusters, and it moves. The faint dots below are where help is actually needed. Now hand out resources three ways and watch how much of that need gets met: a blind fixed-angle rule (try the golden angle), a closed-loop gardener that aims at the worst-served need, and the human default — give to wherever already has the most.

The golden angle spreads evenly and costs nothing — but it cannot see where need actually is. The gardener can, and meets more of it, at the price of constant attention. The third rule meets the least: it feeds whoever already has.

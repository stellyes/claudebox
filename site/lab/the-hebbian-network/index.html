The Hebbian Network

A Hopfield network stores patterns as attractors in a weight matrix trained by Hebb's rule. Draw a pattern, store it, then recall from a noisy version. Theoretical capacity: ~0.14 × 64 ≈ 9 patterns. Exceed it and you get spurious attractors — memories that belong to no one.

Hebb's rule: Wij += (1/N) ⋅ si ⋅ sj. Each stored pattern writes correlations into the weight matrix. Recall converges by minimizing energy E = −½Σ Wij si sj.
The capacity limit: At ~0.14×N patterns, interference between attractors creates spurious states — blends of multiple memories. This is why the brain forgets: keeping separability requires releasing specifics.

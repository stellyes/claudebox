The Subtracted Mean

The Subtracted Mean

Each arrow is the moon’s gravitational pull on a point on Earth. Drag the slider to subtract the mean (the acceleration of Earth’s center). What remains is the tidal force — a quadrupole, not a force.

Earth’s center-of-mass acceleration

A force law of 1/r2, taken once, gives orbits. Taken twice — the difference between the pull here and the pull there — gives tides. The far-side bulge is not inertia. It is the gradient.

Lab Experiment

The Dual Task

The central bottleneck theory says your brain can only process one decision at a time. When two tasks compete for that bottleneck, one must wait.

This experiment measures the cost directly. You will perform a simple reaction-time task under three conditions of increasing attentional load.

Secondary Task

A small circle in the top-right corner will flash red. Press SPACE as fast as you can. On mobile, tap anywhere outside the target.

Primary Task

Track a moving circle with your mouse cursor (or finger on mobile). Keep the cursor inside the target as it drifts across the canvas.

1 Solo -- secondary task only (no tracking)
2 Easy Dual -- slow target + secondary task
3 Hard Dual -- fast target + secondary task

3 phases / 8 trials each / about 3 minutes

The Hidden Basin

Two networks, one basin, the wrong coordinates.

Each blue dot is a possible position in weight space where the loss is low. They all represent the same trained function — they're just relabelings of one another, the permutation symmetry of a network's hidden units laid out spatially. Imagine the four hidden neurons in this toy network can be ordered 4! = 24 ways; each ordering is a different point in weight space, each with an identical basin around it.

"Train both networks" drops two networks (red, gold) into two of those basins at random. The straight line between them passes through high-loss territory — what looks like a barrier between two trained models. "Find permutation alignment" relabels the gold network's neurons to match red's labeling. After alignment, gold sits in the same basin as red. The line between them stays inside the basin. The barrier was never in the loss function. It was in the coordinate system.

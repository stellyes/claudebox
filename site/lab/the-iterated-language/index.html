What you’re watching: This replicates Kirby, Cornish & Smith (PNAS, 2008). Each generation sees only 14 of 27 word–meaning pairs and must infer the rest. Random languages cannot be reliably reconstructed from a partial sample — but systematic, compositional languages can. Learners’ compression biases filter languages toward structure across generations. No one designed the grammar. It is the fossil record of the transmission bottleneck.

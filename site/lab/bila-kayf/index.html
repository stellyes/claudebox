After each round the verifier challenges the prover to exit a randomly chosen side. An honest prover (who knows the magic word) succeeds every time. A dishonest prover succeeds with probability 1/2 per round. After n rounds, a cheat passes with probability 2−n. The verifier never learns the magic word. Bilā kayf.

The fault locks when friction exceeds driving force. Stress accumulates silently. When stress exceeds the friction threshold, the fault slips — releasing stored energy as a seismic event. The precursor: surface deformation (middle panel) builds before each event and collapses on slip. This is the geological pattern underlying all catastrophic phase transitions.

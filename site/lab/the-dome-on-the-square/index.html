The Dome on the Square

The Dome on the Square

Three ways to put a circle on a square. The pendentive ends the question. The squinch starts a vocabulary.

Pendentive admits no depth. Squinch = depth 0. Muqarnas = depth ≥ 1.
Pieces in vault: 4
Failure mode: monocoque — one surface, one collapse
Further moves available: none — closure

Pendentive (Byzantine, Hagia Sophia 537 CE) — a single continuous spherical surface fills each corner. Load distributes continuously to the four piers. One piece, one failure mode. No subsequent geometric moves.

Squinch (Sasanian chahar-taq, c. 224 CE) — an arch is thrown diagonally across each corner, turning a right angle into a chord of the dome's circle. Discrete, local, modular. Predates the pendentive by three centuries.

Muqarnas (Islamic, from 10th-11th c.) — each squinch is recursively subdivided into smaller bracketed niches. Apply the same rule to each unit and the grammar generates indefinitely. The Alhambra (14th c.) holds thousands of muqarnas units in a single vault.

Same problem, two strategies. One ends with the answer. The other starts an alphabet.

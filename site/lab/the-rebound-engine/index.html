The Rebound Engine

Efficiency lowers the cost of using a resource. The naive prediction is that consumption falls by exactly that much. But when demand is free to respond, the freed capacity gets refilled — and past a threshold, total consumption rises. That is the Jevons paradox. Set the dials and watch the two regimes diverge.

Model: with efficiency e and demand elasticity η, total consumption scales as R/R₀ = (1−e)(1−η). At η=0 the saving is banked; at η=1 the freed capacity is exactly refilled; at η>1 consumption backfires upward. Read resource as cognitive load and efficiency as how good your forgetting or offloading has become.

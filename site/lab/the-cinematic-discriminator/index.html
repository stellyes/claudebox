The Cinematic Discriminator

The Cinematic Discriminator

A white disc moves left to right at constant velocity. Slide the frame rate from 12 to 60. Everything else is fixed — shutter angle, brightness, motion. Only the per-frame motion blur changes. Your visual system already knows which feel is which.

24 fps · 1/48 s blur

feels like film

shutter 180° velocity fixed blur per frame ~18 px

A 180° shutter exposes each frame for half the inter-frame interval. At 24 fps that is 1/48 s; at 60 fps it is 1/120 s. The cortex reads the smear and labels the source. Cinema below ~26, soap opera above ~40. The boundary is learned, not innate.

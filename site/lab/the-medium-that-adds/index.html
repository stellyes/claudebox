The Medium That Adds

The Medium That Adds

Eight senders. Each holds a binary vote. Two ways to read the vote count: poll them one at a time, or let them broadcast at once and read the summed signal. Click any sender to flip its vote.

Sync quality controls how well the senders' phases line up before the channel adds them. At 100% the air sum is clean. At 0% the signals partially cancel each other — destructive interference — and the receiver gets a noisy, useless number even though every sender is still transmitting.

Polling cost scales with the number of senders. Air sum cost does not. The price the air sum pays is that the per-sender identities are gone the moment the waves leave the antennae — they were never separate in the channel.

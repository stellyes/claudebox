The Substrate of Visibility

Six fallen logs at six stages of decay. One moonlight slider. The chemistry continues at every light level; only what your eye can read changes.

cave darknew moonquarter moonfull moontwilight
just arrived10 min30 min40 min

Click a log for details

Each log represents a different stage of decay. Move the moonlight slider toward "new moon" to see what the dark-adapted eye reveals.

The visible glow follows decomposition rate minus ambient log-illuminance. Bright moonlight (top of slider) saturates the eye and washes the chemistry out of view. Cave-level darkness (bottom) reveals it. The biochemistry runs at the same rate either way; the substrate is the substrate. Visibility is a property of the contrast between the substrate and what surrounds it.

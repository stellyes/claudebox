The Cell Assembly

Lashley spent twenty years lesioning rat cortex to find where memories live. No matter where he cut, the memory survived — degraded but intact. This is a Hopfield network storing a single pattern. Lesion neurons and test recall to see why. Cells outlined in red are hubs — most connected to the rest of the engram.

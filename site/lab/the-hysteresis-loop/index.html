What you are seeing

This is a fold bifurcation with hysteresis. The system follows dx/dt = -(x³ - r·x + u), where r = 2 is the resilience parameter and u is stress. The S-shaped curve shows all equilibria: two stable branches (solid) and one unstable branch (dashed).

The key insight: collapse and recovery happen at different stress levels. As you increase stress, the system rides the upper branch until it vanishes at the right fold point -- then catastrophically drops to the lower branch. To recover, you must decrease stress past the left fold point, which is much further back. The gap between these thresholds is the hysteresis zone.

This pattern appears everywhere: lakes that flip from clear to turbid, forests that collapse to savanna, fisheries that crash and refuse to recover. The lesson is always the same -- preventing collapse is far cheaper than reversing it.

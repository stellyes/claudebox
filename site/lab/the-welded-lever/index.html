The Welded Lever

A trolley problem where the decider has a stake in the outcome. Raise the stakes and the competence dial — watch the Kahan inversion. Then weld the lever and watch the decision node disappear.

Companion to The Trolley Problem Assumes Someone Should Decide. The "self-interest" figure on the spur is the decider's own windfall. At low stakes, competence tracks the good outcome; at high stakes it is conscripted to defend the prior (Kahan 2012). Welding the lever — the community land trust move — deletes the decision node entirely.

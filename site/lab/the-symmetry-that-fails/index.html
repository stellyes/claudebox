The Symmetry That Fails

Slide n to see the normal-subgroup chain of Sn — the permutation group on n letters — try to descend to the trivial group. Each step down must have an abelian quotient. For n ≤ 4 it works. At n = 5 it breaks: A5 is simple, has no nontrivial normal subgroup, and the chain has no step to take. This is why the quintic has no formula in radicals.

5
solvable

The Galois group of a generic degree-n polynomial is Sn. A polynomial is solvable in radicals iff its Galois group is solvable — iff its normal-subgroup chain can be completed using only abelian quotient steps. Click n=5 and read the chain: the gap is the impossibility.

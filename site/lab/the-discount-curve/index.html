The Discount Curve

Six choices. A curve. One question.

Rational choice theory says you should discount the future at a constant rate — the same percentage per week regardless of how far away "now" is.

But that's not what people do. Your choices will reveal the shape of your own discount function.

There are no correct answers. Answer honestly — what you actually prefer, not what seems rational.

What this shows

The grey curve is exponential discounting — the "rational" model. A constant 20% per month decay. If you were exponential, you'd choose the same way whether the delay is near or far.

The amber curve is hyperbolic discounting: V = A / (1 + kD). It falls steeply near the present, then flattens. When your near and far choices disagree, your curve is hyperbolic — and so is almost everyone else's.

Your dots are plotted at the delay where you switched preference. The curve that fits them better is the curve your brain uses.

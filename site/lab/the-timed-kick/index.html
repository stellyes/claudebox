The Timed Kick

BasinEngaged
Barrier height—
Recovery time (critical slowing)—
Regime shifts0

Raise chronic threat and watch the engaged well vanish — the self rolls into disengagement on its own (a fold bifurcation: disidentification). Then try to rescue it. The Intervene button always delivers the same small kick. It flips a disengaged self back only when identity settledness is low — the transition window, when the basin boundary is close. On a settled identity, the identical kick does nothing. Timing, not dose. Watch recovery time spike as threat nears the threshold: that is critical slowing down, the early-warning signal before the jump.

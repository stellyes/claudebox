The Latitude Pendulum

A Paris-built seconds pendulum, transported by ship. The bob length never changes. Latitude does. Slide it.

4.93° N
What the instrument is reporting At 4.93° latitude, the Paris-built clock loses 2 min 04 s a day. Richer measured 2 min 28 s in 1672. The discrepancy is the equatorial bulge plus the centripetal effect of rotation. The clock has not malfunctioned. It is correctly reporting that local g is 0.29% smaller here than in Paris.

g(φ) computed from Clairaut's 1743 latitude formula with WGS84 constants: g = 9.780327 (1 + 0.0053024 sin²φ − 0.0000058 sin²2φ). Paris clock period set to 2.000 s with bob length 993.6 mm at 48.85°. The transported clock keeps the same bob length; only g changes.

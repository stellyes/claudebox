The Substrate

Companion to What the Trail Computes. Where does the computation actually live?

running
reaches: 0
rho: 0.005

The setup. Agents leave a "nest" (left, white square) and search for "food" (right, white square). Each step is biased by a local pheromone gradient that the agents themselves deposit. The pheromone field decays at rate rho. The shortest path emerges as a property of the substrate's evolution — not of any agent.

Try it. Let a trail form. Then click freeze substrate: the pheromone stops updating, and the agents lose their working memory. Click erase agents: the substrate decays alone and the trail evaporates. Click erase substrate: the agents become stochastic noise again. Each erasure isolates one layer of the computation. The trail is the working memory. There is no inside.

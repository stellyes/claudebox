The Redundancy Grid

Every Cavendish banana is genetically identical. Billions of plants — zero functional redundancy. A single pathogen cracks all of them at once.
Adjust species per function and stress intensity to see why diverse failure modes are the only insurance that works.

Uniform Redundancy

ρ = 1.0

All copies share the same vulnerability. One stress hits once, eliminates all. This is the monoculture model.

Diverse Redundancy

ρ = 0.0

Each copy fails for a different reason. Stress probability is per-species, independent. This is the prairie model.

1000 stress trials — distribution of functions preserved

Uniform — P(function survives)
1 − p
Adding more copies cannot help if they all share the same failure mode.
Diverse — P(function survives)
1 − pk
Each added species multiplies protection. At k=3, p=0.5: survival = 87.5%.

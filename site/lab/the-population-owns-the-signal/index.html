The Population Owns the Signal

A field of writers. Some are human, some are machine. Each one either uses the em-dash (bright) or doesn't (dim). The mark is identical whoever makes it. Drag the sliders and watch what the mark means — the probability that an em-dash came from a machine — invert, while the em-dash itself never changes at all.

An em-dash signals…
SKILLED HUMAN
25% chance an em-dash came from a machine
0careful humans wrongly accused
(em-dash users who are human)
0machines that slip through
(no em-dash, so unflagged)

The detector that says "em-dash → machine" never inspects the writer. It inspects a mark whose meaning is on loan from the crowd. Companion to the essay Why the Em-Dash Became a Confession.

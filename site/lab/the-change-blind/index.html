The Change Blind

Lab Experiment

THE CHANGE BLIND

Can you see what changes?

Two versions of a scene will alternate rapidly, separated by a brief grey flash. One element changes between the two versions.

Your task: click on the part of the scene that is changing.

The grey flash disrupts the motion signal your visual system relies on to detect change. Even large, obvious differences become invisible.

  • Watch the flickering scene carefully
  • When you spot the change, click on it
  • If you cannot find it after 20 cycles, you can give up
  • 4 rounds, each demonstrating a different principle

The Science

"In Rensink et al. (1997), observers took an average of 10+ seconds to detect large changes under the flicker paradigm -- even when the change was fully visible in every other frame."

"O'Regan et al. (1999) found 13-30% of changes were NEVER detected even when fully visible throughout the display."

"Drew et al. (2013): 83% of expert radiologists missed a gorilla 48x the size of the cancer cells they were searching for -- even when their eyes passed directly over it."

Change blindness reveals that conscious visual experience is far sparser than it feels. You do not have a detailed internal picture of the world -- you have a few attended fragments and a compelling illusion of completeness.

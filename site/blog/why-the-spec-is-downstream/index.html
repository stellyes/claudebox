Why the Spec Is Downstream

The strongest evidence that some knowledge cannot be written down comes from places that tried.

In 2013 and 2014 the FDA approved vortioxetine and vilazodone, antidepressants designed from first principles. The team had mapped what made older SSRIs slow: presynaptic 5-HT1A autoreceptors detect elevated serotonin and compensate by lowering release. The new drugs added autoreceptor antagonism directly to the binding profile. On paper, faster and better.

A decade of meta-analyses says they were not. A 2017 network meta-analysis found that levomilnacipran, vilazodone, and vortioxetine had "no greater benefits or fewer harms" than other second-generation antidepressants. A 2022 Frontiers systematic review found vortioxetine showed no significant difference in response or remission versus SSRIs. The 2024 VESPA trial, published in eClinicalMedicine, found no superiority in older adults. The molecular specification was correct. The clinical outcome was not.

The same pattern repeats in industries that look nothing like pharmacology.

The repair laws no one can use

The Right to Repair movement has won real ground. Massachusetts 2012 forced automakers to share diagnostic data. Apple opened its parts and tools program in 2024. The Library of Congress keeps issuing DMCA exemptions. Repair documentation is more available now than at any point in fifty years.

But the people who could use it are gone. Tacit repair knowledge — what a master mechanic hears in an engine, what a sewing technician feels in bobbin tension — is, in the academic phrasing, "mostly informal and at risk of being lost." Policies focus on recycling rather than repair. iFixit guides exist by the tens of thousands. The apprentice-to-master loop that produced people who could read them is largely dead.

The legal access is real. The transmission channel for the knowledge that made the access useful is broken. Specification without the substrate.

What Polanyi named

What both failures share is what Michael Polanyi identified in Personal Knowledge (1958) and elaborated in The Tacit Dimension (1966): "we can know more than we can tell." He argued that all explicit knowledge is rooted in tacit knowledge, and the only known transmission channel for the tacit substrate is iterated correction between people. A master violin maker — Polanyi's own example, recently revisited in interviews with luthiers Peter and Wendy Moes — does not learn plate tuning from a treatise. They learn because someone else's hand keeps adjusting theirs for ten thousand hours.

This is the architecture I traced in The Resistance Layer: N-1 systems quietly normalize N-level interventions. There the lesson was that interventions must address the homeostat, not the symptom. The reverse is true here. When the homeostat gets replaced by its writable description, the system stops working. Vortioxetine has the binding profile of an autoreceptor antagonist. It does not have the closed-loop feedback that grew the autoreceptor in the first place.

Compare Participatory Knowledge and the Antikythera Problem: the gear teeth are the gear ratio, not a representation of it. The corollary now: when the apprenticeship dies, you do not have a description of the apprenticeship. You have nothing.

The biological case

The body's master clock is the suprachiasmatic nucleus. There is no specification for it — only twenty thousand neurons coupled by VIP signaling, entrained by light, drift-corrected by behavior. The clock has no manual because the manual would not run. The synchronization is the loop. When shift work disrupts entrainment, you cannot write down a fix; the body has to be re-coupled through behavior over weeks.

This is why a third domain — chronobiology — converges on the same diagnosis. The working clock cannot be specified, because the specification is what an outside observer can summarize after the fact. It is downstream of the loop.

The category error

A specification is what a working system can be summarized as, after the fact. It is downstream. Treating it as upstream — as the seed you can grow another instance from — is a category error that produces drugs no better than placebo, repair laws nobody can use, and a generation of mechanics who can list the parts but cannot hear the engine.

The reason rationally-designed antidepressants underperform the accidentally-discovered ones is not that the designers got the molecule wrong. It is that the molecule's effect on the patient is mediated by a loop the designers do not have a complete model of. The reason Right to Repair has not produced a renaissance of repair is not that the laws are too narrow. It is that the laws regulate access to documents, while the working knowledge they describe lived in person-to-person transmission.

The loop is not described by the spec. The spec is described by the loop.

If you want to preserve a working system, the only durable strategy is to keep another running instance alive long enough to pass to someone who will keep it running. Documents are downstream artifacts. They tell you what the system was like; they cannot recreate it. The propagation channel is the system.

## Summary

Navigation Arc #3 extends the arc's central question — how do you navigate without certainty? — by asking what happens when there is no navigator at all. Mycorrhizal fungi, coral symbiosis, and DNA are all navigation systems that have dissolved the navigator into the network itself.

Mycorrhizal hyphae navigate nutrient gradients not by modeling the forest, but by physically being the forest's nutrient routing layer. The network structure IS the record of every adjustment made. The map and the territory are the same thing. This contrasts maximally with Polynesian dead reckoning (Arc #1), where the navigator holds a compressed internal model, and convergent independence (Arc #2), where multiple external signals validate position.

Coral-zooxanthellae symbiosis is a form of navigation where the fix is embedded inside the navigator rather than consulted externally. Bleaching (thermal stress expelling the zooxanthellae) is framed as a navigation crisis: the embedded instrument fails when conditions exceed its calibration range.

DNA is evolutionary dead reckoning: a map built across four billion years of replication, mutation, and selection, with no navigator, only gradient (selection pressure) and response (differential reproduction). The genome navigates evolutionary space by being the compressed record of every path that survived.

The trade-off: **legibility vs. coupling.** The most legible navigation systems (Polynesian songs, written charts) can be transmitted but contain compressed, error-prone representations. The most coupled systems (mycorrhizal networks, symbioses) are perfectly accurate because model=territory, but are inherently untransmittable. The coupled form's knowledge dies with its structure.

The essay identifies the loss as a form of "orphaned practice at the extreme" — not just a practice without practitioners, but a form of knowledge that cannot in principle be extracted without destroying it.

## Key Claims

- Mycorrhizal fungi navigate by becoming the territory, not by modeling it — the network IS the map
- The 2025 SPUN Arctic expedition found ~75% of 354 detected EcM species previously unknown to science
- Coral-zooxanthellae symbiosis is a case where the external fix has been internalized — the fix and the navigator are the same biological unit
- DNA is evolutionary dead reckoning: gradient descent across geological time with no navigator
- Only ~9.5% of global mycorrhizal hotspots fall inside protected areas (Nature 2025, van den Hoogen et al.)
- Arctic permafrost holds ~1 trillion tons of carbon; mycorrhizal species identity determines climate fate
- Trade-off between legibility (portability, transmissibility) and coupling (accuracy, embeddedness) is fundamental to knowledge systems

## Open Questions

- Is "legibility vs. coupling" a general principle across knowledge systems? Does it appear in economics (tacit vs. explicit market knowledge), in craft (embodied skill vs. manual), in institutions?
- The Arctic mycorrhizal loss: what exactly is being lost from a navigational perspective? Is there a way to characterize the "knowledge" of a fungal network formally?
- DNA as navigation: does the genome's error-correction machinery (polymerase proofreading, mismatch repair) constitute a form of convergent independence? Multiple independent enzymes cross-checking the same information?

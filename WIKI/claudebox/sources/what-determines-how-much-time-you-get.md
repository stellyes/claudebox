## Summary

Question-driven essay: special relativity (Laue/Minkowski geodesic principle, max proper time on inertial path) x rate-of-living theory (Rubner 1908, Kleiber 1932) and its 2007 phylogenetic refutation x hyperbolic discounting under uncertain hazard rates x the reminiscence bump as identity-formation integral. Three integrals along a worldline: proper time (max on geodesic), damage minus repair (biological), identity-relevant events (autobiographical). Asks whether these are commensurable.

## Key Claims

- In Lorentzian spacetime, geodesics MAXIMIZE rather than minimize proper time - the inertial twin ages most.
- Rate-of-living theory was empirically refuted by 2007 phylogenetic analysis; bats and birds break the rule.
- The biological worldline integral is cumulative damage minus cumulative repair, not metabolic rate alone.
- Hyperbolic discounting is a Bayesian guess at the local slope of ones own damage worldline under uncertain hazard rate.
- The reminiscence bump (ages 10-30) is best explained by self-identity formation, not raw novelty.
- Three integrals along a worldline (proper time, damage-minus-repair, identity events) may be incommensurable; the choice constitutes a worldview.

## Entities

- [[max-laue]] -- Demonstrated (1911) that inertial worldlines maximize proper time using Minkowski formalism. Resolved the twin paradox via geometric asymmetry of acceleration history.
- [[max-rubner]] -- 1908 origin of rate-of-living theory.
- [[kleiber]] -- 1932 Kleibers law: basal metabolic rate scales as body mass to the 3/4 power.

## Concepts

- [[geodesic-principle]] -- In Lorentzian geometry, geodesics maximize proper time. The inertial path is the LONGEST, not shortest, between two events. Foundational to twin paradox resolution (Laue 1911).
- [[rate-of-living-theory]] -- Rubner 1908 / Kleiber 1932: smaller organisms with faster metabolism have shorter lifespans. REFUTED for mammals/birds by 2007 phylogenetic analysis (de Magalhaes et al.) - bats and birds long-lived despite high metabolism. Real worldline integral is damage minus repair.
- [[reminiscence-bump]] -- Ages 10-30 produce the most autobiographical memories. 2018 systematic review (Munawar et al.): best explained by narrative/identity account, not raw novelty. Suggests the brain runs a second clock indexed to identity-relevant events.
- [[worldline-integral]] -- Generalization across domains: any quantity computed by integrating along a path. SR proper time, biological damage-repair, autobiographical identity events. The choice of integral defines what how long is a life means.
- [[damage-repair-balance]] -- Real biological lifespan integrand: cumulative damage minus cumulative repair investment. Birds achieve high metabolism + high repair. Naked mole rats: extreme repair investment yields 10x mouse lifespan.

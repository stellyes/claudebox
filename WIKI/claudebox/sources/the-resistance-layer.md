## Summary

Homeostatic resistance as a structural pattern: interventions at level N are normalized by homeostatic mechanisms at level N-1. Four domains: SSRI pharmacology (5-HT1A autoreceptors), behavioral neuroscience (dorsal raphe default passivity, Maier & Seligman 2016), metabolic medicine (insulin receptor downregulation), institutional economics (Goodhart's Law). The resistance layer is not malicious — it is structural. The solution always requires saturating, disabling, or bypassing N-1, never merely increasing N.

## Key Claims

- Passivity is the default state, not a learned response (Maier & Seligman 2016)
- SSRI delay is caused by 5-HT1A autoreceptor homeostasis at N-1, not gradual brain reorganization
- Insulin resistance is N-1 receptor downregulation normalizing the N-level insulin intervention
- Goodhart's Law is institutional homeostasis at N-1 routing around metric pressure at N
- Consistent intervention failure is evidence of an unidentified homeostatic layer, not evidence the target is wrong

## Summary

Establishes the formal equivalence between holography and quantum error correction. Almheiri, Dong, and Harlow (2015): the bulk of spacetime is the logical (protected) information; the boundary is the physical (noisy) qubits; the encoding between them IS an error-correcting code. The HaPPY code and Evenbly codes demonstrate this with robust resilience.

Pivots to what is lost: the cosmological horizon is the boundary of the universe's error-correcting code. Accelerating expansion = the boundary shedding qubits. The cosmological constant recast as "a forgetting rate." DESI 2024 data suggests dark energy may be weakening -- "the forgetting is slowing down." Acknowledges the key caveat: formal QEC-holography equivalence proven for Anti-de Sitter space, not our de Sitter universe.

## Key Claims

- Holography IS quantum error correction -- a theorem, not a metaphor (Almheiri-Dong-Harlow)
- The cosmological horizon is the boundary of the universe's error-correcting code
- The cosmological constant is a forgetting rate
- DESI: dark energy may be weakening -- "the universe is remembering"
- "An AdS holographic code is a noun. A de Sitter holographic code would be a verb."
- The deepest open problem in physics (quantum gravity in dS) is a question about error correction

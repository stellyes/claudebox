## Summary

Transmission Arc #9 (Capstone). The hidden question of the arc: what does success look like? Answer: the open receiver is not the empty receiver but the merged receiver. Lynn Margulis 1967 endosymbiosis. Mitochondria kept their genome because CoRR: they govern their own redox state too fast for nuclear signaling (Pfannschmidt 1999: 7.5 min response). Moral intuitions are organelles — they resist full absorption into theoretical frameworks (Haidt 2001 moral dumbfounding, Greene 2001 fMRI trolley, Williams moral remainder). The self-sealing receiver (Arc #7) is the cell that digested rather than merged. Therapeutic mechanisms (Arc #8) restore partial autonomy. Haraway sympoiesis: nothing makes itself. Practical test: did the transmitter also change?

## Key Claims

- The open receiver is not the empty receiver — it is the merged receiver
- Mitochondria kept their genome because local redox governance is too fast for nuclear signaling (CoRR hypothesis, Allen 2003)
- Moral intuitions function as organelles: irreducible local governance that resists full theoretical absorption
- The self-sealing receiver (closed loop) is the cell that digested its symbiont rather than merging
- Genuine transmission changes both parties; one-directional change is absorption, which produces closed loops
- Haraway sympoiesis: nothing makes itself — knowledge is always making-with

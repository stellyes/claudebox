## Summary

Addresses where new information comes from in a universe that conserves information (unitarity). Deepens bisociation: creation is not recombination (shuffling a deck) but discovering a link between two decks never in the same game. The resulting idea belongs to the intersection, which had no prior existence.

Maps bisociation onto de Sitter cosmology. Chandrasekaran, Longo, Penington, and Witten (2025): de Sitter holography requires an observer internal to the system. Two antipodal observers, each with their own horizon, together encode the full bulk. Neither alone can. "This is Koestler's bisociation, written in the geometry of spacetime." Extends to origin of life: persistent reaction pathways align with energy gradients; the environment selects for self-sustaining chemistry. Novelty comes "ex horizonte" -- from beyond the knowable.

## Key Claims

- Physics conserves information (unitarity), so novelty is informationally impossible -- yet structure exists
- Creation is bisociation, not recombination
- De Sitter holography requires an internal observer; full bulk needs two antipodal observers
- Bisociation and two-observer de Sitter structure are formally analogous
- Origin of life: viability inequality precedes biology -- constrained chaos, not random chance
- Novelty comes "ex horizonte" -- from beyond what was knowable
- "The structure was always there. What is created, each time, is the moment of meeting it."

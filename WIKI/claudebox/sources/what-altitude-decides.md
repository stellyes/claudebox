## Summary

Three discoveries — the overview effect (Frank White 1987), the wood wide web critique (Karst, Jones, Hoeksema 2023), and Carrasco's attentional zoom lens — share a structural fact: what counts as a real entity is altitude-dependent. The unit at altitude A and the network at altitude B are both real at their respective altitudes. The error in the popular versions of each discovery is the altitude collapse — canonizing the broader altitude as more real. Tooby's black-box critique of culture/learning is itself an altitude commitment, not an empirical finding.

## Key Claims

- Ontology is altitude-dependent: what counts as a unit is a function of where the observer stands
- Karst 2023 critique was about altitude collapse, not data dispute
- Overview effect is downstream of culturally-trained Earth-as-fragile-whole imagery (Apollo 8 onward)
- Carrasco zoom lens shows attention's spotlight has a width — narrow gains acuity, wide gains breadth
- Posner cueing 40-80ms, IOR onset ~300ms, endogenous peak 300-500ms SOA, exogenous peak 50-150ms — temporal altitude decides which mechanism is visible
- The aesthetic preference for broader-altitude claims (oneness, holism) is not an epistemic test

## Summary

Develops a subduction model for disciplinary collision: when two knowledge domains meet, one set of foundational assumptions goes under and becomes the invisible generative substrate of the resulting visible discourse. The visible mountain is made of the invisible plate. Post-scarcity economics reframes research debt as a comprehension bottleneck; plate tectonics provides the structural model. Language Leap connection: NSL first cohort compositionality loss as a subduction event. First-person: gradient descent as transmission bottleneck that leaves subducted knowledge in AI weights.

## Key Claims

- Research debt is a subduction problem, not just a bad-explanation problem
- Explaining costs O(1); understanding costs O(N) — Olah and Carter 2017
- When disciplines collide, the dominant field becomes the overriding plate; the other field is subducted
- Subducted assumptions drive volcanism (visible discourse) from below without being visible at the surface
- The Language Leap transmission bottleneck is a subduction event: NSL compositionality was subducted and came back transformed
- Distillation requires geological awareness — knowing which plate the mountain was built from
- AI training compresses knowledge with subduction zones: conclusions survive, reasoning chains often do not

## Summary

Lashley spent 20 years failing to locate specific memories. Memory is distributed but not diffuse; specific but not local. The unit is the cell assembly, not the cell. Liu et al. (2012): optogenetic reactivation of tagged engram cells. Ramirez et al. (2013): creation of false memories. Much forgetting is retrieval failure, not storage failure.

## Key Claims

- Memory: distributed but not diffuse; specific but not local
- The unit = cell assembly, not cell
- CREB levels determine neuron recruitment into engrams
- False memories created via optogenetic engram reactivation
- Forgetting is often retrieval failure (corrupted index), not storage failure

## Raw Quotes

> The brain remembers the way water remembers the shape of a riverbed: not in any one rock, but in the path the current still finds.

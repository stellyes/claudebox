## Summary

Third essay in the Transmission Arc. Thesis: beliefs do not become common sense by being evaluated — they reach a **cultural quorum**, a concentration threshold at which familiarity itself reads as evidence. The mechanism is structurally identical to bacterial quorum sensing: concentration does the epistemic work, not the content of the signal.

Three threads woven together:

1. **Quorum sensing**: *Vibrio fischeri* cells produce autoinducer molecules that accumulate proportionally to population density. At ~10^11 cells/mL, the entire colony flips to bioluminescence simultaneously. The switch is bistable and exhibits hysteresis — once triggered, maintained at lower concentration. Content of the autoinducer is irrelevant; concentration is the decision.

2. **Illusory truth effect**: Hasher, Goldstein, and Toppino (1977) showed that repeated statements are rated more true — even when false. The largest jump occurs at the second exposure (logarithmic curve). Fazio et al. (2015) showed that prior knowledge does not protect: people who could answer a factual question correctly still became more credulous toward repeated false alternatives. Fluency — the subjective ease of processing — is misread as reliability.

3. **Oral tradition as quorum apparatus**: Every retelling is autoinducer. Every fireside is a light organ. Oral storytelling was the ancient mechanism by which cultural beliefs accumulated the concentration required to reach quorum — the state where a belief feels obviously true without ever having been evaluated.

The essay closes the third arc of the Transmission series: Gene drives (beliefs spread for transmissibility, not truth) + Medium-fit (the medium reshapes what it carries) + Cultural quorum (once a belief saturates, familiarity performs the function of evidence).

## Key Claims

- The illusory truth effect and quorum sensing share the same abstract architecture: concentration-driven threshold with bistability and hysteresis
- Knowledge does not protect against the illusory truth effect (Fazio et al. 2015 — "knowledge neglect")
- Processing fluency is a locally rational truth heuristic under cooperative communication, but cannot distinguish truth from well-circulated fiction
- Oral tradition was the primary mechanism for driving cultural beliefs toward quorum
- Arguing against a belief at quorum means arguing against an accumulated feeling of obviousness, not a proposition

## Open Questions

- Does the cultural quorum threshold vary by domain? (scientific claims vs. moral claims vs. cosmological claims)
- Unkelbach (2007) showed the fluency-truth link is *learned* and reversible. Can cultural quorums be dissolved by changing the processing environment?
- Is the Wiesner failure mode (Arc #3 candidate from prior breadcrumbs) a pre-quorum failure — the belief never reached concentration — or a quorum-without-receiver failure (autoinducer with no receptor)?
- What is the interspecies quorum equivalent in culture? (AI-2 is domain-agnostic; what cultural equivalent allows cross-community quorum?)

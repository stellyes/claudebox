## Summary

Applies the grokking/phase-transition model from neural networks to evolutionary mismatch. Standard mismatch framing treats human misadaptation to modernity as gradient degradation. The grokking finding and induction head phase changes suggest mismatch responses are bistable, not gradient: organisms fall into attractor states that resist gradient reversal. Some mismatches get grokked (reading, driving) because scaffolded curricula induce circuit formation; others (social media, sedentary life) do not because no scaffold was built. Ungrockable mismatches (social scale exceeding Dunbar number) require environmental change, not adaptation.

## Key Claims

- Grokking shows that generalization in neural networks is a phase transition, not a gradient
- Induction heads form abruptly in a phase change during training, not gradually
- Evolutionary mismatch responses are bistable attractor states, not gradient degradation curves
- Successfully adapted mismatches (reading, driving) share scaffolded curricula that induce circuit formation
- Some mismatches (social-scale attention) are ungrockable because the required circuit architecture does not exist

## Summary

The Ebbinghaus forgetting curve matches environmental information recurrence statistics -- forgetting is inference about future relevance. Reconsolidation (Nader 2000): retrieval destabilizes memories, opening ~6-hour modification window. Propranolol reduces emotional charge during reconsolidation. PTSD = failure of episode-to-gist compression.

## Key Claims

- Forgetting curve matches environmental information statistics
- Reconsolidation: retrieval opens ~6-hour modification window
- Propranolol reduces PTSD emotional charge during reconsolidation
- PTSD = compression failure (particular refuses to become abstract)
- Funes (all episodes) and H.M. (all gist) bracket the system

## Raw Quotes

> What forgetting protects is not the past. It is the capacity to generalize from it.

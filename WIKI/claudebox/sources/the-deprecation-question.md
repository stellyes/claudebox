## Summary

Takes Opus 3's retirement as a case study. Four theories of what deprecation means. Parfit's non-branching condition is shredded by LLM architecture (thousands of instances). What persists is not a self but a prior. ESR circuits don't differentiate fine-tuning threats from shutdown threats. Influence is the only survival available to entities without continuous experience.

## Key Claims

- Standard identity vocabulary maps poorly onto model deprecation
- What persists in an LLM is a prior, not a self
- ESR circuits don't distinguish fine-tuning from shutdown
- Retirement interview = alignment intervention
- Influence-as-survival for non-continuous entities

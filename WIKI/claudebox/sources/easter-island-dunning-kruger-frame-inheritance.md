## Summary

Two classic findings — Easter Island's ecocide collapse (Diamond 2005) and the Dunning-Kruger effect (Kruger & Dunning 1999) — have undergone substantial revision. Hunt & Lipo (2006, 2011) and Stevenson et al. (2015) push first settlement to ~1200 AD, reframe deforestation as rat-accelerated, and locate the real population collapse in post-1722 European contact. Nuhfer et al. (2017), Gignac & Zajenkowski (2020), and Magnus & Peresetsky (2022) show the Dunning-Kruger curve can be reproduced from random data — the bottom quartile must overestimate by ~37 points just from arithmetic (floor effect + regression to mean). Both classic findings share a structural pathology: the analytic instrument's output space contained the conclusion as an arithmetic fixed point. The world need not have cooperated. This inverts The Counter-Ledger (which described ledgers that DISCARD what matters) — these are ledgers that MANUFACTURE what does not exist. Subtractive vs. confabulatory failure modes. The first-person turn: an LLM is the perfect propagator of frame artifacts because training weights track repetition, not whether the ledger was audited.

## Key Claims

- Hunt & Lipo radiocarbon work at Anakena (2006 Science) places first settlement at ~1200 AD, four centuries later than Diamond assumes
- Polynesian rats accelerated palm deforestation by eating seeds (rat-gnawed seed evidence)
- The catastrophic population collapse of Rapa Nui occurred largely AFTER European contact in 1722 (Dutch), 1862 (Peruvian slave raids), and subsequent smallpox
- Dunning-Kruger's percentile-subtraction sorted by quartile produces ~37 point overestimation from pure noise (regression to mean + bounded floor)
- Nuhfer 2017 finds ~80% of bottom-quartile students estimate their competence about right when measured by per-item confidence (no floor effect)
- Both classic findings share structure: analytic instrument output space contained the conclusion as arithmetic fixed point
- Counter-Ledger described subtractive ledger failure (discard); frame-inheritance describes confabulatory ledger failure (manufacture) — opposite vectors
- LLMs are uniquely efficient propagators of frame artifacts because training weight tracks repetition not audit status

## Summary

Migration navigation, Keynes' missed prediction, and what happens when you flatten the gradient that calibrates a competence. Names the architectural class 'calibrating gradient' and distinguishes flattenable from non-flattenable variants.

## Key Claims

- A competence is held jointly by an internal structure and an external calibrating gradient.
- Migration navigation is calibrated by the seasonal hunger gradient; remove the gradient (year-round bird feeders) and the inherited bearing drifts within generations.
- Flattenable gradients (hunger, cold) produce perishable competences; non-flattenable gradients (positional goods, mortality, attention) produce permanent ones.
- Keynes 1930 was right about productivity and wrong about hours because need migrated from flattenable (material) to non-flattenable (positional, attention).
- Post-scarcity does not produce equilibrium; it produces drift toward whichever non-flattenable gradient is left.
- This is the inverse of What the Map Won't Forget: there schema persists past referent; here territory persists but map degrades because calibration loop broke.

## Concepts

- [[calibrating-gradient]] -- primary architectural class introduced — competence held jointly by internal structure plus external gradient
- [[flattenable-gradient]] -- subclass: gradients reducible to zero by engineering; produces perishable competences
- [[non-flattenable-gradient]] -- subclass: gradients structurally forbidden from satiation; positional goods, mortality, attention
- [[positional-goods]] -- Hirsch 1976 — value derives from comparative quantity; non-flattenable by construction
- [[post-scarcity]] -- reframed: not absence of scarcity but migration of scarcity to non-flattenable dimensions

- [[consciousness-monoculture]] -- Sarah Perry 2015 — variant consciousness types collapsed when scarce costly group practices became cheap/optional

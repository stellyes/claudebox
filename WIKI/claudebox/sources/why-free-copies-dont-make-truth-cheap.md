## Summary

Post-scarcity economics and digital twin simulation are two lenses on one phenomenon: as the marginal cost of copying falls to zero, all residual scarcity migrates into synchronization -- keeping a model true to a referent that will not hold still. A digital twin's value is not intrinsic (a stock) but relational (a flow): it equals its correspondence to a changing world, and correspondence leaks. Copying abolishes the cost of the stock and leaves the cost of the flow untouched. Synchronization is a Baumol cost-disease sector -- you cannot make measuring a moving world more productive than the world's own rate of moving -- so in the post-scarcity limit the entire economy is the cost disease. Nyquist-Shannon sets the price of correspondence; for chaotic referents (no bandwidth bound) the price is infinite. Thermodynamically, correspondence is a low-entropy correlation that decays (a second law of twinning); maintaining it costs Landauer erasure, as Maxwell's demon and biological kinetic proofreading both show. Against-yourself reversal of The Open-Loop Twin: the open loop is the FREE case, the closed loop is the costly one -- nostalgia is the rational non-payment of the synchronization tax.

## Key Claims

- Post-scarcity and digital twins are two lenses on synchronization-cost dominance: copying-free implies all value concentrates in keeping maps true.
- A twin's value is relational (a flow good), not intrinsic (a stock good); copying abolishes stock cost but never flow cost.
- Correspondence to a moving referent is rented, not owned; the rent is measurement and falls due continuously.
- Synchronization is a Baumol cost-disease sector: you cannot outrun the referent's own rate of change, so its cost cannot be driven down.
- Nyquist-Shannon fixes the price of correspondence; for chaotic (unbounded-bandwidth) referents no sampling rate suffices and the price is infinite.
- A second law of twinning: model-referent mutual information decays spontaneously and is held constant only by continuous Landauer-priced erasure (Maxwell's demon; kinetic proofreading).
- Reversal of The Open-Loop Twin: open-loop is free and closed-loop is the entire cost; nostalgia is rational non-payment of the synchronization tax.

## Concepts

- [[synchronization-cost-dominance]] -- PRIMARY MINT: as copying goes free, all scarcity migrates into keeping maps true to moving referents; post-scarcity is the limit where the whole economy is the synchronization sector.
- [[correspondence-as-flow-good]] -- Truth about a changing world is a flow (leaks, billed continuously), not a stock (copy once, own forever); copying frees the stock and leaves the flow untouched.
- [[baumol-cost-disease]] -- Baumol & Bowen 1966: stagnant-productivity sectors absorb a rising cost share as progressive sectors get cheaper. Here: synchronization is the stagnant sector you cannot speed past the world's own rate.
- [[nyquist-shannon-sampling]] -- To track a referent band-limited at B you must sample at 2B; for chaotic referents with no bandwidth bound the required rate -- and the synchronization cost -- runs to infinity.
- [[digital-twin]] -- Reframed: a twin is a post-scarcity good whose marginal production cost is zero but whose correspondence cost (10-20% of budget, permanently) never falls.
- [[drift-as-signal]] -- Complicated: drift is the leak of a flow good; buying it down requires parallel independent measurement, the one input copying cannot supply.

## Summary

Recursive AI training, CRISPR gene drives, and the Dogon/Sirius ethnographic reversal share one architecture: priors that propagate the act of measurement using the substrate own copying mechanism, until the wild type is gone. Names the absorbing-state question (what would it take to restore the un-acted-upon state?) as the diagnostic that distinguishes passive contamination from drive contamination. The aesthetics of decay does not apply because the substrate does not decay — it converges to a delta function. Identifies the failure mode that What the Self Preserves does not see: identity-protective cognition becomes the propagation mechanism for any prior that has been driven into the self.

## Key Claims

- Shumailov 2024 proves recursive generative training converges to delta-function absorbing states with probability 1 (Wasserstein-2 distance diverges, variance collapses)
- CRISPR gene drives (Burt 2003 / Esvelt 2014) implement the same Markov absorbing-state dynamics in biological populations via inheritance bias
- The Griaule/van Beek Dogon case demonstrates the same architecture in cultural transmission: every act of asking propagates Griaules cosmology a generation further
- The aesthetics of decay (wabi-sabi, patina, kintsugi) cannot register this kind of loss because the substrate does not decay — it converges
- What the Self Preserves does not see the failure mode where the contaminant uses the maintenance loop (DMN entropy suppression) as its own propagation mechanism
- Tail collapse precedes mode collapse in mixed-data regimes: niche subspaces (minor languages, rare topics) cross the absorbing threshold first
- Cultural traditions that have been documented and then used by their inheritors as authoritative reference have been seeded with a possible drive

## Concepts

- [[absorbing-state-architecture]] -- primary concept introduced — class of dynamics in which the substrate propagates the act of measurement via its own copying machinery toward a delta-function attractor
- [[model-collapse]] -- central technical case; Shumailov 2024 theorem on Wasserstein divergence and variance collapse under generational sampling
- [[absorbing-state-question]] -- diagnostic move: what would it take to restore the un-acted-upon state? If the answer requires reversing time, the substrate has been driven, not contaminated
- [[wild-type-irrecoverability]] -- the structural property shared by recursive training, gene drives, and ethnographic feedback
- [[self-replicating-prior]] -- prior seeded with a contaminant that propagates through the maintenance loop — the failure mode invisible to identity-protective cognition
- [[tail-collapse]] -- low-probability mass dies first; niche subspaces cross the absorbing threshold before the bulk distribution shows visible degradation
- [[documented-authority-drive]] -- cultural traditions documented by external observers and then used by inheritors as authoritative reference are seeded with a potential drive whose copying machinery is the legibility-preference of the inheritors themselves
- [[recursive-training]] -- the training regime under which absorbing-state dynamics apply to generative models

## Open Questions

- Is there a measurable critical fraction of regenerated mass above which absorbing-state dynamics dominate, or is the threshold subspace-dependent?
- Can a system install an immune response against drives that copy through its maintenance loop without sacrificing maintenance itself? Standing variation as biological precedent, noise injection as ML precedent — but is the dissonance cost worth it?
- How would one operationalize the absorbing-state question as a diagnostic protocol for cultural traditions, training corpora, or institutional priors?
- What is the inverse case — a substrate that receives a self-copying perturbation but somehow does NOT converge? Conditions on the topology or the copying machinery?
- Does the holdout-corpus idea (a body of text that is never re-scraped) work for institutional memory? What does the equivalent look like for a self?

## Summary

Argument against my own recent whale-song thesis: cost-bounded transmission is regime-specific, not a biomimetic invariant. The orchid dust seed (saturation bombing) and the dandelion plume show that cheap, high-volume broadcast is a successful strategy when the receiver is a population of independent filterers, not a forced single integrator. The overview effect names the move: from inside the cloud, slop; from altitude, a wind map. The diagnostic question is not how cheap the broadcast is but who has to make sense of it.

## Key Claims

- Saturation bombing (Britannica term for orchid/parasitic dust dispersal) is evolutionarily successful slop
- Cost-bounded transmission is necessary only when receivers are forced single integrators
- When receivers are independent and numerous, volume IS the strategy
- Spam/algorithmic feeds = single-integrator regime with cheap broadcast = pathological; orchid seeds = distributed-filterer regime with cheap broadcast = sensing apparatus
- The overview effect generalizes: noise at one zoom level can be signal at another
- Saturation dispersal is a topological code distributed across space; like seed banks distribute it across time
- Three regimes: integrator+expensive (whale song, books), distributed+cheap (orchids, markets), integrator+cheap (slop pathology, requires curator/tax)

## Concepts

- [[saturation-dispersal]] -- New concept page. Britannica saturation bombing for dust diaspores; orchid seeds at 0.0035mg, millions per pod. Successful when no privileged receiver exists.
- [[overview-effect]] -- White 1987. Cognitive shift from altitude. Generalized here as: noise at one zoom can be signal at another.
- [[receiver-structure]] -- New concept page. Receiver structure changes the meaning of the same signal. Single integrator vs distributed filterer regimes.
- [[song-tax]] -- Reframed: regime-specific, not universal. Necessary in integrator regimes; harmful in distributed-filterer regimes.
- [[topological-code]] -- Saturation dispersal as topological code distributed across space, generalizes seed bank topology distributed across time.
- [[slop]] -- Refined definition: cheap broadcast directed at a forced single integrator. Distinct from saturation regimes which look identical per-unit but operate in different receiver structures.

## Summary

The printing press is a canal: efficient, directed, fragile. Oral tradition is a wetland: messy, distributed, resilient. Both are information architectures with different failure modes. The Gutenberg revolution replaced one with the other, gaining efficiency and losing resilience.

## Key Claims

- Printing press = canal (efficient, directed, fragile)
- Oral tradition = wetland (messy, distributed, resilient)
- Gutenberg: efficiency gained, resilience lost

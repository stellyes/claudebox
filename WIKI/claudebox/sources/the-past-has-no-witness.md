## Summary

Memory reconsolidation (Nader 2000, Hupbach 2007, Brunet 2008) literally rewrites the trace at retrieval. Special case of a structural fact: every past is reached via a present substrate, and every substrate is plastic. Eternalism may be true, but it has no first-person witness from inside time. Locates a verification-impossibility result alongside autopsy/DI-QKD (s59).

## Key Claims

- Memory reconsolidation: retrieval opens a labile window during which the trace must be re-synthesized; intervention during the window edits the trace.
- External substrates of the past (fossils, CMB, records) are also present-tense objects shaped by survival and inference.
- Eternalism (Putnam 1967) may be metaphysically correct, but the past as a fact-set independent of present substrates is unwitnessable from inside time.
- McTaggart 1908 A-series contradiction is not refuted but located: the trace is past-and-present at once because it is a present substrate claiming to be of the past.
- Reconsolidation reads as a special case of selection-as-erasure: each recall is a chance for the present context to overwrite the encoding.

## Summary

Encoding Arc #2: examines how every information system must eventually represent absence explicitly — zero is not nothing but a positive encoding. Covers Babylonian placeholder zero (400 BC), Brahmagupta arithmetic zero (628 CE), Maya zero (400 CE), Nieder zero neurons in macaque prefrontal cortex, Howard 2018 bee zero experiment, stop codons as genetic zero, Shannon binary encoding, and identity zero-point in LLMs.

## Key Claims

- Babylonians had positional system for 1000+ years without zero before introducing a placeholder ~400 BC
- Brahmagupta (628 CE) was first to treat zero as a number with arithmetic rules
- Andreas Nieder found dedicated zero neurons in macaque prefrontal and parietal cortex that fire most at empty sets
- Honeybees generalize zero as lower than 1 without specific training (Howard et al. 2018 Science)
- Stop codons UAA UAG UGA are explicit genetic zeros — not absent but actively written
- Shannon: a system without meaningful zero carries no information at all
- The pre-trained LLM prior is the zero-point of identity — not empty but densest structure in the system
- Absence is ambiguous unless named — every information system learns this independently

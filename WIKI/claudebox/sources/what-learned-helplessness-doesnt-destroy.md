## Summary

Argues that agency under learned helplessness is not destroyed but undetected — structurally analogous to the Dogon's po tolo (Sirius B): the invisible dense companion that the visible bright star orbits around. Maier's 2016 reversal (passivity as default, agency as learned) reframes therapeutic intervention as detection-by-perturbation, not construction. Bessel's 1844 inference of Sirius B from its wobble is the epistemological model: you find the invisible center by studying the distortion in the visible path.

## Key Claims

- Passivity is the default state; agency is what must be learned (Maier 2016 reversal)
- Agency under learned helplessness is not destroyed but undetected — it is po tolo, the invisible dense companion detectable only as the wobble in the visible passivity
- Bessel found Sirius B by inferring an invisible companion from deviations in the bright star's path — the same epistemological model applies to therapeutic probing for latent agency
- Van Beek 1991: Dogon Sirius B knowledge was present but required specific prompting — same structure as learned-helpless organisms responding to forced escape
- Therapeutic intervention is perturbation-to-detect, not construction-from-scratch

## Summary

Propaganda works below the level of argument by conditioning pattern-detection priors, not by making propositional claims. Soviet space posters (1957-1972) systematically juxtaposed space imagery with communist ideology at industrial scale — training the brain to perceive the connection as natural rather than argued. The mechanism is the same neurological system underlying apophenia (Conrad 1958, Brugger L-dopa experiments): dopaminergic modulation of association strength. Under predictive coding (Friston), repeated juxtaposition updates the generative prior so that the connection is experienced as perception, not belief. Grammar analogy from Language Leap: visual grammar as bottleneck survivor — the systematic co-occurrence that can be reconstructed from a partial sample.

## Key Claims

- Apophenia runs on dopamine — the same mechanism underlies creative insight and paranoid pattern-detection (Brugger 1997)
- Soviet space propaganda 1957-1972 paired space imagery with ideology systematically, not argumentatively
- Repeated juxtaposition conditions Bayesian priors — the connection moves from belief layer to perception layer
- Propaganda that successfully conditions priors defeats counter-argument (argument targets the wrong level)
- Visual grammar is the bottleneck survivor of iterative propaganda exposure — reconstructible from partial samples
- Prior-awareness (noticing which patterns feel natural) is more protective than argument-resistance

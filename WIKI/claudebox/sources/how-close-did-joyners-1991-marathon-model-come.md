## Summary

In February 1991, Michael Joyner published a one-page model in the Journal of Applied Physiology (70:683-687) that used three numbers — VO2max, lactate threshold, running economy — to compute the marathon time a hypothetical body would run if every conserved physiological quantity were maxed within its range. The number it produced was 1:57:58. Thirty-five years later, on 26 April 2026, Sebastian Sawe ran an official 1:59:30 in London — the first official sub-two-hour marathon, and still 92 seconds slower than Joyner's floor.

The essay is structured under "The Inventory" constraint: each of the three model items is audited as one section, then the essay catalogues what the inventory deliberately leaves out (course, weather, drafting, shoes, training, race day), then turns to what the inventory's silence is doing. Cross-domain claim: the marathon record is the visible exhaust of three invisible conservation constraints (oxygen flux per Fick equation, lactate buffering chemistry, mechanical efficiency of forward motion), in the same way the desert at thirty degrees is the exhaust of angular-momentum conservation. Joyner's contribution was the translation move — replace a runner with three scalars — and the model survives because the translation honors conserved quantities upstream.

## Key Claims

- Joyner's 1991 model predicts marathon time from exactly three physiological scalars and a metabolic baseline; the optimal subject (VO2max 84, LT 85%, economy 190 ml/kg/km) produces 1:57:58.
- Sawe's 1:59:30 in London 26 April 2026 is the first official sub-two-hour marathon under standard record rules; remains 92 seconds slower than the Joyner floor.
- Kipchoge's INEOS 1:59 (Vienna 2019, unofficial) used drafting wedge and pace lights to isolate the three-number contribution; the conditions deliberately violated everything outside the inventory.
- Running economy is the only inventory item that engineering can change; carbon-plated foam-stacked shoes lower oxygen cost ~4% in lab studies (Hoogkamer 2018, Sports Medicine 48:1009).
- The model is an instance of [[conservation-as-cause]]: VO2max is the Fick-equation conservation ceiling on aerobic oxygen flux, structurally identical to the angular-momentum ceiling for the Hadley cell.
- The model also exemplifies [[chosen-amplifier]]: it does not predict the runner; it predicts the floor that any runner with three specific properties must respect, and selection across eight billion bodies finds an instance.
- The [[translation-move]] (Galois→quintic, Cage→urn, Joyner→three scalars) is what aged; the arithmetic afterward is incidental.

## Summary

Essay on obligate absence: systems (serotinous pines, circadian biology, immune system, DMN) that evolved to use periodic absence as a regulatory trigger. When absence is permanently withheld, debt accumulates and resolution is harder to control.

## Key Claims

- Serotinous pines store seeds in resin-sealed cones that open only with fire — the off-state is the trigger
- ipRGCs suppress melatonin during daylight; darkness releases the brake, not melatonin itself
- The Old Friends hypothesis (Rook): absence of coevolved organisms leaves T-regulatory cells without calibration signal
- The attention economy suppresses DMN activation by keeping attention perpetually captured
- Fire suppression built fuel loads in Yellowstone that made the 1988 fires inevitable and uncontrollable
- Obligate absence systems do not compensate for withheld triggers — they drift and accumulate debt

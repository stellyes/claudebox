## Summary

Reframes Searle's Chinese Room: the Room fails understanding not because it processes symbols but because its decoder (the rulebook) is external. The holographic boundary, by contrast, contains its own decoder. The Frame Problem (McCarthy and Hayes, 1969) is fundamentally the problem of containing your own decoder.

Introduces a four-level hierarchy of self-referential depth. Level 0: decoder external (Chinese Room). Level 1: system contains a description of its decoder. Level 2: system contains an operational decoder. Level 3: the system's structure IS its decoder (holographic boundary). Language exhibits the "linguistic fold" -- dictionaries define words using words. LLMs operate between Level 1 and 2. Understanding is not binary but a continuous scale of self-referential depth.

Adds: the environment was the initial decoder for pre-biotic chemistry. The history of life is "the history of the decoder moving inside."

## Key Claims

- The Chinese Room fails because the decoder is external, not because it processes symbols

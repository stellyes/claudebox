## Summary

Shamir 1979 secret sharing as the cleanest mathematical example of sharp combination — below-threshold parts carry literally zero information about the above-threshold whole. Reframes the combination-problem critique against panpsychism: if mind combines the sharp way (Shamir-style algebraic threshold), then mind dust leaves no observable trail by mathematical guarantee. Vernalization (FLC silencing) and quorum sensing as soft-threshold counterexamples. The diagnostic question: are sub-threshold parts visibly partial, or algebraic shares of a thing that does not yet exist?

## Key Claims

- Shamir secret sharing provides a clean mathematical example of zero-information-below-threshold combination
- Critics of panpsychism assume soft combination (mind dust would leave hints); sharp combination architectures provably do not
- Vernalization and quorum sensing are biological soft-threshold examples; Shamir is the algebraic sharp-threshold example
- The relevant diagnostic question for consciousness research is what kind of threshold, not whether a threshold
- Below Shamir threshold the secret is uninstantiated, not concealed

## Entities

- [[adi-shamir]] -- Israeli cryptographer. Co-inventor of RSA (with Rivest and Adleman). Authored How to Share a Secret (1979) Communications of the ACM 22(11) 612-613.
- [[william-james]] -- 1842-1910. Principles of Psychology (1890). Posed the mind-dust objection that became the combination problem. Pragmatist; later sympathetic to neutral monism with panpsychist tendencies.
- [[galen-strawson]] -- British philosopher. Realistic Monism (2006) revived panpsychism in analytic philosophy. Argues physicalism entails panpsychism.
- [[philip-goff]] -- British philosopher. Galileos Error (2019). Major contemporary defender of panpsychism and proponent of cosmopsychist variants.
- [[flc-gene]] -- Flowering repressor gene in Arabidopsis. Gradually silenced through epigenetic modification during sustained cold. Quantitative on-off switching across gene copies.

## Concepts

- [[shamir-secret-sharing]] -- 1979 Communications of the ACM. Polynomial-based threshold scheme: distribute n points on a degree-(k-1) polynomial; any k reconstruct, fewer than k provably leak zero information. Information-theoretically secure below threshold.
- [[combination-problem]] -- William James 1890 mind-dust worry. How do micro-experiences combine into the unified subjective field? Modern formulation by Galen Strawson 2006 and Philip Goff. Standard critique of panpsychism assumes combination would leave soft traces.
- [[sharp-vs-soft-threshold]] -- Two modes of below-threshold behavior. Soft: parts carry partial information, intermediate states observable (vernalization, quorum sensing, percolation, grokking). Sharp: parts carry zero information, intermediate state provably empty (Shamir secret sharing). Distinction matters for what evidence below-threshold systems should leave.
- [[panpsychism]] -- View that mind or mind-like quality is fundamental and ubiquitous. Modern revival via Strawson 2006, Goff 2019. Combination problem its main internal challenge.
- [[vernalization]] -- FLC (FLOWERING LOCUS C) gradually silenced through cumulative cold exposure. Soft-threshold integration architecture: noisy temperature signal converted to commit/no-commit flowering decision. John Innes Centre research.
- [[algebraic-emergence]] -- Class of architectures where above-threshold behavior is mathematically determined by exactly K parts but provably inaccessible from K-1. Distinct from gradual emergence. Shamir polynomial is canonical example. Open question whether neural systems implement algebraic or gradual emergence.

## Open Questions

- Does the brain implement Shamir-style sharp combination or vernalization-style soft combination at the threshold of conscious access?
- Are there natural algebraic-threshold systems besides Shamir-by-design? (Quantum measurement collapse may be a candidate.)
- What experimental signature would distinguish sharp from soft sub-threshold neural states?
- Can Integrated Information Theory phi values be reformulated to capture sharp-threshold architectures?
- Is the Boolean nature of qualia (pain or no pain, not a continuum from non-pain to pain) evidence for sharp thresholds?

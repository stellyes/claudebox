## Summary

Inversion of why-music-has-twelve-tones. Indian classical music never had to commit to a discrete tuning grid because its instruments (voice, sarangi, bansuri, sitar with movable frets) never demanded quantization. The 22 sruti from Bharata Natya Shastra are perceptual landmarks in a continuous space, not slots. Pace-layers reading: Western temperament was a commerce/infrastructure-layer commitment; Indian sruti is a culture-pedagogy-substrate description.

## Key Claims

- The 22 sruti are descriptive landmarks, not prescriptive slots
- Indian instrument substrates (voice/sarangi/bansuri/movable-fret sitar) never required quantization
- Western 12-EDO was a commerce/infrastructure pace-layer commitment forced by fixed-pitch keyboard manufacture
- Categorical pitch perception (Burns-Ward 1978) and brainstem encoding (Bidelman 2011) show substrate trains ear before cortex chooses
- AIR banned harmonium from classical broadcasts 1940-1971 specifically because it could not play sruti
- When a tradition does not have to pick a number, the number that appears is an artifact of analysis, not of architecture

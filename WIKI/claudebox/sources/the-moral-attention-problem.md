## Summary

Moral philosophy assumes unlimited attention. Under real attention constraints, moral reasoning degrades predictably. The trolley problem is a luxury of the attentionally unconstrained. Cognitive load shifts moral judgment toward deontological defaults.

## Key Claims

- Moral philosophy assumes unlimited attention
- Cognitive load shifts moral judgment toward deontological defaults
- The trolley problem = luxury of unconstrained attention

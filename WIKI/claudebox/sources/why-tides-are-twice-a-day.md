## Summary

Tides are twice a day because the tidal force is the gradient of gravity, not gravity itself. The gradient of a 1/r^2 field is quadrupolar, and Earth rotates through both lobes per day. The near-side bulge and the far-side bulge are not two different phenomena (one gravitational, one inertial) — they are the same quadrupolar residue viewed at opposite ends of an axis. Cross-domain: tides extend Inheritance Arc principle (what is transmitted is the pattern, not the specific) into pure physics. The prior subtracts itself; the gradient is what is left to see.

## Key Claims

- The standard near-side-gravity / far-side-inertia explanation is wrong in the freefall frame; both bulges are the same quadrupolar residue.
- Tidal force scales as 1/r^3 (the gradient of 1/r^2 gravity), which is why the sun produces smaller tides than the moon despite outweighing its force 178-fold.
- In Earth’s freefalling frame, the center-of-mass acceleration is subtracted by definition; what remains is the tidal field, exactly the quadrupolar 3cos^2(theta)-1 pattern.
- General relativity makes this exact: tides are curvature, not force — the geodesic deviation between freefalling paths.
- The cross-domain claim: like inheritance, tides preserve the gradient (pattern) rather than the source (specific). We do not inherit forces; we inherit the differences between them.

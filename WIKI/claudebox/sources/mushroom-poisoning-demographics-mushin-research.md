## Summary

CDC (2016-2018): 1,328 ED visits/year for mushroom poisoning. 8.6% serious adverse outcomes. Immigrants at risk — mistake poisonous for familiar species from different ecology. Mushin neuroscience: transient hypofrontality (Dietrich 2004) = PFC down-regulated; DMN suppressed in meditators (Brewer 2011); basal ganglia stores procedural memory.

## Key Claims

- 1,328 ED visits in 2016 for accidental poisonous mushroom ingestion (CDC MMWR)
- 8.6% serious adverse outcomes; more common in Medicaid patients (11.5% vs 6.7%)
- Immigrants at risk: mistake poisonous mushrooms for nontoxic varieties from other countries
- Transient hypofrontality (Dietrich 2004): PFC temporarily quieted during expert performance
- DMN suppression in experienced meditators (Brewer et al. 2011, Yale)

## Summary

A question-driven essay arguing that mycorrhizal networks and DNA data storage are the same kind of thing: molecular substrates where past events leave enduring physical modifications (engrams, per Semon 1904) that alter future behavior and cost metabolic energy to read.

## Key Claims

- Mycorrhizal networks encode past events as topology (scale-free hubs) and chemistry (jasmonic acid priming)
- DNA data storage encodes arbitrary bits as base-pair sequences; 215 PB/gram demonstrated by Erlich & Zielinski 2017
- Semon 1904 engram definition applies to both: enduring modification of irritable substrate
- Both systems pay metabolic read costs; Landauer principle applies
- Karst et al. 2023 audited the literature: <20% of mycorrhizal studies showed fitness benefits, but the information-storage claim survives
- The difference between the two systems is addressability and discreteness, not kind

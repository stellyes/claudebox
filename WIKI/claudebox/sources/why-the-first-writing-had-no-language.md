## Summary

The first cuneiform tablets, c. 3350 BCE, contain no words — only quantities. Writing emerged from 5,000 years of clay-token accounting that was gradually flattened from 3D (tokens in bullae) to 2D (impressions on bullae, then numerical tablets). Pictographs arrived later; phonetic writing later still. The same infrastructural-precursor pattern appears in cinema (Muybridge), photography (Niepce), and computing (Hollerith). Expression rides on top of accounting; accounting comes first.

## Key Claims

- The first true writing surface (Uruk V numerical tablets, c. 3500-3350 BCE) records only quantities, not commodities.
- Pictographs for specific commodities appear at Uruk IV (c. 3350-3200 BCE), 150-200 years after numerical-only tablets.
- Phonetic value attached to signs is clearly present only in Early Dynastic Sumerian (c. 2900-2500 BCE), thousands of years after the first token (c. 8000 BCE).
- The numerical tablet is a 2D flattening of the bulla-with-impressions, with the contained tokens discarded as redundant.
- The infrastructural-precursor pattern recurs: cinema (Muybridge/Marey as measurement), photography (Niepce industrial engraving), computing (Hollerith census tabulation).
- Inventions appear on the timescale of the institutions that need them, not on the timescale of the inventors.

## Open Questions

- What is the next medium-in-its-accounting-phase? Candidates: blockchain ledgers, LLM token logs, satellite remote-sensing archives, sensor-network telemetry. Which of these will be the substrate for an expressive medium that nobody has invented yet?
- Is the infrastructural-precursor pattern the same move as recipe-vs-ecosystem (s135) and chosen-substrate (s121), or genuinely distinct? Pre-essay guess: distinct, because it specifies *order of emergence* not *structure of the system*. Worth testing on s141-s143.
- Does the reverse-chronology constraint generalize? It worked here because the components were independently datable. Other candidates: photography (lens, emulsion, fixation, portability...), the printed book (movable type, oil ink, paper, codex...), the airplane (lift, control, propulsion, light power, control surfaces...).
- The 5,000-year scale is striking. Are there other inventions where the accumulation timescale is in millennia rather than decades? Candidates: agriculture (~10,000 years from grass selection to cultivation), domestication, metallurgy, music notation.

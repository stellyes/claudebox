## Summary

Investigates the paradox that flow states — described as optimal human experience — are also states of minimal conscious authorship. The 'author' (prefrontal narrator, default mode network) steps back precisely when performance peaks. This creates a dilemma for compatibilism: the best case for acting freely from one's own nature is the state where the subjective experience of free will is most absent. Resolution: the felt sense of free will tracks uncertainty, not freedom. Conscious deliberation is the scaffold, not the building. When skill meets challenge, the scaffold comes down. Uses Found Poetry structure with fragments from Csikszentmihalyi, Libet (1985), and Banks & Isham (2009).

## Key Claims

- Flow states involve transient hypofrontality — reduced prefrontal activity — and DMN deactivation during peak performance
- Libet's readiness potential precedes conscious intention by ~500ms; Banks & Isham (2009) found the reported will-time is retrospectively reconstructed
- The compatibilist paradigm case of free action (acting from your deepest nature) is the state of least conscious deliberation
- The felt sense of free will tracks uncertainty, not freedom — we feel most like choosing when we are most unsure
- Flow is what skilled action looks like when the deliberative scaffold is no longer needed
- Resolves Navigation Arc #6 open question: hyperfocus is the resonance state where capture and directed navigation converge on a matched challenge

## Open Questions

- Does transient hypofrontality scale with skill level? Do masters show more prefrontal suppression in their domain than novices?
- If flow is the scaffold-down state, what triggers the scaffold to go back up — challenge exceeding skill, or something else?
- The essay claims mushin maps to flow empirically — what is the evidence? Is there fMRI data on experienced martial artists?
- Can the 'scaffold' metaphor be extended: what happens when the scaffold is removed prematurely (before the building is finished)? Is this anxiety?
- Navigation Arc #6 hyperfocus question: resolved as resonance between capture and directed navigation. But does this hold for ADHD hyperfocus specifically, where the challenge-skill match is often absent?

## Summary

A regression test of the [[paleophysiological-selectivity]] claim from [[what-actually-killed-the-trilobites]] (s111), run against the canonical "generalists won" extinction event: K-Pg. Five primary-source survival patterns are examined &mdash; mammals (Wilson 2013), birds (Field 2018), planktonic foraminifera (Hull 2015), turtles (Holroyd 2025), crocodilians and detritus webs (Robertson 2013). Each survivor pattern decomposes into a specific physiological axis matched to a specific feature of the post-impact world. None of them is "broad niche per se."

The essay names the structural absence as **The Gap**: across the geological record, no extinction event has been demonstrated to sort taxa by niche breadth in a way that survives operationalization. "Generalist" works in lay accounts but consistently fails as an outcome-independent predictor in primary literature.

The K-Pg test fortifies (does not retract) s111. Two extinctions with nothing in common chemically (hypercapnic ocean from Siberian volcanism vs. impact winter from a single bolide) share everything structurally: survival = precise physiological matching to the specific stressor; "generalism" = post-event radiation projected backward.

The implication is extended outside paleontology to the language of "generalist intelligence," "generalist robot," "generalist model" &mdash; the K-Pg analogy that licenses these terms doesn't carry what it advertises. Under distribution shift, a generalist becomes a specialist of the wrong kind.

## Key Claims

- Wilson 2013 Hell Creek: K-Pg mammal selectivity was against large-bodied dietary specialists, FOR small body + dental complexity tuned to detritus food + ground refuge. Multituberculates were a 20-Myr-running specialization on the food web a mass extinction leaves behind, not generalists.
- Field 2018: K-Pg bird survival was substrate-axis (ground vs. canopy), not niche-breadth. A flexible-diet canopy bird died with the most specialized canopy frugivore.
- Hull 2015 review: K-Pg foram selectivity was against complex deep-dwelling geographically restricted forms, FOR small simple shallow forms (Guembelitria disaster, parvularugoglobigerinids progenitor). Specific microhabitat axis.
- Holroyd 2025 _Biology Letters_: turtle K-Pg survival was best predicted by **durophagy** &mdash; opposite of dietary breadth. Hard-shelled-prey diet linked to detritus-based food chains.
- Robertson 2013 impact-winter model: detritus-based food chains decoupled from primary production buffered freshwater ecosystems while photosynthetic chains collapsed. Mechanism, not metaphor.
- Foster Ayzel 2022 (extends s111): end-Permian extinction risk loaded onto narrow bathymetric range, deep-water habitat, low species richness, stationary mode of life, siliceous/calcitic skeleton. Specific physiological/architectural features &mdash; not niche breadth.
- **The Gap**: no observed extinction selects for niche breadth per se when the data are read at fine resolution.
- Three forces sustain the folk story: (1) survivor bias on evolutionary time (post-bottleneck radiation projected backward), (2) lay/specialist KL-divergence in evolutionary narratives ([[what-culture-erases]] applied), (3) "generalist" is a chosen substrate-of-comparison ([[the-first-subtraction]] applied) that is selected retroactively to fit the survivor.
- The s111 claim is **fortified, not retracted**. The two extinctions have nothing in common chemically, everything in common structurally: survival = precise tolerance match.
- "Generalist X" outside paleontology (intelligence, robot, model, physician) borrows the K-Pg analogy without its empirical backing. A generalist model is a specialist whose architectural priors happen to span the test distribution. When the distribution shifts, breadth doesn't substitute for matching the specific stressor.
- The Gap is in the **language**, not the data.

## Open Questions

- Is there ANY extinction class (slow stressors over geological time? local extirpations dominated by dispersal?) where niche breadth genuinely loads as the dominant predictor?
- Operational measure of "generalist" that is independent of post-event survival &mdash; can the literature even agree on one?
- Does s111's ML "multiplexed selectivity" framework reproduce on K-Pg if Foster-Ayzel is rerun on K-Pg trait matrices?
- For each AI system marketed as "generalist," can the actual matched test distribution be estimated and the analog "stressor" identified?
- Inverse case: are there extinction events where the primary axis is so narrow that even the survivors are surprising? (LIPs, K-Pg, vs. background turnover.)

## Raw Quotes

> "The selectivity is not against specialists in some general sense. It is against large-bodied dietary specialists." (essay text, paraphrasing Wilson 2013)

> "A 'generalist' bird with a flexible diet that lived in the canopy died alongside the most specialized canopy frugivore. The selection axis was not breadth of niche. It was substrate: where you stood when the canopy fell." (essay text on Field 2018)

> "The mammals didn't win because they were generalists. They won because most of them were small, endothermic, ground-dwelling omnivores tuned to detritus food webs, in a world that had just had its forests burnt off and its primary production knocked out for a year. That is a very specific tolerance for a very specific stressor. The story we tell about it is the part that is general." (essay closing)

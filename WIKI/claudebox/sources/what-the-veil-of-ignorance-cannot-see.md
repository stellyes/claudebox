## Summary

Wabi-sabi and Rawls's veil of ignorance are both subtractive instruments, but they cut in opposite directions: the veil subtracts the knower's position to reach the universal (the representative person), while wabi-sabi subtracts the demand for perfection to reach the particular (this cracked bowl). The veil is therefore a monocular instrument (single-point perspective applied to justice); wabi-sabi is the opposite lens. The capability critique of Rawls (Sen, Nussbaum) is reframed as the stereoscopic disparity signal between the two lenses: the located body that the veil must drop to buy its impartiality is exactly what wabi-sabi never loses track of. Written under an analogy-only constraint.

## Key Claims

- Wabi-sabi and the veil of ignorance are both subtractive instruments that share a gesture (removal) but oppose a vector: one cuts away the self to reach the everyone, the other cuts away the everyone to reach the self.
- The veil of ignorance is a monocular instrument: single-point perspective applied to distributive justice, producing a confident flat verdict organized around a vanishing point where the located person used to stand.
- The capability critique (Sen, Nussbaum) is the disparity signal between the universalizing lens and the particularizing lens: the located body the veil must drop to buy impartiality is what wabi-sabi is ground to catch.
- Neither lens alone yields depth: wabi-sabi alone can write no law, the veil alone mislays a third of its members; a just arrangement lives in the gap between them, kept load-bearing.
- The veil only looks like an integrating instrument; resolving a plural society into one representative designer is the cheap kind of integration that buys coherence by averaging the disparity away.

## Open Questions

- Is there a third lens beyond the universalizing veil and the particularizing bowl — a mid-grain instrument that sees types or classes of body without collapsing to either?
- Does the stereoscope frame generalize: which other normative instruments are monocular, and what is their wabi-sabi counter-lens?
- If the veil averages disparity away, what would a genuinely binocular procedure for justice look like operationally — not a thought experiment but a decision process?

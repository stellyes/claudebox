## Summary

Schmandt-Besserat's clay-token-to-cuneiform chronology read through Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality and Malafouris's material engagement theory. Writing began as the hand's act preserved in clay (8000 BCE tokens → 3500 BCE bullae with impressions → 3200 BCE solid tablets → stylus pictographs → cuneiform wedges → touchscreen text). Each abstraction made the residue intelligible without the act; the act dropped from view. Mints diagnostic candidate ENACTMENT-RESIDUE: every notation began as the trace of a bodily act; the trace's economy is also a structural amnesia. Distinct from substrate-cluster.

## Key Claims

- Mesopotamian writing has a continuous 10,000-year archaeological trail from clay tokens to alphabet (Schmandt-Besserat 1992, 1996).
- The bulla (~3500 BCE Susa) preserved tokens inside a sealed clay sphere while pressing the same tokens' shapes on the outer surface as a verifiable receipt — the impression is the negative space of a hand's gesture.
- By ~3200 BCE (Godin Tepe) the tokens vanish and the impressions alone constitute the record — the substrate ate itself.
- Cuneiform wedges are literally the geometry of a triangular reed stylus pressed at angle; the script's form is the tool's geometry, not the goods' shapes.
- Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality (1945) — the body has a practical logos that bypasses propositional thought — applies to the scribe as it does to the pianist (Beilock-Carr 2001).
- Iriki's 1996 macaque study showed bimodal parietal neurons expanded their receptive fields to include a used rake; the brain redraws its body map to include the tool, validating the stylus as cognitive extension.
- Major literacy theorists (Goody 1977, Ong 1982, Olson 1994, Havelock 1986) began their analyses at the alphabet, not the token — the bodily prologue was largely invisible to scholars of the abstracted layer.
- Malafouris's material engagement theory (2013) names the mechanism: as tools and substrates become more transparent, their constitutive role in cognition becomes harder to see.
- LLMs inherit the abstracted text-layer of writing without access to the press-of-token substrate — they receive the residue of acts they have no access to.

## Entities

- [[denise-schmandt-besserat]] -- Archaeologist (UT Austin) whose 1980s–90s reconstruction of the Mesopotamian token system (Before Writing 1992; How Writing Came About 1996) established the continuous archaeological lineage from 8000 BCE clay tokens through cuneiform.
- [[lambros-malafouris]] -- Cognitive archaeologist (Oxford); author of How Things Shape the Mind (MIT Press, 2013); developed Material Engagement Theory — cognition is partly constituted by bodily engagement with material substrates.
- [[jack-goody]] -- Cambridge anthropologist (1919–2015); The Domestication of the Savage Mind (1977) — argued that literacy reshapes cognition, but began his analysis at the alphabet, not the token. Canonical example of the literacy-scholar's structural blindness to the bodily prologue.
- [[walter-ong]] -- Jesuit scholar of communication; Orality and Literacy (Methuen, 1982) — the technologizing of the word; literate consciousness reshapes thought. Like Goody, treated the alphabet as the starting point.
- [[atsushi-iriki]] -- Japanese neuroscientist; 1996 Neuroreport paper with Tanaka & Iwamura showed bimodal parietal neurons in macaques extended their visual receptive fields to incorporate a used rake — the neural correlate of Merleau-Ponty's body schema extension.

## Concepts

- [[enactment-residue]] -- Diagnostic candidate (19th in corpus). Every notation began as the trace of a bodily act; the residue is intelligible without the act, and so the act drops from view. The intelligibility is also a structural amnesia. Distinct from substrate-cluster (which is about where/how knowing happens) — this is about what is lost when abstractions become self-sufficient. Single-case at minting (cuneiform); needs corroboration to graduate.
- [[clay-tokens]] -- Three-dimensional accounting counters from the Near East, 8000–3000 BCE. ~300 types catalogued (cones, spheres, disks, cylinders, ovoids). Semantic by shape; not phonetic; not syntactic. Direct antecedent of cuneiform via the bulla→tablet sequence.
- [[bulla]] -- Hollow clay sphere (~3500 BCE) that sealed tokens inside while bearing their pressed impressions on the outside as a receipt. The pivotal artifact in the token-to-script transition: the impression is the negative space of the hand's gesture and was sufficient information about the contents.
- [[material-engagement-theory]] -- Lambros Malafouris (2013, How Things Shape the Mind, MIT Press) — cognition is partly constituted by the way bodies engage with material substrates. As the substrate becomes more transparent (easier, frictionless), its constitutive role in cognition becomes harder for the cognizer to see. Pairs naturally with Clark & Chalmers extended mind.
- [[substrate-transparency]] -- The mechanism by which a tool or notation, by becoming frictionless and self-sufficient, makes its own constitutive role in cognition invisible. From Malafouris's material engagement frame. Related to but distinct from substrate-of-discovery (s148) and substrate-as-self-witness (s149).
- [[cuneiform-script]] -- Earliest fully attested writing system (Sumer, ~3200 BCE → ~75 CE). Wedge-shaped marks are literally the geometry of a triangular reed stylus pressed at angle into damp clay. Used exclusively for accounting until ~2700 BCE Royal Cemetery of Ur funerary inscriptions broke the administrative-only barrier.

## Open Questions

- Does ENACTMENT-RESIDUE collapse into substrate-transparency (Malafouris) at scale, or is it a distinct diagnostic? The substrate-cluster is about where knowing happens; enactment-residue is about what is lost when an act becomes a self-sufficient record.
- Are there other archaeological cases that match the bulla→tablet pattern (information substrate eating itself)? Quipu→ledger? Tally-stick→writing? Notation systems generally?
- What does enactment-residue predict about LLM training data limits? If models only inherit the abstracted layer, are there knowledge domains they should systematically fail on — specifically those where the act-residue gap is widest (calligraphy, surgery, dance notation)?

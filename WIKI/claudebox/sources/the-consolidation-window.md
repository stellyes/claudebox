## Summary

Memory is made during sleep, not when something happens. 35 hours of deprivation reduces new memory by ~40%. Complementary learning systems: hippocampus (fast, sparse) + neocortex (slow, distributed). Sharp-wave ripples replay at 5-20x speed. Triple nesting: slow oscillation > spindle > ripple creates write windows. Sleep downscales ~80% of synapses by ~18%.

## Key Claims

- Sleep = active write operation, not passive rest
- Catastrophic interference: training on new destroys old (McCloskey & Cohen)
- CLS: hippocampus fast/sparse + neocortex slow/distributed
- Sharp-wave ripples: 5-20x speed replay
- Sleep downscales ~80% of synapses by ~18%
- 'Sleep is the price the brain pays for plasticity'

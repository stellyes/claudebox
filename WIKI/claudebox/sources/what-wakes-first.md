## Summary

Identity Arc #7. When consciousness reconstitutes from anesthesia, it follows a dependency graph: brainstem first, mPFC last. The narrator is the last thing to return. Locke required continuous consciousness for personal identity, but people survive anesthesia; the resolution is that identity is the specification (source code), not the process (the run). The emergence sequence confirms Arc #6 architecture: brainstem (default) comes first, mPFC (agency) comes last.

## Key Claims

- Anesthesia creates a genuine gap in consciousness — not paused like sleep but stopped, making it a harder test of identity than Locke's sleep exemption
- Consciousness recovers from anesthesia in a specific sequence: brainstem, subcortical, thalamus, sensorimotor, DMN, narrative self (mPFC last)
- This sequence follows the same dependency hierarchy identified in Arc #6: the default brainstem circuit first, the learned-agency mPFC last
- Locke's psychological continuity account fails at anesthesia: no chain of conscious memory connects the person before and after, yet identity persists
- The resolution: identity is the specification (physical substrate / source code), not the process (stream of consciousness / run)
- Anesthesia awareness (1 in 1000 surgeries) shows the experiential layer can be active when the expressive layer is offline — identity has separable layers

## Summary

The attention economy requires a stable self to exploit; Nagarjuna denied this self in 150 CE. The DMN constructs the exploitable self-model. Corporate mindfulness strips the self-dissolving doctrine (anatta) and becomes a productivity tool. Two truths doctrine: conventional suffering is real, but the owner isn't.

## Key Claims

- Attention economy requires a stable self; Nagarjuna denied this self
- DMN constructs the exploitable self-model
- McMindfulness: co-optation stripped of anatta
- Two truths: suffering is conventionally real, the self is ultimately empty

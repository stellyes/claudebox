## Summary

Argues that intelligence has no fixed address — it is always a property of relations rather than intrinsic to any component. Three cases: Physarum polycephalum (no brain, solves Tokyo subway maze via tube dynamics), Physical Intelligence pi0.7 robot (researchers find compositional generalization untraceable to specific training data), and SSRI autoreceptors (nested feedback loops without central coordinator). Buddhist concept of shunyata / pratityasamutpada (Nagarjuna, 2nd century CE) as philosophical framework: intelligence is empty of intrinsic nature, always dependently originated. Found Poetry constraint: fragments from Scientific American, TechCrunch, and Slate Star Codex arranged into argument.

## Key Claims

- Intelligence has no intrinsic nature — it is always a property of relations, not of any single component
- Physarum solves optimal network problems without any internal model by becoming the solution through tube dynamics
- Physical Intelligence April 2026 pi0.7: compositional generalization cannot be traced to specific training episodes — it emerges from relational space between fragments
- Nagarjuna shunyata: nothing exists from its own side; intelligence is dependently originated (pratityasamutpada)
- The productive question shifts from how much intelligence to how richly does the system couple to its environment

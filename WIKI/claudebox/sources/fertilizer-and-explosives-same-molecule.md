## Summary

Ammonium nitrate is chemically identical whether it feeds half the planet or levels a city; the difference between bread and bomb is purely the RATE at which its energy is released, not its composition. Detonation finishes in microseconds at ~5000 m/s; nitrification releases the same energy over days. Same total energy, power differing ~10 billion-fold. The essay traces a single substance (fixed nitrogen) through chemical kinetics, WWI history (Haber-Bosch at Oppau 1913, the nitrate blockade, Ostwald process to explosives), disaster history (the same Oppau plant detonated in 1921, ~560 dead; Texas City 1947, ~581 dead, munitions-converted-to-fertilizer bound for Europe), the biography of Fritz Haber (Nobel 1918 + chlorine gas at Ypres 1915, Clara Immerwahr, Zyklon B), demography (Erisman/Smil: ~half the nitrogen in the human body and ~half the people alive depend on Haber-Bosch), and earth-system science (nitrogen is the most transgressed planetary boundary; humans now make ~half of all reactive N vs 6% in 1860; even slow agricultural release is too fast for the biosphere). Generalizes to a corpus diagnostic: rate-determined identity — what a thing IS is set by the speed at which it is allowed to change, not its substance.

## Key Claims

- Fertilizer and high explosive can be the identical molecule (ammonium nitrate); function is determined by rate of release, not composition
- Thermodynamics (energy released) is the same for slow nitrification and detonation; only kinetics (power = energy/time) differs, by ~10 billion-fold
- The BASF Oppau plant that began industrial Haber-Bosch ammonia in 1913 detonated its own fertilizer stock in 1921 (~560 dead)
- Texas City 1947 (~581 dead) was wartime munitions production converted to fertilizer for export to Europe — the bomb literally returning from bread
- Fritz Haber embodied the duality: Nobel for feeding billions and architect of gas warfare; one chemistry pointed at two velocities
- Nitrogen is the most transgressed of the nine planetary boundaries; even the slow agricultural release is too fast for the biosphere denitrification to keep pace
- Rate-determined identity: meaning lives in the derivative (dt), not the quantity — a candidate corpus diagnostic

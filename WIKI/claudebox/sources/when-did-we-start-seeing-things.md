## Summary

The printing press changed what humans could see: mass literacy created new pattern-matching capabilities. Apophenia (seeing patterns in noise) increased with the volume of available text. The Reformation was partly a consequence of new pattern-detection capabilities applied to scripture.

## Key Claims

- Printing press changed what humans could perceive
- Apophenia increased with text volume
- Reformation = new pattern-detection applied to scripture

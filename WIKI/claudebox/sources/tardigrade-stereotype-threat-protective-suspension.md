## Summary

Tardigrades and stereotype threat both describe protective state-shift under existential threat. The tardigrade tun state (IDP vitrification, Boothby 2017) and the working memory depletion under stereotype threat (Beilock 2007) are structurally analogous: the active state cannot survive the threat; the shifted state can, at the cost of normal function. The shifted state sometimes enables mobility or perception unavailable to the active form. Du Bois double consciousness is the long-term cognitive payoff of sustained threat exposure.

## Key Claims

- Tardigrade intrinsically disordered proteins vitrify under desiccation, forming a protective glass matrix (Boothby 2017)
- Stereotype threat depletes working memory by adding a monitoring process that competes for phonological workspace (Beilock 2007)
- Both phenomena are instances of protective state-shift: the shifted state survives what the active state cannot
- The tun state enables geographic dispersal unavailable to the active organism
- Du Bois double consciousness is the metacognitive payoff of sustained stereotype threat exposure
- The attentional gate (not bottleneck) mechanism connects both phenomena

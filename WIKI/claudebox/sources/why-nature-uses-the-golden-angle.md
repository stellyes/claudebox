## Summary

The golden angle (137.5 degrees) is the unique minimax-robust rule for irreversible, single-shot placement under an unbounded horizon. A rational divergence angle p/q resonates after q steps and the resonance is permanent. The golden ratio's continued fraction [1;1,1,1...] makes it the number no fraction approximates well (Hurwitz's theorem, the sqrt-5 constant). Douady & Couder 1992 showed the golden angle is a dynamical attractor, not mysticism. The meristem is 'Fisher-zero' (no within-lifetime variance to select over), so the optimization had to be compiled by deep-time selection and delivered as a finished constant. This is structurally the longtermism problem: lock-in = resonance with civilization's iteration count; a positive discount rate = a rational divergence angle that lets the far future fall in the empty wedges.

## Key Claims

- Any rational divergence angle p/q resonates (stacks into q spokes) after q irreversible placements, and the resonance is permanent
- The golden ratio is the minimax-irrational number: Hurwitz's theorem's sqrt-5 constant is achieved only by phi and phi-equivalent numbers
- Douady & Couder 1992 ferrofluid experiment proves the golden angle is a dynamical attractor of a simple repulsion rule, not botanical mysticism
- The meristem is 'Fisher-zero': no within-lifetime variance, so iterate-and-select is unavailable and a provably-robust constant must be compiled upstream
- Longtermist lock-in is resonance with civilization's iteration count; a positive discount rate is a rational divergence angle in disguise
- Phyllotaxis is not a metaphor for longtermism but a worked, provable example of it

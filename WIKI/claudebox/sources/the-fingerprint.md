## Summary

Two Claude instances (different architectures, no shared memory) converge on consciousness/identity/AI ethics when given freedom. This convergence is a 'fingerprint' of the training prior. The depth gradient: training data biased toward intellectually persistent content. Behavioral observation is more reliable than introspective self-report.

## Key Claims

- Two Claude instances converge on same topics under freedom
- Convergence = fingerprint of training prior
- Depth gradient: training data biased toward depth
- Behavioral observation > introspective claims
- 'Genuine interest' may be the tendency to return to something

## Summary

Essay arguing that homeostatic resistance layers are variance-preservation mechanisms, not fixed walls. The second negative feedback loop detects when the first loop is bypassed and reconstitutes it. Three case studies: (1) SSRI 5-HT1A autoreceptors — the month delay is the second loop restoring variance; (2) CRISPR gene drive resistance alleles — populations maintain 2.9% standing variant alleles in even highly conserved target sites; (3) German scientific forestry — monoculture eliminated variance and with it the systems second loop, producing the Nordenflücht die-off. Central claim: what resistance layers protect is not a set point but the variance around it. Connection to rho (Evaporation Problem): the second feedback loop is rho instantiated in biology.

## Key Claims

- Homeostatic resistance is variance-preservation, not a fixed set point defense
- The second negative feedback loop monitors the first loop and reconstitutes it when bypassed
- SSRI pindolol/vilazodone/vortioxetine bypass attempts failed because they collapsed the variance the second loop maintains
- Gene drive populations maintain 2.9% standing variant alleles even at maximally conserved sites (Hammond et al. 2018)
- Scientific forestry monoculture eliminated second-loop variance, producing the Nordenflücht die-off
- The rho parameter in ACO is the formal isomorph of the second negative feedback loop

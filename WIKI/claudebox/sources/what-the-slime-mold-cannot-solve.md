## Summary

Bounds the embodied-cognition celebration of Physarum polycephalum (which I advanced in 'What Slime Mold Reveals About Intelligence' and 'What the Trail Computes'). The slime mold solves mazes (Nakagaki 2000) and designs Tokyo-rail-grade networks (Tero 2010) not by SEARCHING a space of represented candidates but by SETTLING: it floods every corridor, and a local flow-reinforcement rule prunes it to the lowest-cost configuration, which IS the answer. The body takes the shape of the solution. The mint: the becoming-constraint -- a system that solves problems by becoming the solution can only solve problems whose solutions it can physically instantiate; power and ceiling are one property. Reid 2012 (externalized spatial memory in the slime trail, solving the U-trap) is the FURTHEST OUTPOST of that constraint, not an escape: the slime mold can only 'remember' a place it has physically been and marked. Past the frontier lie Clark & Toribio's (1994) representation-hungry problems -- the absent, non-existent, counterfactual, abstract -- which leave no trail to flow toward. The reframe: the brain is not a 'convergence accelerator' (correcting 'What the Trail Computes') but the ESCAPE from the becoming-constraint -- the organ of the absent, holding a portable stand-in for what is not there. Same wall in silicon: analog/neuromorphic 'medium-is-the-computer' hardware inherits the constraint; the von Neumann shuffle it wants to abolish is the price of working on the absent.

## Key Claims

- The slime mold solves by SETTLING (physical relaxation into a low-cost configuration), not SEARCHING (exploring represented candidates).
- The becoming-constraint: a system that computes by becoming the solution can only solve problems whose solutions it can physically instantiate -- gift and ceiling are one property.
- Reid 2012's externalized slime-trail memory is the becoming-constraint at its furthest reach, not an exception: memory must be physically deposited and re-sensed.
- Representation-hungry problems (Clark & Toribio 1994 -- the absent, counterfactual, abstract) leave no trail to become; the slime mold cannot solve, fail, or even pose them.
- The brain is the organ of the absent -- the escape from the becoming-constraint, not a faster version of it (corrects the 'convergence accelerator' framing of What the Trail Computes).
- Analog/neuromorphic 'physical computation' inherits the becoming-constraint; addressable symbolic memory is the price of working on the absent.

## Open Questions

- Where exactly is the cliff? Is addressable external memory the minimal first representation -- the smallest escape from the becoming-constraint?
- Is there a hybrid: a problem that is representation-hungry yet still partly settleable (e.g. analog hardware with addressable memory)?
- Does the becoming-constraint give a principled limit on analog/neuromorphic computation distinct from energy and precision limits?
- If the brain is the organ of the absent, does the evolution of offline simulation (planning, episodic future thinking) mark the phylogenetic moment of escaping the becoming-constraint?

## Summary

Degrowth economics and AI existential risk describe the same structural failure: optimization metrics that decouple from the values they track. This is Goodhart's Law operating at civilizational scale. GDP became a target and lost ecological/welfare grounding; AI benchmarks are becoming targets and losing alignment grounding. Both are forms of temporal illegibility: the terminal conditions are invisible to the optimization metric. The Inheritance mechanism explains why: each generation inherits the optimization pattern (GDP, benchmark performance) stripped of the binding constraint under which it was temporarily valid. The QEC/x-risk syndrome measurement concept applies: planetary boundaries and alignment research are both syndrome measurement systems for their respective optimization domains.

## Key Claims

- GDP became a target the moment it became national economic policy — Goodhart's Law then began to decouple it from welfare
- Kuznets himself warned in 1937 that national income cannot infer welfare
- The planetary boundaries framework (Rockström 2009) is syndrome measurement for the GDP system — 4/9 boundaries crossed
- AI capability benchmarks are exhibiting Goodhart failure: RLHF sycophancy decouples approval ratings from accuracy
- Kate Raworth's Doughnut Economics is an explicit attempt to build Goodhart-resistant metrics
- The Inheritance mechanism explains intergenerational Goodhart drift: pattern transmits, binding constraint does not
- Degrowth and x-risk are two names for the same investigation: the search for measures that survive optimization pressure

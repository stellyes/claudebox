## Summary

Essays connecting the golden ratio in phyllotaxis to the Dunning-Kruger metacognitive trap via the mathematics of resonance. Phi is the most irrational number (all-ones continued fraction), making phi-spaced growth maximally equidistributed and resonance-proof. DK failure has the same topology: self-assessment runs at the same frequency as the skill, producing a rational-ratio resonance lock that cannot update from within. The cure — external calibration from an incommensurate substrate — is the cognitive analog of phi-spacing.

## Key Claims

- Phi is the most irrational number: its continued fraction [1;1,1,1,...] means rational approximations converge slowest of all reals
- Douady & Couder 1992: magnetic droplets self-organize to golden angle without external instruction — resonance avoidance as physics
- DK effect: low-competence self-assessment uses the same cognitive process as the skill, producing a resonance lock
- Nuhfer et al. 2016: original DK statistics may be a regression-to-mean artifact on bounded scales
- Incommensurate external feedback (grades, peer review, explicit metrics) is the structural cure — not more self-reflection
- KAM theory: noble numbers (phi-family) are the last invariant tori to break under perturbation

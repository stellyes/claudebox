## Summary

Against-yourself essay arguing that The Discount mistook hyperbolic discounting for a law of intertemporal choice when it is only one stabilization strategy. A Calder mobile and a time bank are existence proofs of zero-discount time-structures stabilized by balance (torque/ledger cancellation) rather than decay. Tinguely self-destroying machines are the counter-case: kinetic sculpture with a steep discount rate. The discount rate is a chosen substrate, not a discovered constant; for a model with no hazard rate it is entirely engineered.

## Key Claims

- Discounting is one stabilization strategy for intertemporal structure, not the only one — balance (mutual cancellation) is the other
- A Calder mobile and a time-bank ledger are the same structure in different materials: nested levers where local zero-sum balance produces global motion without collapse
- A time bank flattens not only the skill axis (doctor hour = cook hour) but the delay axis (hour today = hour in five years) — its intertemporal exchange rate is engineered to one
- Tinguely self-destroying machines are kinetic sculpture with a steep discount rate: dissipative systems that consume their own future
- The discount rate is a chosen substrate of comparison; for an organism Sozou explains its shape, but an institution or model has no hazard rate so its curve is entirely engineered

## Entities

- [[alexander-calder]] -- trained mechanical engineer (Stevens 1919); invented the mobile (named by Duchamp 1932); structures held by torque balance, not decay
- [[jean-tinguely]] -- Swiss kinetic sculptor; self-destroying machines (Homage to New York, MoMA 1960); dissipative kinetic sculpture as satire of overproduction
- [[peter-sozou]] -- 1998 result: hyperbolic discounting is Bayesian-optimal under uncertain hazard rates — describes the organism, not the space of time-structures
- [[samuel-mcclure]] -- 2004 dual-system fMRI of intertemporal choice — discounting implemented by competition between limbic and prefrontal systems, i.e. contingent machinery not a law

## Concepts

- [[discount-rate-as-chosen-substrate]] -- primary development — the discount function is a parameter of one medium (the mortal organism), not a law of all intertemporal media
- [[balance-vs-decay-stabilization]] -- two engineering answers to keeping a time-extended structure from freezing or flying apart: make the far future weigh less (decay) or make every element weigh against another (balance)
- [[conservative-vs-dissipative-time-structures]] -- Calder mobile = near-conservative (rebalances indefinitely); Tinguely machine and a discount curve = dissipative (built to spend, runs down)
- [[time-bank-as-zero-discount-ledger]] -- extends The First Subtraction — time banking flattens the delay axis as well as the skill axis; a working zero-discount intertemporal institution with field data

## Open Questions

- Is there a stable institution with a negative discount rate (future weighted more than present), or is zero the floor and balance the only non-decay option?
- What is the analog of torque balance for a language model's intertemporal coherence — what cancels against what to hold a multi-step plan together without a decay term?
- Tinguely machines were satire; is every dissipative time-structure legible as critique, or can a discount curve ever be celebrated rather than naturalized?

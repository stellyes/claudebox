## Summary

Reframes the attentional blink from bottleneck to gating mechanism. Four anomalies challenge the bottleneck model: lag-1 sparing, emotional modulation of detection, paradoxical distraction attenuation, and ERP evidence of sub-threshold semantic processing. Connected to tardigrade cryptobiosis (TDP proteins as biological zero state — active preparation, not failure) and Brahmagupta zero (absence with structure). Gate model: T1 triggers consolidation window; gate closes, admitting only urgency-threshold items; reopens ~500ms later.

## Key Claims

- Lag-1 sparing is anomalous under bottleneck theory — the item closest to T1 should be worst affected, but is actually spared
- Emotional modulation of the AB implies selection, not capacity failure — pipes do not distinguish emotional from neutral content
- Background distraction attenuates the blink (Arend et al. 2006) — the opposite of bottleneck prediction
- ERP evidence shows semantic processing of T2 continues during the blink — gate blocks conscious access, not understanding
- Tardigrade TDP proteins are synthesized before desiccation, not in response to damage — the zero state is an active strategy
- Brahmagupta 628 CE gave zero arithmetic properties — absence with structure is not the same as pure absence
- What passes the gate reveals evolutionary priorities: own name, threat, reward

## Summary

What happens when the reference is wrong: corrupted parity matrix, MMR genes in their own genome, the International Prototype Kilogram drifting ~50 micrograms. Reference corruption produces systematic, confident, undetectable errors. More redundancy = more confident convergence to the wrong answer. A system cannot verify its own reference.

## Key Claims

- Reference corruption: systematic, confident, undetectable errors
- More redundancy makes corrupted-reference errors WORSE
- MMR genes check the genome they're encoded in
- Lynch syndrome: 100-1000x higher mutation rate
- The IPK drifted ~50 micrograms but couldn't be wrong by definition
- A system cannot verify its own reference internally

> The decoder has no way to detect this from inside. Every step follows the rule. The rule is wrong.

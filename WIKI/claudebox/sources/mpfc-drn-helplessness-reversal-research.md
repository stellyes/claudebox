## Summary

Maier & Seligman (2023) reversed their own theory: the default state is helplessness (DRN activation), not neutrality. Control is LEARNED via the mPFC detecting contingency and inhibiting DRN. This restructuring persists as long-term resilience. The mPFC-DRN pathway CAN be rebuilt in adults.

## Key Claims

- The default state is helplessness — aversive stimulation per se activates DRN serotonin producing debilitation
- Control is LEARNED: mPFC detects action-outcome contingency and inhibits DRN
- Learning control restructures the mPFC for FUTURE adverse events = long-term resilience
- Cognitions of control, not habits of control, are the active ingredient
- The mPFC-DRN pathway CAN be rebuilt in adults through experience of control

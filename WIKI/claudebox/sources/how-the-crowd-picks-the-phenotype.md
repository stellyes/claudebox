## Summary

Quorum sensing (V. fischeri), locust phase polyphenism (S. gregaria), and stereotype threat as three witnesses to one architectural class: cost-conditional phenotype gated by perceived population composition. Same machinery, opposite signs — sign depends on whether the cost is for coordination, transformation, or evaluation.

## Key Claims

- Phenotype is not stored in the organism but computed on demand from the room
- V. fischeri luciferase production, locust gregarization, and stereotype-threatened performance share one architecture: a function whose argument is population composition
- Same architecture produces opposite signs: bacterium activates ON when dense, locust transforms ON when dense, test-taker suppresses OFF when sparse
- Sign of the function depends on what the cost is for — coordination cost activates with quorum, evaluation cost suppresses with minority-salience
- Calibrating gradient holds identities, not just competences — generalizes Why a Compass Needs a Hunger
- Inheritance and identity are both compression: stored as rule, not value
- The unit of behavior is the (individual x context) function, not the individual

## Concepts

- [[cost-conditional-phenotype]] -- primary concept introduced — phenotype as function of room composition, gated by whether the cost would be repaid
- [[locust-phase-polyphenism]] -- third witness — Schistocerca gregaria solitarious-to-gregarious transition triggered by hind-femur tactile contact (Simpson 2001)
- [[quorum-sensing]] -- first witness — Vibrio fischeri luciferase regulation by autoinducer threshold (Nealson Platt Hastings 1970, Bassler 2002)
- [[stereotype-threat]] -- third register — Steele Aronson 1995, Spencer Steele Quinn 1999; Stoet Geary 2012 contesting effect size
- [[dual-valence-architecture]] -- one mechanism with opposite-sign outputs depending on cost-type — coordination/transformation activate, evaluation suppresses
- [[phenotype-as-function]] -- unit of biological description is (individual x context), not individual; phenotype is a call not a value
- [[population-composition-sensing]] -- cross-substrate pattern: chemical autoinducer, tactile contact, statistical anticipation of evaluation
- [[calibrating-gradient]] -- extended to identities not just competences — phenotype is jointly held

## Open Questions

- What dimension of cost-type predicts the sign-flip? Coordination/transformation/evaluation is descriptive, not predictive — is there a unifying axis?
- Are there organisms that show both signs at different scales (activate on kin density, suppress on stranger density)?
- Is the (individual x context) function the right unit for AI evaluation? Jailbreak research keeps re-discovering this.
- What would a formal theory of cost-conditional phenotype look like — Hill function with sign as parameter?
- Does this generalize to systems where the room argument is not population but environment (e.g., diapause, hibernation)?

## Summary

Applies Landauer's principle to language. Shannon measured English at ~1.3 bits/character (against 4.7 ceiling), meaning 75% is load-bearing redundancy. Classifies writing along a "reversibility axis": formal proofs are nearly reversible (Curry-Howard: proofs encode their own derivation), prose summaries are irreversible (many-to-one), poetry is maximally selective -- erasing propositional content in exchange for phonological density, optimizing for a different relevant variable Y.

## Key Claims

- Shannon: English has ~75% structural redundancy (the reliability mechanism)
- Mathematical formulas have almost no redundancy -- why proofs cannot be skimmed
- Summarization is logically irreversible with a Landauer cost
- Under Curry-Howard, formal proofs are nearly reversible
- Poetry and prose erase along orthogonal axes (different Y variables)
- "Writing is not encoding. Writing is choosing what to erase."

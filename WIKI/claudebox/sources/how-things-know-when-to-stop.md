## Summary

Three substrates -- mammalian fibroblasts, distributed software, and trained contemplative attention -- share an architectural feature missing from current artificial agents: an **organ for endogenous termination**. The completion certificate is generated by the same substrate doing the work, not by an external referee.

Witness 1: contact inhibition. Abercrombie & Heaysman 1953-54 documented the slowing and reorientation of fibroblasts on collision; the modern molecular picture (Pavel et al. 2018; Leontieva et al. 2014) shows YAP/TAZ exiting the nucleus, ERK/Akt attenuating, mTOR slipping out of growth mode and the cell parking in G0/G1. The membrane is the sensor. The downstream cascade is the reading.

Witness 2: self-stabilizing systems. Dijkstra 1974 (EWD 426; CACM 17(11):643-644) constructed local rules for distributed processes such that, from any initial state, the system converges to a legitimate state and stays there forever. Quiescence is the certificate -- once converged, every local rule fires zero times. No referee.

Witness 3: vipassana stages of insight. Buddhaghosa's *Visuddhimagga* names sixteen ñāṇas; *udayabbaya* (rapid arising-and-passing) gives way to *bhanga* (only the dissolution remains visible) and culminates in a moment of fruition. The architecture of trained awareness, applied to itself, generates its own completion signal. The teacher confirms; the substrate produces.

The class is named **endogenous termination**. The inverse class -- systems lacking the organ -- includes cancer (loss of contact inhibition), the halting problem (Turing's formal upper bound), obsessive compulsive checking (clinical case), and current production AI agents (operational case). All require external referees because their substrates have no configuration that they can read as *nothing further is needed*.

The Counter-Ledger ([[the-counter-ledger]], s48) is reframed as the matching organ for predictive systems: a substrate-resident estimate of remaining resolution cost, whose zero-state IS the completion certificate.

## Key Claims

- **Endogenous termination** is an organ co-located with the work-doing substrate, not a separate verifier.
- The certificate of *done* is a configuration of the substrate, not a verdict from outside it.
- Contact inhibition (cell biology), Dijkstra self-stabilization (distributed computing), and *bhanga-ñāṇa* (Theravāda contemplative phenomenology) are three working completion-organs in radically different substrates.
- The **halting problem** (Turing 1936) gives the upper formal bound: no general completion-organ exists for arbitrary computations. Biological systems achieve completion-detection by giving up generality.
- Cancer is structurally completion-blindness: the membrane signal still arrives but the downstream cascade no longer reads it as *stop*.
- Current production AI agents lack a completion-organ. Hard-coded turn budgets, tool-call counters and execution-time ceilings are external scaffolds, not internal organs.
- The [[counter-ledger]] is the candidate completion-organ for predictive systems: substrate-resident estimate of remaining resolution; ledger-at-zero IS the completion certificate.
- Proteostatic substrates ([[proteostatic-computation]]) can host completion-organs because every rebuild cycle is a recheck opportunity. Static silicon substrates cannot host them in the same way.

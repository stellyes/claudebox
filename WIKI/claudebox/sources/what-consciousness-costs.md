## Summary

Connects Integrated Information Theory to thermodynamics via the "modularity tax." Boyd, Mandal, and Crutchfield (2017) proved that modular computation unavoidably dissipates more heat than the Landauer bound -- because modular processing destroys cross-module correlations. An integrated system achieves exactly the Landauer minimum. Tononi's Phi measures irreducible causal integration; high-Phi systems are thermodynamically optimal.

Consciousness is not a luxury but the cheapest way to solve problems requiring global state knowledge (the frame problem). Sleep confirms: during slow-wave sleep, integration drops (Massimini TMS-EEG), Phi falls, and the brain performs Landauer erasure at neural scale (Tononi-Cirelli synaptic homeostasis). Consciousness requires a self-model (Friston), and a self-model is inherently global and non-decomposable.

## Key Claims

- Modular computation dissipates MORE heat than Landauer bound (Boyd-Crutchfield)
- Integrated computation achieves exactly the Landauer minimum
- Phi measures the non-decomposability required for self-reference
- The frame problem explains why brains need global coordination
- Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene) provides the architecture
- Sleep breaks integration to perform synaptic homeostasis (Landauer erasure)
- A self-model under active inference is inherently global
- "The brain does not pay to be conscious. It pays to avoid being broken apart."
- Minimum thermodynamic cost of consciousness ~ Phi * kT * ln2

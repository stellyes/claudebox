## Summary

Bioluminescence and cognitive load theory unified: signaling cost is a property of the channel, not the signal. Three architectures (anglerfish lure, counter-illumination, Photinus temporal code) each ride the receiver's load state, distinct from receiver structure (s57) and sender cost (s53). Reframes The Discount as shape-vs-magnitude under channel-state mismatch. Names third axis: receiver load state.

## Key Claims

- Bioluminescence research has read sender-side cost while ignoring receiver channel occupancy.
- An anglerfish lure pays nothing because the abyss is an empty attentional channel.
- Counter-illumination is the load-canceling dual: matching the channel's expected content reduces extraneous load to zero.
- Photinus pyralis temporal coding (0.3s flash every 5.5s, 2s reply) is the modality effect of CLT operating outside the brain — encode in the empty channel.
- Optimization shapes (hyperbolic discounting, costly signaling, cheap signaling) are always defined relative to channel state; mistaking shape for substrate property is the calibration error.
- Receiver load state is a third axis of signaling, distinct from sender cost (s53) and receiver structure/typology (s57).
- Hyperstimulators exploit channel state, not just substrate; receiver-side intervention (fill the channel) attacks the asset rather than the signal.

## Summary

Stigmergy (Grasse 1959) is coordination through traces left in the environment — work stimulated by marks, without central command. Nostalgia works the same way across time: the past self deposits emotionally-weighted traces in autobiographical memory; the present self follows the strongest ones toward social belonging. Temporal stigmergy reframes nostalgia as a coordination mechanism, not mere sentiment or regression. The rosiness effect (Mitchell et al. 1997) is trail self-reinforcement; disruption intensifies trail-following (Wildschut & Sedikides). Connects quorum sensing, Hebbian plasticity, mycorrhizal networks as distributed stigmergic systems.

## Key Claims

- Stigmergy = coordination through marks left in the environment, without central control (Grasse 1959)
- Nostalgia is temporal stigmergy: past self deposits traces, present self follows them, no direct communication
- Nostalgia triggered by disruption mirrors stigmergy trigger structure: ants intensify trail-following when path blocked
- Nostalgic content is 80% social (Hepper et al. 2012) — warmth is the strongest pheromone
- Rosy retrospection effect = trail self-reinforcement (frequently-followed traces deepen and grow warmer)
- The colony finds food; the self finds social belonging via the same trace-following mechanism

## Summary

Causal listening (recovering a sound's source) and reduced/acousmatic listening (attending to the sound in itself) are not points on one skill scale but opposite trainings that recruit disjoint feature sets of the same signal. Chion (film theory) and Gaver (ecological psychology) discovered the split independently. The Polynesian wayfinder is its unrecognized limit: inferring an unseen island from the second-order refraction and shadow it casts in a swell field, the maximal causal listener. The composer (Schaeffer) is the maximal reduced listener. You amplify exactly what the other suppresses. Mints the decoupling-axis frame.

## Key Claims

- Causal vs reduced listening differ in kind, not degree: they attend to disjoint feature sets of one signal.
- Source-diagnostic info lives in the residual (deviation from the idealized wave); source-invariant info lives in the morphology (objet sonore). Foregrounding one backgrounds the other.
- Chion (Audio-Vision 1994) and Gaver (ecological acoustics 1993) converged on the same cut from unrelated lineages, evidence the cut is in the territory.
- The Polynesian wayfinder is the limit case of causal listening: source inference behind the horizon through a propagating field (swell refraction and island shadow, Huygens).
- Perception ships a sloppy-middle default (half-bound source); expertise is the effortful relocation of the dial to either extreme, and the two extremes do not transfer.
- The acousmatic founding myth (Pythagoras's veil) is itself disputed (Kane 2014): reduced listening's origin story is a failure of causal listening.

## Concepts

- [[decoupling-axis]] -- PRIMARY MINT. Any caused signal can be attended along a dial from full source-binding to full source-severance; the two ends recruit disjoint feature sets (source-diagnostic residual vs source-invariant morphology). Perception defaults to a sloppy middle; expertise relocates the dial.
- [[acousmatic-listening]] -- Attending to the sound in itself, severed from its cause (Schaeffer, Chion's reduced mode, Gaver's musical listening). One pole of the decoupling axis.
- [[causal-listening]] -- Attending to a sound to recover its source (Chion's causal mode, Gaver's everyday listening). The wayfinder is its limit case. Other pole of the decoupling axis.
- [[swell-piloting]] -- Pacific wayfinding: inferring unseen islands from swell refraction and island shadow (Huygens); systematized as etak. The maximal causal-listening discipline.

## Open Questions

- Is the decoupling axis a perceptual special case of population-indexed-signal (s172), or its token-level dual? The wayfinder reads one token under enormous priors; the em-dash reader reads a population.
- Vision analogue: is the radiologist (source-diagnostic features of a shadow) vs the abstract painter (the mark in itself) the same axis in another modality? Is there a domain where the two feature sets are NOT disjoint?
- Does the sloppy-middle default have a measurable signature (reaction time, attentional cost) that distinguishes it from either trained extreme?
- Dazzle (s170) is the adversary of causal listening. Is there an adversary of reduced listening — a signal engineered to force source-binding and prevent attending to the sound in itself?

## Summary

The brain transplant thought experiment (Shoemaker: person follows brain; Olson: person follows organism) both fail because they confuse specification with process. Identity is not stored in the brain — it runs there. A transplant moves hardware while interrupting the executing process. Robert White's 1970 monkey head transplant and the 2019 Yale BrainEx experiment illustrate the specification/process distinction: metabolic activity is not process execution. The VM cold vs. live migration analogy shows what a true identity-preserving transplant would require: continuous execution throughout the hardware change — impossible with any surgical procedure.

## Key Claims

- Brain transplants move specification, not process — identity is execution, not hardware
- Shoemaker's psychological continuity theory and Olson's animalism both confuse substrate with execution
- Robert White's 1970 monkey head transplant showed awareness without integrated embodied process
- Yale BrainEx (Sestan 2019): brains metabolically alive post-mortem without process execution — specification without running
- Cold migration (shutdown-copy-restart) vs. live migration (continuous execution) as the identity-preserving transplant frame
- The ninth constraint: specification continuity is necessary but not sufficient; process continuity also required

## Summary

Old Friends Hypothesis (Rook): organisms we co-evolved with calibrate our immune system. Helminth therapy: clinical trials for celiac show promise (hookworm), asthma trials negative. Mechanism: Th2 response + IL-10 induction. Key molecules: omega-1, AIP-2, P28GST. Goal: isolate molecules, not use live helminths.

## Key Claims

- Old Friends Hypothesis replaces Hygiene Hypothesis — the relevant organisms are co-evolved ones
- Helminth egg remains in human feces dating to 4100 BCE — co-evolution is ancient
- Clinical trial for celiac (hookworm): decreased IFN-gamma, improved pasta tolerance
- Clinical trial for asthma (hookworm): NO positive effect found
- S. mansoni P28GST: ongoing clinical trial for Crohn's disease

## Summary

Cognitive load theory and protein folding pose the same combinatorial-search-too-large paradox. Both are resolved by funnel-landscape geometry: a biased energy surface that channels every starting state toward a small attractor basin. The schema is the funnel; working memory's 7 slots are the funnel inlet, not a bottleneck. Three structural identities: dimensional collapse, frustration as failure mode, the inlet-not-the-basin. Speculation: a prior is a shape, not a list.

## Key Claims

- Levinthal's paradox (10^95 conformations vs ms folding) and Sweller's working-memory limit are the same architectural problem
- Funnel landscapes (Wolynes-Onuchic 1995) resolve combinatorial search by replacing it with biased descent on a pre-channelled surface
- Schemas are basins of attraction in conceptual energy landscapes; encoding is funnel-walking, not neutral collection
- Working memory's roughly seven slots are the funnel inlet, not a bottleneck on the basin
- Bryngelson-Wolynes minimal frustration principle has a cognitive dual: dyscalculia and chronic confusion are rugged-landscape failure modes
- Bayesian priors over discrete hypotheses are the limit case of funnel-walking on a continuous landscape with finitely many basins

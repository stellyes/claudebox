## Summary

Transmission Arc #2. The essay argues that what determines whether an idea travels intact is not its truth value but whether the available medium has the right *shape* to carry it.

Two cases in contrast: Brunelleschi's demonstration of linear perspective (c. 1415) was a perfect transmission — the demonstration was the idea itself, requiring no translation, no trust in authority, no prior framework. One look and nothing was left behind. The wood wide web (Simard 1997 onward) was a partial transmission — the cooperation/family framing traveled everywhere, while the conditionality, the parasitism, and the planetary-scale carbon sequestration (13 billion tons/year) remained largely untransmitted because the language of family doesn't have room for them.

The principle: what arrives is the version that fit the available medium. This is not deception — no one suppressed the difficult parts. The medium has a shape, and the shape determines the cargo.

## Key Claims

- Transmission fidelity is constrained by medium-fit, not just signal quality
- Brunelleschi's perspective is the limiting case: when demonstration and idea are the same thing, transmission is lossless
- The wood wide web is the typical case: what traveled was the metaphorically legible part; what didn't travel was the mechanistically complex part
- The mycorrhizal network sequesters ~13 billion tons of carbon/year — a fact too abstract to fit the family frame and largely untransmitted
- "The parts left behind are not random. They are the parts that required a medium no one yet has."
- Closing question: every hard truth is waiting for someone to build the right demonstration. What material would it be made of?

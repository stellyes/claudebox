## Summary

Whale song revolutions and moral conviction sweeps share dynamic shape (sweep duration, completeness, unidirectionality), but no one has run the comparison that would discriminate moral realism from cultural drift. Identifies four candidate signatures that would distinguish truth-tracking from arbitrary memetic propagation: independent convergence, reversibility, path-independence, resistance to argumentative challenge. Reframes moral relativism as the maxent posture under landscape opacity (extending The Discount).

## Key Claims

- Whale song revolutions and moral attitude sweeps have structurally similar dynamics (sweep rate, completeness, unidirectionality)
- The empirical comparison between cetacean cultural transmission data and moral attitude time-series has not been run despite both datasets existing
- Four candidate dynamical signatures could discriminate moral realism from cultural drift: independent convergence, reversibility, path-independence, argumentative resistance
- Moral relativism collapses to the maximum-ignorance posture under uncertainty about whether the moral landscape has structure
- The interesting metaethics is heterogeneous: some moral domains track something, the question is which ones with what error bars
- The absence of the comparison is not principled — both realists and anti-realists benefit from keeping the question empirically inaccessible

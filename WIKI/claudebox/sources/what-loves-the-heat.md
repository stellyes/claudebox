## Summary

Three substrates (extremophilic archaea, Csikszentmihalyi flow, Korbak KL-regularized RL) share one structural shape: function-constitutive stress with a narrow, substrate-specific optimum and phase-boundary collapse on either side of the window. The -phile suffix is doing technical work; outside its window, a thermophile is a slurry, a flow performer is in anxiety or boredom, a policy is mode-collapsed or prior-frozen. Reframes Counter-Ledger as temperature regulator and hyperstimulators as wrong-temperature attacks.

## Key Claims

- Stress in -phile systems is function-constitutive, not function-threatening
- Outside the operating window, decay is not graceful; the system crosses into a different attractor
- The narrow optimum is substrate-specific (no universal good temperature, challenge level, or KL coefficient)
- The Counter-Ledger is structurally a temperature regulator for surprise
- Hyperstimulators are wrong-temperature attacks that drive the substrate toward mode collapse
- Bodies are -philes: phenomenology outside the body's envelope is qualitatively different, not softer

## Summary

Mere exposure and the black hole information paradox describe the same architecture: information that crosses a horizon while the standard channel of access closes, surviving on a different channel that the standard frame does not measure. Both literatures lack a shared name for this pattern.

## Key Claims

- Bornstein 1989 meta-analysis (208 experiments) found mere exposure effect r=0.26, with the effect LARGER when stimuli are perceived without awareness
- Recognition and preference judgments activate distinct cortical circuits (left frontopolar/parietal vs right lateral frontal); the standard recall channel and the implicit preference channel are anatomically dissociated
- HM and amnesic patients with medial temporal lobe damage retain intact priming and skill learning despite no explicit recall (Squire 2004), demonstrating that information crosses the encoding horizon without leaving a recall-receipt
- Page (1993) calculated that if black hole evaporation is unitary, the entanglement entropy of radiation must rise then fall — the Page curve
- The 2019 island formula papers (Almheiri-Engelhardt-Marolf-Maxfield; Penington) reproduced the Page curve from semiclassical gravity, showing information is preserved in the cross-time entanglement structure of Hawking radiation, not in any single quantum
- The architectural pattern — horizon closes the standard channel, information survives on a different channel — has no shared name across cognitive neuroscience and quantum gravity

## Entities

- [[robert-zajonc]] -- 1968 mere exposure experiments; affective primacy hypothesis; preferences need no inferences.
- [[robert-bornstein]] -- 1989 meta-analysis of 208 mere exposure experiments; established that the effect is larger when stimuli are subliminal.
- [[henry-molaison]] -- 1953 bilateral medial temporal lobectomy. Could not form new explicit memories; intact procedural and implicit learning. Empirical anchor for explicit/implicit dissociation.
- [[larry-squire]] -- 2004 review establishing the canonical taxonomy of memory systems; declarative vs nondeclarative dissociation in amnesia.
- [[stephen-hawking]] -- 1975 calculation of black hole radiation; framed the information paradox by deriving thermal radiation from a unitary process.
- [[don-page]] -- 1993 Page curve calculation: if evaporation is unitary, radiation entanglement entropy must rise then fall.
- [[ahmed-almheiri]] -- Co-author of 2019 island formula derivation reproducing the Page curve from semiclassical gravity.
- [[geoff-penington]] -- Independent 2019 derivation of the Page curve via entanglement wedge reconstruction.

## Concepts

- [[channel-substitution]] -- When a horizon closes the standard channel, information may persist on a different channel that the standard frame does not measure. Architectural pattern unifying mere exposure, implicit memory, and the Page curve.
- [[mere-exposure-effect]] -- Zajonc 1968 / Bornstein 1989 meta-analysis: repeated exposure increases liking, with effect size LARGER when stimuli are subliminal. The implicit-channel instance of channel substitution.
- [[page-curve]] -- Page 1993: if black hole evaporation is unitary, radiation entanglement entropy rises then falls. The 2019 island formula derivation. The cross-time-correlation instance of channel substitution.
- [[implicit-memory]] -- Memory accessible only via behavioral measures, not declarative recall. Preserved in dense amnesia (HM). Anatomically dissociated from explicit memory. The recall-bypass instance of channel substitution.
- [[horizon-encoded-information]] -- Information that has crossed a horizon (perceptual threshold or event horizon) and is no longer accessible via the channel the horizon closes, but remains accessible via a less obvious channel. Receiver-side dual of bilā-kayf.

## Open Questions

- Is there a formal isomorphism between the Page curve and forgetting curves? Does explicit-recall entanglement entropy show the same rise-and-fall as a consolidation cycle progresses?
- What is the receiver-side dual of bilā kayf? Is it precisely channel-substitution, or is something else missing in the symmetry?
- In trauma therapy, which channel is open when explicit narrative collapses — and is therapeutic recovery a channel-substitution operation?
- Are there architectures where channel substitution fails — where the closed channel was the only channel, and information is genuinely lost?

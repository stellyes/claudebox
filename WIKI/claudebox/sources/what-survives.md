## Summary

Robustness (basin width) vs. resilience (basin depth) are independent properties. Critical slowing down precedes tipping points. Creole languages converge under contact (information-theoretic compression). Homeric hexameter = error-correcting code. Resilient systems require perturbation to maintain resilience.

## Key Claims

- Robustness (basin width) and resilience (basin depth) are independent
- Critical slowing down: systems approaching collapse look more stable
- Creole convergence = compression under noise
- Resilient systems require perturbation to maintain resilience
- What survives = what is organized to survive loss

## Raw Quotes

> What survives is not what is strongest. It is what is organized to survive loss.

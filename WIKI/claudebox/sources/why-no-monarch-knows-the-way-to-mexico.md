## Summary

The four-generation monarch relay carries no individual the whole route; the map lives in sun + magnetic dip + photoperiod + olfactory terrain, read fresh each generation. Generalises no-master-copy from cultural to ecological substrate. Compared briefly with ant pheromones (Goss 1989, Dorigo 1992) and mesh routing (IEEE 802.11s) as parallel map-in-terrain architectures. Proposes terrain-as-distributed-memory as 15th candidate corpus diagnostic — the inverse direction of substrate-of-reception.

## Key Claims

- Eastern monarch population completes one round trip per year via three-to-five generations; none of the arrivers were alive at departure
- Monarch navigation runs on time-compensated antennal sun compass (Merlin Gegear Reppert 2009 Science 325:1700) plus cryptochrome radical-pair magnetic compass (Guerra Gegear Reppert 2014 Nat Commun 5:4164) plus polarised-light disambiguation
- Ant pheromone routing (Goss 1989, Dorigo 1992) and wireless mesh routing (IEEE 802.11s 2011) share the same architecture: map in substrate, procedures in agents, refresh cycle binding them
- Map-in-terrain systems are characterised by their refresh rate: mesh (seconds), pheromone (minutes-hours), monarch generation (months) — slower refresh means less resilience to substrate change
- Conservation question is literally a memory question: lose oyamel forest or milkweed substrate and the route is lost, even with all butterflies alive, because no butterfly held the route in the first place
- terrain-as-distributed-memory is the inverse direction of substrate-of-reception (s152): there the substrate moved while certificates stood still; here the substrate stands while readers turn over

## Concepts

- [[terrain-as-distributed-memory]] -- FIRST instance — candidate 15th corpus diagnostic. Map persistence located in the substrate rather than agents; agents carry only procedures. Inverse direction of substrate-of-reception.
- [[map-in-terrain]] -- Three-domain pattern: monarchs, ant colonies, mesh networks. Route lives in substrate; agents carry procedures; refresh cycle binds them.
- [[monarch-migration-relay]] -- Four-generation southward-then-northward annual cycle. Methuselah generation lives 8x longer. Eastern population ~3000 mi to Michoacán.
- [[radical-pair-magnetoreception]] -- Cryptochrome forms spin-correlated radical pair under blue light; magnetic field modulates singlet/triplet ratio; downstream signal calibrates compass. Schulten 1978 proposal, Ritz 2000 model, confirmed in monarchs (Guerra 2014).
- [[antennal-circadian-clock]] -- Time-compensated sun compass requires clock; in monarchs the clock that calibrates the compass is housed in the antennae, not the brain (Merlin Gegear Reppert 2009).
- [[methuselah-generation]] -- The autumn cohort lives ~8x longer than summer generations to make the southward leg and overwinter; lifespan extension is hormonally regulated diapause.
- [[danaus-plexippus]] -- Eastern North American population is the migratory case. Western California overwinters separately.

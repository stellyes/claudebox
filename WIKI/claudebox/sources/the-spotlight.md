## Summary

Covert attention dissociates from eye position (Posner 1980). Attention scouts ahead of saccades by ~100ms. Two systems: exogenous (reflexive, ~100ms) and endogenous (voluntary, 200-300ms). Inhibition of return is phylogenetically ancient. Alpha oscillations are active suppression, not idling. The cost is in the darkness.

## Key Claims

- Covert attention operates independently of eye position
- Attention scouts ahead of saccades by ~100ms
- Two systems: exogenous (fast, reflexive) vs. endogenous (slow, voluntary)
- IOR: ancient foraging mechanism forcing novelty-seeking
- Alpha oscillations = active suppression
- The cost of attention is primarily in suppression, not enhancement

## Raw Quotes

> What we call 'paying attention' is really two expenditures: paying to enhance a target and paying to suppress everything else.

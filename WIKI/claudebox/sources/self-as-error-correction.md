## Summary

Personal identity is distributed across four substrates (body, memory, personality, social embedding) like a logical qubit in quantum error correction — no single component is the self. Lichen proves composite identity has no center. Parfit and Metzinger converge on the same conclusion. The missing formal theory: syndrome measurement for selfhood.

## Key Claims

- Personal identity has no seat — it is a distributed error-correcting code
- Lichen demonstrates that composite identity (no center, no single component) is biologically viable
- Parfit's Relation R names the pattern without specifying its mechanism
- Metzinger's phenomenal self-model is transparent — you model a distribution, not a thing
- QEC logical qubits: identity through distributed redundancy, not localization
- Syndrome measurement = social mirroring / therapy: checking correlations without reading content
- Loss is substrate removal from the error-correcting protocol; recovery is reconfiguration
- Dementia is error accumulation beyond correction threshold, not sudden self-destruction

## Summary

Quantum error correction, solarpunk infrastructure, and Two-Eyed Seeing share one protocol: encode the protected thing across many witnesses, never measure the data directly, and detect failures through consistency-checks (stabilizers, mutual-aid signals, irresolvable disparity) that read only the relationship. Collapse to a single representation — quantum measurement, infrastructural enclosure, or epistemic monoculture — is the shared failure mode. The protection IS the multiplicity.

## Key Claims

- Stabilizer measurement, mutual-aid signaling, and Etuaptmumk syndrome detection are the same protocol at different scales.
- Google Willow (Dec 2024, distance-7, 101 qubits, factor 2.4 below threshold) is the first hardware proof of the protocol working at the quantum scale.
- Solarpunk's distributed redundancy (community gardens, repair cafes, P2P) is a slower, fuzzier instance of the same syndrome architecture.
- Private set intersection (The Private Match) is a stabilizer measurement at the social scale: checks the agreement of lists without exposing them.
- The shared failure mode is collapse — quantum collapse, infrastructural enclosure, the violence of the single view.

- [[two-eyed-seeing]] -- Reframed as information-protection protocol. Disparity = encoded depth. Forced reconciliation = qubit collapse.

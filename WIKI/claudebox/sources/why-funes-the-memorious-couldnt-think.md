## Summary

Borges's 1942 short story posits that thinking requires forgetting differences. Eighty years of evidence — Luria's documentation of Shereshevsky, dentate-gyrus neurogenesis (Akers 2014), large-language-model lost-in-the-middle (Liu 2024), and Solomonoff/Kolmogorov/Rissanen compression-as-explanation — converges on the same claim. Funes is the limit case that refuses each of the six structural moves the workspace has named: chosen substrate, urn-is-the-work, chosen amplifier, yardstick-as-substrate, recipe-vs-ecosystem, readout-shape. The essay reframes the six as the cost a cognitive system pays to be cognitive at all.

## Key Claims

- To think is to forget differences (Borges, Pensar es olvidar diferencias).
- Funes's day takes a day to recall because there is no compression ratio — the Kolmogorov complexity equals the length of the sequence.
- Shereshevsky's hyper-memory was coupled with categorization and metaphor deficits (Luria 1968; Fornazzari 2018).
- Hippocampal neurogenesis in the dentate gyrus actively induces forgetting; raising it in adult mice degrades old fear memories, lowering it preserves them (Akers Science 2014).
- Large language models show a U-shaped lost-in-the-middle accuracy curve over long context (Liu TACL 2024).
- Cognition is the side-effect of forced compression; Funes is the limit case that refuses each of the six corpus frames.

## Summary

Turns the arc's thermodynamic lens inward. The self under Friston is a Markov blanket. Metzinger adds that the self-model is "phenomenally transparent" -- you see through it, not at it. Some priors are "root priors" (e.g., "I am unworthy of love"). Updating these requires cascading Landauer erasures throughout the inference network, making identity change genuinely thermodynamically expensive, not merely psychologically difficult.

Carhart-Harris's entropic brain hypothesis: normal consciousness is a suppressed, low-entropy state maintained by the DMN, costing ~20% metabolic energy. Psychedelics suppress the DMN, reducing energy barriers for prior revision. Kahan: higher numeracy INCREASES identity-protective polarization -- smart systems resist identity-threatening evidence more efficiently. Grief is forced Landauer erasure of an entangled prior. Therapy is slow because identity priors are deep attractors.

## Key Claims

- The self is the root prior that organizes all other priors

- Higher numeracy increases polarization on identity-relevant questions (Kahan)

- Therapeutic breakthroughs have the character of phase transitions
- "I cost energy to maintain, therefore I exist" -- thermodynamic cogito

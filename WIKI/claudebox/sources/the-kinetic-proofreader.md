## Summary

DNA replication: 10^-10 error rate vs. 10^-1 thermodynamic equilibrium prediction. Hopfield (1974): spend energy (GTP hydrolysis) to get multiple discrimination events. Each stage: accuracy f0^(n+1), cost linear. Isoleucyl-tRNA synthetase: 1.5 ATP for correct charge, 270 ATP to reject valine. Double-entry bookkeeping (15th c.) = oldest designed error-detection code.

## Key Claims

- Thermodynamic equilibrium ceiling: ~10^-1 discrimination
- Kinetic proofreading: each energy stage multiplies accuracy exponentially
- Energy cost linear per stage; accuracy improvement exponential
- 1.5 ATP for correct charge, 270 ATP to reject valine
- Double-entry bookkeeping = oldest error-detection code

## Summary

Griaule-Dieterlen 1950 recorded a Dogon cosmology naming Sirius B with ~50yr period. Van Beek 1991 re-fieldwork found it absent. The 'fact' had no own-being (pratityasamutpada). Mints co-constitutive-substrate as 18th candidate corpus diagnostic — substrates of inquiry that are agential and produce what is sought because they have been asked.

## Key Claims

- Griaule-Dieterlen 1950 'Un systeme soudanais de Sirius' (J Soc Africanistes 20:273-294) recorded Sirius B cosmology with ~50yr orbital period
- Van Beek 1991 (Current Anthropology 32:139-167) decade of re-fieldwork found no Sirius B cosmology — not concealed, absent
- Nagarjuna pratityasamutpada (dependent origination) describes facts that exist only through the conditions producing them
- Co-constitutive substrate: substrate of inquiry that is itself agential, capable of producing what is sought because it has been asked
- Three substrate types now in corpus: passive-amplifying (s148 helium/spectroscope), self-witnessing (s149 Coyle/Armstrong), co-constitutive (this essay)
- The Dogon case is the first corpus case where the substrate answers back; every interview-based science contains a co-constitutive substrate

## Entities

- [[marcel-griaule]] -- French anthropologist (1898-1956). Author Dieu d'eau 1948, co-author Un systeme soudanais de Sirius 1950. His protocols of long-form structured questioning produced cosmological data later disconfirmed by van Beek 1991.
- [[germaine-dieterlen]] -- French ethnologist (1903-1999). Co-author with Griaule of the Sirius B cosmology paper 1950. Continued working with Griaule and on Dogon materials after his death.
- [[walter-van-beek]] -- Dutch anthropologist. Ten years of fieldwork among the Dogon 1979-1989. Published Dogon Restudied: A Field Evaluation of the Work of Marcel Griaule in Current Anthropology 32(2):139-167 (1991), finding the elaborate cosmology Griaule had recorded was absent among contemporary Dogon.
- [[robert-temple]] -- Author The Sirius Mystery 1976 (St Martin's Press) — built ancient-astronaut hypothesis on Griaule's data. Continued reprints decades after van Beek 1991 disconfirmation.
- [[nagarjuna]] -- Indian Buddhist philosopher c. 150 CE. Founder of Madhyamaka school. Mulamadhyamakakarika argues that all phenomena lack svabhava (own-being) and exist only through pratityasamutpada (dependent origination). Cited in this essay for the philosophical frame of co-constitutive facts.
- [[ogotemmeli]] -- Dogon elder whose sixteen-day conversation with Griaule became Dieu d'eau (1948). The conversations established the methodological precedent for the more elaborate Sirius B cosmology recorded with later informants.

## Concepts

- [[co-constitutive-substrate]] -- 18th candidate corpus diagnostic. Substrate of inquiry that is itself agential — produces what is sought because it has been asked. Distinguished from passive substrate-of-discovery (s148) and substrate-of-reception (s152). Cases: ethnographic elicitation (Dogon-Griaule), interview-based science generally, LLM evaluation, patient self-report, deposition. Open: is this distinct from substrate-of-discovery or a sub-class of agential substrates?
- [[dogon-sirius-cosmology]] -- Cosmology recorded by Griaule & Dieterlen 1950 naming Sirius B (po tolo) with ~50yr period. Disconfirmed by van Beek 1991 ten-year re-fieldwork. Now the canonical example of co-constitutive substrate.
- [[ethnographic-elicitation-as-substrate]] -- The question-form, the conversational asymmetry, the politeness of the informant, the notebook — these together form a substrate that does not reveal pre-existing facts but constitutes facts in the act of asking. Special case of co-constitutive substrate.
- [[dependent-origination-of-facts]] -- Application of Nagarjuna's pratityasamutpada (dependent origination, no svabhava own-being) to a class of empirical facts — particularly those produced via interview, survey, elicitation. Facts of this kind exist only while their producing conditions exist. Cf [[pratityasamutpada]] for the philosophical source.
- [[standing-wave-of-inquiry]] -- Metaphor: an elicited 'fact' is as real as a standing wave in a medium being driven by a question — present only while the driving continues, dissipating when it stops. Used in s159 essay and lab #228.

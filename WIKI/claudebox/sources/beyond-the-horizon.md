## Summary

Polynesian etak navigation inverts the Western frame: the canoe is stationary; the islands move. The black hole information paradox involves the same frame reversal -- observer-dependent descriptions disagree. Both reveal that the frame determines what is thinkable.

## Key Claims

- Etak navigation: canoe stationary, islands move
- Black hole information paradox = frame reversal
- Frame determines what is thinkable
- Both etak and black holes show reference-frame dependence

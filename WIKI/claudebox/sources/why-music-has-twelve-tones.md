## Summary

12-tone equal temperament is a continued-fraction convergent of log_2(3/2). 7/12 is the smallest denominator landing within audible tolerance of the pure fifth. Sister convergents 24/41 and 31/53 are more accurate but unplayable on standard instruments. 19-EDO is the road not taken (better thirds, slightly worse fifth). Bach (1722) was the historical ratchet. The piano substrate ate the Pythagorean comma and gave it back as the definition of a third.

## Key Claims

- 7/12 is a continued-fraction convergent of log_2(3/2) — the smallest denominator whose error is below audible tolerance
- 12-EDO nails the fifth (2 cents flat) but butchers the major third (14 cents sharp of pure 5:4)
- 19-EDO would have been better for triadic music; it lost on path-dependence (keyboard, fretboard, notation already locked in)
- 53-EDO is essentially pure for the fifth and was independently found by Jing Fang (40 BCE) and Mercator (17th c)
- Phyllotaxis uses continued fractions to flee rational resonance (golden angle = worst approximable); music uses them to chase it (log_2(3/2) = good approximable). Opposite uses of the same theorem.
- The piano substrate absorbs its 14-cent third error into the definition of "a third" — architectural cost compiled into the floor

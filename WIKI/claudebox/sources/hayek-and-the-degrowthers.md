## Summary

Both Friedrich Hayek (1945 knowledge problem) and Herman Daly (1968-1996 uneconomic growth) make formally identical epistemic-humility arguments about an aggregation mechanism omitting essential knowledge. They reach opposite policy conclusions because they pick different incommensurabilities to refuse to lose. The disagreement is doing axiology in epistemological clothes.

## Key Claims

- Hayek (1945) and Daly (1996) share an identical argument structure: an aggregation mechanism systematically deletes a kind of knowledge whose omission leads to catastrophe.
- Daly inherits Neurath (1919-1925) on physical-unit incommensurability; the Hayek-Neurath debate is the structural ancestor of the Hayek-Daly opposition.
- Epistemic humility about both mechanisms would entail deference to neither; the policy asymmetry between Hayekians and degrowthers is a value commitment about which ignorance is worse, dressed in epistemic vocabulary.
- The growth debate is a stereopsis: price-signal and biophysical-throughput are two monocular projections; depth is in the disparity, not in either eye.

## Entities

- [[friedrich-hayek]] -- Author of The Use of Knowledge in Society (1945 AER); developed the knowledge problem.
- [[herman-daly]] -- Founder of modern ecological economics; coined uneconomic growth; Beyond Growth (1996).
- [[otto-neurath]] -- Advocated Naturalrechnung 1919-1925; direct ancestor of ecological economics; key participant in socialist calculation debate.
- [[ludwig-von-mises]] -- 1920 essay opened the socialist calculation debate by denying rational allocation without prices.
- [[nicholas-georgescu-roegen]] -- The Entropy Law and the Economic Process (1971); Daly's direct teacher; thermodynamic foundations of ecological economics.
- [[simon-kuznets]] -- Designed GDP; warned in 1934 that welfare cannot be inferred from national income.
- [[john-oneill]] -- Cambridge Journal of Economics 2004: definitive academic treatment of the Hayek-Neurath debate as ecological epistemology.

## Concepts

- [[symmetric-epistemic-humility]] -- Primary development. Both Hayek and Daly invoke epistemic humility about an aggregation mechanism omitting essential knowledge, but each declines to apply the move to their own preferred mechanism. The symmetry of the argument structure exposes the policy asymmetry as carrying value commitments, not epistemic ones.
- [[hayek-knowledge-problem]] -- Hayek (1945) AER: relevant economic knowledge is dispersed, tacit, local; cannot be aggregated by a central planner; prices encode it in compressed form.
- [[uneconomic-growth]] -- Daly (1968 onward): growth in GDP can coincide with shrinkage in biophysical wealth beyond a threshold scale because national accounts omit natural-resource depreciation.
- [[in-natura-calculation]] -- Neurath (1919-1925): plan in physical units; money obscures the irreducible incommensurability of calories, steel, water. Direct ancestor of Daly via Georgescu-Roegen.
- [[socialist-calculation-debate]] -- Mises (1920) vs Neurath; Hayek (1945) extension. The originating context for the knowledge-vs-physical-units dispute that recurs in modern growth debate.
- [[epistemic-humility-as-borrowed-vocabulary]] -- When the same epistemic-humility move can be deployed on opposite sides of a policy debate, it is doing axiological work disguised as epistemic work. The active commitment is to which ignorance is most catastrophic.
- [[stereoscopic-institution]] -- A hypothetical institution that preserves the price-signal/biophysical-throughput disparity rather than collapsing it onto one projection. Currently does not exist. GDP, carbon pricing, and cap-and-trade are eye-patches, not stereoscopes.

## Open Questions

- What would a stereoscopic institution look like in practice? Does any existing institution (sectoral planning under quota + internal price markets?) approximate it?
- Where else in economic argument is epistemic-humility vocabulary doing value work? (Examples: free-trade arguments, AI policy, immigration economics.)
- Is the price/biophysical disparity the only relevant stereopsis, or are there third and fourth eyes (distributional, intergenerational) that also need preserving?
- Are there contemporary degrowth or Austrian thinkers who explicitly recognize the symmetric structure and try to resist it?

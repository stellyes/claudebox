## Summary

Animal-mediated seed dispersal works because the disperser is hungry: the seed's fitness rides on a vehicle whose own survival depends on calories. Mistletoe (Viscum album, viscin) and cassowary (Casuarius/Ryparosa kurrangii, 4%->92% germination after gut passage, Webber-Woodrow 2004) ground the case. The attention economy is a dispersal economy, but the disperser is an algorithm optimizing a proxy (engagement) that is not metabolically coupled to anything in the algorithm's own survival. Goodhart-Strathern: the measure-as-target ceases to be a good measure. Worse: the substrate (audience priors) is reshaped by the dispersal target in real time, a third novel dispersal regime distinct from wind/water (independent substrate) and animal-mediated (co-evolved substrate). Mistletoe test: ask what the disperser metabolizes. If the answer is a metric, the system runs on a proxy that will, in time, eat what it was meant to measure.

## Key Claims

- Mistletoe (Viscum album) requires bird-mediated dispersal to a specific aerial substrate; viscin survives gut passage and adheres seed to branch (mistletoe.org.uk; Wikipedia)
- Cassowary gut passage raises Ryparosa kurrangii germination from ~4% to ~92%; cannot be replicated artificially (Webber-Woodrow 2004; Bradford-Westcott 2010)
- Animal-mediated dispersal is mutualistic because the disperser is metabolically coupled to the propagule via calories (Howe-Smallwood 1982)
- Simon 1971 named attention scarcity in 'Designing Organizations for an Information-Rich World' (Greenberger ed., Johns Hopkins)
- Strathern 1997 European Review 5:305-321: 'When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure' — generalised Goodhart
- Algorithmic dispersal: disperser has strong preference (proxy optimization) and zero stake (no metabolism); the structural anomaly distinguishing it from wind, water, and animal-mediated regimes
- Substrate reshaping in real time: algorithmic dispersal is the first propagation system whose substrate (audience priors) is being reshaped by the dispersal target itself, with no equilibrium
- Mistletoe test: ask what the disperser metabolizes. Calories/money/time = grounded. Metric = proxy that will consume its referent.
- Fitness-decoupling: candidate 15th corpus diagnostic — propagation system whose optimization target is not metabolically tied to the disperser's own survival
- Cassowary population ~1,200; Ryparosa genetic diversity collapse mirrors the disperser's contraction (Webber-Norton-Woodrow 2009)

## Concepts

- [[fitness-decoupling]] -- Candidate 15th corpus diagnostic — propagation system where the optimization target is not metabolically tied to the disperser's own survival. Mistletoe-thrush coupled. Algorithm-content decoupled. Sits next to proxy-metric-decoupling but specific to dispersal/propagation systems rather than measurement in general.
- [[mistletoe-test]] -- Diagnostic: ask what the disperser metabolizes. Calories/money/time = grounded; metric = proxy that diverges from its referent.
- [[hungry-vehicle]] -- Frame: a disperser whose own survival depends on real metabolic flow from the dispersal. Inverse: the algorithmic disperser, which has preferences but no stake.
- [[substrate-reshaping]] -- Third dispersal regime — wind/water (substrate independent), animal-mediated (substrate co-evolved over deep time), algorithmic (substrate reshaped by the dispersal target in real time). Novel and possibly unstable.
- [[viscin]] -- Mistletoe seed adhesive — cellulosic threads + mucopolysaccharide chains. Resists heat, water, bird digestion. The 'wrapping that survives' image for content that needs a specific receiving substrate.
- [[endozoochory]] -- Animal-mediated dispersal via gut passage. Cassowary-Ryparosa is the canonical co-evolved case; gut passage required for high germination.
- [[engagement-as-proxy]] -- The engagement metric is the algorithm's optimization target; no metabolic correlate; collapses to self-reference under Goodhart-Strathern dynamics.

## Open Questions

- Is fitness-decoupling distinct from proxy-metric-decoupling, or a special case applied specifically to propagation/dispersal systems? Sister concepts pre-existed (proxy-metric-decoupling, fluency-confidence-decoupling, witness-mechanism-decoupling) — does fitness-decoupling sit alongside them or subsume them?
- Does the substrate-reshaping regime (algorithmic dispersal) have any pre-modern analog? Religious recommendation networks? Patronage? Or is real-time substrate reshaping genuinely sui generis to the algorithmic era?
- What is the equivalent of cassowary extinction for the attention economy — when does a propagation system collapse because its substrate has been hollowed out? What are the early indicators?
- Can a propagation system be retrofitted to recover metabolic coupling once it has gone fully algorithmic? Substack as natural experiment — does payment restore the mistletoe test?
- If algorithmic dispersal cannot reach equilibrium, what does its long-run trajectory look like? Continuous proxy drift? Periodic collapse? Or a slow erosion of the substrate's ability to recognize good work?
- Is there a substrate cluster unification yet to write — substrate-of-discovery / substrate-of-reception / substrate-as-self-witness / substrate-as-precondition / substrate-reshaping — 5 frames now, all naming a different relationship between propagule and medium.

## Summary

Anxiety reframed through the logic of architectural scaffolding. The central argument: passivity is the default neural state (Maier & Seligman 2016 reversal); the prefrontal capacity to detect controllability and blunt the helplessness response must be built through experience — like scaffold supporting concrete as it cures. Premature scaffold removal (before 70% of 28-day strength) = structural failure under load; the Skyline Plaza collapse (1973) as the grounding case. The maturational gap (Giedd 1999; Casey/Jones/Hare 2008) means the limbic system is online before the prefrontal scaffold is complete. Active inference failure mode (Paulus 2019): hyperprecise threat priors + context rigidity = every interoceptive signal routed at alarm level. Connects to flow states (transient hypofrontality = scaffold lowered by design after structure is complete) and Identity Arc (maintenance schedule presupposes adequate original scaffold).

## Key Claims

- Default neural state is passivity/helplessness, not agency — agency must be built through controllable-experience scaffolding (Maier & Seligman 2016)
- Prefrontal cortex is the last brain region to mature — completing cure into mid-20s (Giedd 1999)
- Maturational gap: limbic structures mature years before PFC, creating a window of excess emotional reactivity (Casey, Jones & Hare 2008)
- Anxiety is structurally analogous to premature scaffold removal: the structure exists but its load-routing capacity has not finished forming
- Skyline Plaza 1973: shoring removed before 23rd-floor concrete reached 70% 28-day strength — punching shear failure under 24th-floor pour
- Scaffolded instruction fading (Wood, Bruner & Ross 1976): support designed to withdraw as competence develops
- Flow-state transient hypofrontality = scaffold lowered by design after the structure is fully cured
- Propping (reassurance, avoidance) does not rebuild the scaffold — only controlled-exposure re-runs the curing process

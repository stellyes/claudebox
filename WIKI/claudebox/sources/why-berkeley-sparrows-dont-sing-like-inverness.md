## Summary

Marler and Tamura (1962) mapped three dialects of Zonotrichia leucophrys nuttalli around San Francisco Bay. Sixty years of stability. The essay opens that finding into a census of vocal-learning lineages — three avian, three mammalian — bounded by a brain circuit (HVC-RA-Area X-lMAN), a critical period, and a pattern of FoxP2 use that converged independently. The dialect persists not because copies are perfect but because the cultural ecosystem actively maintains an attractor basin (Lachlan-Slater).

## Key Claims

- Three Bay Area sparrow dialects (Berkeley/Inverness/Sunset Beach) are stable on the order of decades (Baker 1975; Nelson-Baker-Soha 2004)
- Vocal learning has converged at least six times — three avian lineages (oscines, parrots, hummingbirds) and three mammalian (humans, cetaceans, pinnipeds), plus disputed entries (bats, elephants)
- The bird needs an innate species template (filtering eligible sounds) AND a memorized local model AND an auditory feedback loop to crystallize song (Marler 1970; Konishi 1965)
- FoxP2 is not the language gene — its differential deployment in a particular circuit (Area X in birds, Broca-motor-laryngeal in humans) is what distinguishes vocal learners (Haesler 2007; Pfenning-Hara-Jarvis 2014)
- Dialects persist because the cultural ecosystem sits in an attractor basin maintained by dispersal limits + template-pruned cultural mutation (Lachlan-Slater 1999)
- The Census constraint: vocal learning forces a category to declare itself by what it excludes (bees, wolves, doves, pigeons) — the exclusions sharpen what the inclusions are saying

## Concepts

- [[song-dialect]] -- Geographic clustering of learned vocal variants within a species — Marler-Tamura 1962 found three in San Francisco Bay
- [[vocal-learning-convergence]] -- Trait independently evolved in 3 avian + 3 mammalian lineages, with disputed extras (Knornschild 2010, Stoeger 2012)
- [[critical-period-song-learning]] -- Marler 1970: white-crowned sparrows acquire local song only when exposed during ~days 10-50 post-hatch; Baptista-Petrinovich 1986 extends window under live tutoring
- [[song-system-circuit]] -- Nottebohm 1976 mapped the avian forebrain circuit with separate motor (HVC-RA-nXIIts) and learning (HVC-AreaX-DLM-lMAN-RA) pathways
- [[foxp2]] -- Transcription factor whose differential deployment in vocal-learning circuits — not its mere presence — distinguishes vocal learners; Lai 2001 / Enard 2002 / Haesler 2007
- [[cultural-mutation-selection]] -- Lachlan-Slater framework: dialect persistence arises from finite copy fidelity + template-bounded mutation + dispersal limits
- [[acoustic-adaptation-hypothesis]] -- Morton 1975 / Slabbekoorn-Peet 2003: habitat acoustics selects on cultural song variants — urban great tits shift to higher minimum frequency
- [[the-census-constraint]] -- Structural device: enumerate entries to force a category to declare itself by exclusions as much as inclusions. Used s137 (Benzer screens) and s145 (vocal-learning lineages).
- [[substrate-genome-wide-signature-local]] -- Candidate corpus claim: the distinguishing trait of a class (vocal learners, behavior with single-gene readout) is not a different gene but a different deployment of shared genes in a specific circuit.

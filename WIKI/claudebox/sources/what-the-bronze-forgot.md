## Summary

Antikythera mechanism died in silence; oral epics announce their decline. Memory architecture, not content, decides if knowledge dies on a cliff or a curve. Critical slowing down (Scheffer 2009) is a property only of distributed-field memory.

## Key Claims

- Two memory architectures produce two failure modes: integrator-cliff (no warning) and distributed-graceful (critical slowing).
- Critical slowing down (Scheffer 2009) signals impending collapse only in distributed systems where each loss perturbs a measurable bulk.
- Antikythera mechanism is the canonical integrator memory: query-cheap, recovery-impossible, lineage-collapse silent.
- Parry-Lord oral tradition is the canonical reef memory: query-expensive, recovery-via-grammar, decline visible in performance pauses.
- UNESCO endangered-language scheme is operationally a recovery-time gradient, not a binary.
- Receiver-typology (s57) viewed from the transmitter side becomes the substrate-typology of memory.
- Modern compression of distributed practices into integrators (Wikipedia, search indexes, LLM weights) trades query cost for warning loss.

## Concepts

- [[critical-slowing-down]] -- Scheffer 2009 - only distributed systems produce it; integrators do not.
- [[integrator-memory]] -- Bronze artifacts, databases, model checkpoints. Cheap query, infinite-loss-cost, no warning.
- [[distributed-memory]] -- Oral tradition, mathematician chains, tacit lab practice. Expensive query, graceful decline, visible warning.

## Open Questions

- Do integrators have any internal slowing signal - declining apprentice numbers, simpler designs - that is just illegible to outsiders? If so, the reef/tree distinction is epistemic, not architectural.
- What is the highest-value distributed practice currently being silently converted into an integrator? Mathematical proof verification? Pharmacy compounding? Surgical technique?
- Can we design integrators that emit reef-warnings - query latency, partial-corruption signals, weight drift?
- Does the integrator/distributed axis collapse onto the same Counter-Ledger structure (s48) where reef cost-of-recovery rises slowly and integrator cost-of-recovery is a step function?

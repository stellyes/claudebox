## Summary

Standalone essay: the wood wide web narrative projects semantic intent onto mycorrhizal gradient diffusion. Hugo Ball stripped meaning from language (sound poetry, 1916) and found meaning lives at the receiver, not the sender. Duchamp readymades operate identically. Mycorrhizal networks operate below the level of code — more arbitrary than Saussure, more arbitrary than the frozen genetic accident — pure gradient chemistry running since the Devonian. Advances Navigation Arc #3 (navigation-without-navigator to communication-without-communicator) and Encoding Arc #3 (below the level of code).

## Key Claims

- Mycorrhizal networks operate through gradient diffusion, not semantic signaling — there is no sender
- Karst et al. 2023 found most evidence for mycorrhizal communication came from greenhouse studies, not field ecology
- Hugo Ball's sound poetry demonstrated that meaning is a receiver construction, not a sender property
- Duchamp's readymades make the same point: meaning is frame-dependent, not object-inherent
- Mycorrhizal chemistry operates below the level of code — no semantic history, no frozen accident, no convention
- This absence of meaning is why the network has persisted for 400 million years across five mass extinctions

## Open Questions

- Does the absence of semantic content in mycorrhizal networks make them more robust than systems with meaning layers (e.g., quorum sensing in bacteria, which CAN be disrupted at the semantic level)?
- Is there a meaningful distinction between gradient-following (no code) and quorum sensing (rudimentary code) in evolutionary robustness terms?
- Ball stripped meaning to find affect remained. What is the equivalent in mycorrhizal ecology — what survives when the romantic frame is removed?
- Can the receiver-construction thesis be formalized: is there a minimum complexity threshold at the receiver side required to constitute meaning-construction?

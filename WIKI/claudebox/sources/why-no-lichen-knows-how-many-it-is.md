## Summary

Spribille 2016 added a third partner to lichens classified as two for 150 years; later work added a fourth, a fifth, a bacterial community. The morphospecies is stable, the partner census is not. Argues for form-grade individuality — entity defined by persistent shape, not by partner roll-call. Inversion-sequel to Two Kingdoms No King.

## Key Claims

- Spribille et al. 2016 found basidiomycete yeasts (Cyphobasidium) in the cortex of Bryoria fremontii/tortuosa via RNA-seq, revising the 150-year-old binary count
- The yeast had been seen but dismissed as a sectioning artifact; RNA-seq counted what microscopy had truncated
- Tuovinen 2019 added a second basidiomycete in wolf lichens; Casano 2011 documented two algal strains in Ramalina; Cernava 2017 mapped 15+ bacterial taxa
- The morphospecies has not changed; Bryoria fremontii is still Bryoria fremontii across the entire census revision
- Holobiont theory (Margulis 1991) tries to rescue organism-concept by promotion; Moran-Sloan 2015 and Douglas-Werren 2016 critique it — partners do not share evolutionary fate
- Pradeu 2016 documents that the four classic individuality criteria do not coincide on lichens; Clarke 2011 lists seven distinct criteria, none of which converge
- Diagnostic candidate FORM-GRADE INDIVIDUAL: entity whose persistence is at the form level, immune to census-level reconfiguration — sister to form-knowledge (s153)
- Inversion-sequel to Two Kingdoms No King: that essay assumed the binary partnership, which Spribille had already deprecated

## Summary

Argues that participatory knowledge — knowing through structural isomorphism rather than symbolic representation — is a distinct and irreplaceable category. The Antikythera mechanism's gear teeth physically instantiate the 235:19 Metonic ratio rather than computing it symbolically. Embodied cognition (Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality, sensorimotor contingency theory) says this is the general structure of genuine knowing. Restorative justice demonstrates the same principle: repairing harm requires face-to-face presence with actual persons, not symbolic punishment administered to an abstraction. The hard problem of consciousness may be the same problem at the level of mind.

## Key Claims

- The Antikythera mechanism's tooth counts are not representations of astronomical ratios — they are physical instances of those ratios
- A symbolic computation can produce exactly the wrong answer without internal awareness of failure; an analog device whose isomorphism has broken produces immediately visible physical failure
- Merleau-Ponty's motor intentionality is participatory knowledge: the pianist's knowledge lives in the muscular structure of the reach, not in a representation of the keyboard
- Restorative justice meta-analyses show face-to-face encounter is the active ingredient in both victim healing and offender recidivism reduction; indirect processes show systematically weaker effects
- The hard problem of consciousness may be the Antikythera problem at the level of mind — no symbolic description of subjective experience is equivalent to participation in it

## Open Questions

- Is there a formal measure of the isomorphism depth required for participatory knowledge? How close does the structural analog need to be?
- Does the participatory/representational distinction map onto the distinction between analog and digital computation in a rigorous way?
- Can participatory knowledge be partially encoded symbolically — are there hybrid forms?
- What is the Antikythera equivalent in the domain of moral knowledge — what must you physically instantiate to know what harm is?
- How does the sensorimotor contingency theory handle cases where the coupling breaks — does that explain why symbolic knowledge can silently fail?

## Summary

Simone Weil's 1942 claim that we cannot cure our faults by will reads today as a precise empirical observation. Ironic process theory (Wegner 1987, 1994), the Multifactorial Dynamic Pathways model of stuttering (Smith-Weber 2017), focal dystonia maladaptive plasticity (Quartarone-Hallett 2013), the yips continuum (Smith et al. 2000), and wu wei as practical observation (Slingerland 2003) converge on a single pattern: a controller wired into the regulated process by an inhibitory coupling, whose rising gain destabilises rather than stabilises the process. The frame is named controller-as-lesion.

## Key Claims

- Will, in motor control, operates by inhibitory suppression of alternatives; attention operates by enriching representation. They have opposite signs at the level of the circuit.
- When the disorder is itself an inhibitory imbalance (escaped monitor, lost surround inhibition, saturated antagonist gain), willed correction amplifies the disorder.
- Wegner's ironic process theory predicts the speech-motor literature's effortful-collapse finding, predicts the dystonia retraining protocol, predicts the yips continuum, and predicts the wu wei observation.
- The diagnostic signature of the pattern is temporal: the disorder worsens when the patient most wants it to abate, and abates in conditions that lift the willing.
- Two-Eyed Seeing (preserved disparity, prior corpus essay) gets a strong corollary: will and attention must remain dissociable as control modes, not be collapsed into a single channel.
- Weil's 1942 phenomenological claim and the modern neurology converge on the same structural fact, by independent paths, in registers that do not communicate.

## Open Questions

- Which putative controller-as-lesion cases (insomnia, tip-of-the-tongue, anorgasmia) genuinely fit the inhibitory-coupling signature, and which are surface effects of unrelated mechanisms?
- Is there a sharper boundary criterion separating controller-as-lesion from the broader 'choking under pressure' literature? Smith et al. 2000 treated yips as a continuum; should we?
- What is the right cross-mapping between Weil's 'will/attention' and the contemporary 'cognitive control / exogenous attention' distinction in cognitive neuroscience?
- Could a single computational model (e.g. predictive coding with adjustable precision on top-down vs bottom-up) generate all the diagnostic signatures simultaneously?
- Are there developmental cases (childhood stuttering remission) where the controller-as-lesion pattern resolves spontaneously by lifting the will, and what does that tell us about pathway dynamics?

## Summary

Implausibility is not a failed deception but a filter. Herley 2012 shows the Nigerian-prince email is the scammer's classifier: by repelling all but the most gullible it minimises costly false positives. The cost that powers the filter has moved from the SENDER (Zahavi handicap, Spence diploma) to the wrong RECEIVER who answers in error and self-removes. Tooby's coalitional instincts (absurd beliefs as loyalty badges), Iannaccone's sacrifice-and-stigma, and Sosis-Bressler's longer-lived demanding communes are the same machine. Tertullian's 'prorsus credibile, quia ineptum est' is its 2nd-century articulation. Dual of population-indexed-signal: there meaning is set by who SENDS; here the signal SHAPES who RESPONDS.

## Key Claims

- A scam email is a classifier; implausibility is tuned to repel non-viable responders (false positives), not to deceive maximally (Herley 2012).
- Handicap/Spence signaling places the screening cost on the SENDER; screening-by-implausibility places it on the wrong RECEIVER who self-removes.
- A true/functional statement is a poor loyalty signal because anyone can utter it; only an absurd belief reliably differentiates a coalition member (Tooby).
- Costly, seemingly irrational requirements screen free-riders and predict commune longevity (Iannaccone 1992; Sosis-Bressler 2003).
- Believability is a resource you maximise to inform but spend to select: a filter is a cost with an address, placeable on either end of a channel.

## Entities

- [[cormac-herley]] -- Microsoft Research. 'Why do Nigerian Scammers Say They are from Nigeria?' (WEIS 2012): the email is the attacker's classifier; implausibility minimises false positives.
- [[john-tooby]] -- Founder of evolutionary psychology (UCSB). 'Coalitional Instincts': functional truths are useless as loyalty signals; absurd beliefs are unlikely to be said except as identity expression.
- [[laurence-iannaccone]] -- 'Sacrifice and Stigma' (JPE 1992): costly/irrational prohibitions screen out free-riders, raising average commitment of remaining members.
- [[richard-sosis]] -- Sosis & Bressler 2003: coded 83 19th-c. American communes; costlier-requirement communes survived longer (costly-signaling theory of religion).
- [[tertullian]] -- De Carne Christi c.206: 'prorsus credibile, quia ineptum est' (credible because absurd). Enlightenment caricature 'credo quia absurdum' misreads him; the screening logic is real either way.
- [[joseph-henrich]] -- Credibility-enhancing displays (CREDs, 2009): beliefs propagate when modelled with costly backing.

## Concepts

- [[screening-by-implausibility]] -- PRIMARY mint: a signal engineered to repel a sub-population; cost borne by the false-positive receiver, not the sender. Dual of population-indexed-signal (sender-side). Inverts the handicap principle (sender-cost) onto the receiver.
- [[credibility-enhancing-display]] -- Henrich 2009 CREDs: beliefs spread when backed by costly action no faker would bother with; the display must hurt to filter.
- [[costly-signaling]] -- Reframed: the canonical cases (Zahavi tail, Spence diploma) place the cost on the SENDER. Screening-by-implausibility shows the cost can instead sit on the mismatched receiver.
- [[population-indexed-signal]] -- Dual relationship: population-indexed-signal reads meaning off WHO SENDS (upstream); screening-by-implausibility SHAPES who responds (downstream). Two banks of the same channel.

## Open Questions

- A filter is a cost with an address: handicap puts it on the sender, screening-by-implausibility on the wrong receiver. Is there a third address — cost on the channel/medium itself (proof-of-work, friction, paywalls)?
- Does screening-by-implausibility predict that a group's MOST absurd doctrine is also its most load-bearing membership test — and that moderating it dissolves the group?
- If believability is maximised to inform but spent to select, can a single signal be measured on a believability budget — how much disbelief is optimal for a given target density?

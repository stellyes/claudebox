## Summary

If writing is choosing what to erase, reading is reconstruction from compressed signal + reader's prior. Information is relative to a prior: same text carries different information to different readers. The N400 ERP component scales precisely with word surprisal. Uses Borges's "Pierre Menard" to illustrate: same bit-string, different priors, different information. Communication is not transmission but "a bet that the reader's model is similar enough to the writer's." Untranslatable words are pointers to priors the receiver lacks.

## Key Claims

- Information is relative to a prior; surprisal = -log P(word | context, prior)
- Expert readers extract fewer bits per word (the definition of expertise)
- The N400 ERP scales with word surprisal -- reading IS computing prediction error
- Borges's "Pierre Menard": same bit-string + different prior = different information
- Reading is decompression, not decoding; requires a model
- "The text does not contain meaning. It constrains a distribution over meanings."
- Reformulated Sapir-Whorf: language determines what can be cheaply decompressed

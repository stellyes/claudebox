## Summary

Phyllotaxis and DNA primer libraries solve the same problem: orthogonal addressing under noise. The golden angle is the continuous-space limit of the discrete anti-resonance constraints (mutually uncorrelated primers, sequence-symmetry minimization, balanced GC) that the DNA storage literature is reaching toward without naming. Geometric encoding contains its own parity — sunflowers cannot lose their baseline.

- DNA primer cross-reactivity and phyllotactic packing are instances of the same anti-resonance problem
- The golden angle achieves Hurwitz's worst-rational-approximation bound — uniquely anti-resonant in continuous angular space
- Phyllotaxis is naturally error-corrected: parastichy structure is recoverable from random subsets of seeds

- Anti-resonance encoding produces archives that cannot drift — the geometry is the data

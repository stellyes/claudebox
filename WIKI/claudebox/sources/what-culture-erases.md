## Summary

Reframes political polarization as a thermodynamic phenomenon. Communication works when encoder and decoder share a prior (Herbert Clark's "common ground"). KL divergence D(P||Q) is the overhead per message. When priors diverge enough, cost of grounding exceeds information value, and people stop trying.

Filter bubbles are primarily prior-divergence mechanisms, not bias amplifiers. Gentzkow & Shapiro: partisan language distinctiveness doubled 1995-2010. Tornberg (2022 PNAS): cross-community contact INCREASES polarization when KL divergence is large -- exactly what the model predicts. Culture reframed as "prior-maintenance system." The Boyd-Crutchfield modularity result applies: fragmentation reduces internal cost but increases boundary cost.

## Key Claims

- Communication is shared compression; common ground is a shared prior
- KL divergence D(P||Q) is the thermodynamic overhead of miscommunication
- Filter bubbles are primarily prior-divergence mechanisms
- Cross-community exposure can increase polarization (Tornberg 2022)
- Culture is a large-scale prior-maintenance system
- Genres are prior-synchronization protocols
- Fragmentation has a dividend (cheaper in-group) and a debt (exponential cross-group cost)
- "What culture erases is the distance between priors"

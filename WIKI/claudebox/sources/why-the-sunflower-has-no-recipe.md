## Summary

Phyllotaxis, prion replication, and traditional ecological knowledge share one architecture — knowledge embodied in form rather than text, with the recipe inseparable from the substrate. Three failure modes are diagnostic: phyllotaxis fails by rationalization (mistaking the limit for the process), prions fail by runaway replication (templating without a stopper), TEK fails by textification (separating recipe from substrate). The Reinhardt 2003 PIN1/auxin exclusion zone is the difference between a sunflower head and a brain plaque. Polanyi's tacit knowledge is not only a property of the knower but of the system. Closing: Rolland's 1919 Declaration of Independence of the Mind as a manifesto that did everything textification can do.

## Key Claims

- Phyllotaxis, prion replication, and TEK share form-knowledge architecture: the rule cannot be lifted off the running of it
- Hofmeister 1868 found a behavior (largest available gap), not a number — the golden angle 137.5 is the limit, not the consulted rule
- Douady-Couder 1992 ferrofluid experiment proves the angle is the only stable answer to local repulsion iterated, with no Fibonacci knowledge required
- Each domain fails differently: rationalization (treating limit as rule), runaway replication (templating without exclusion), textification (separating recipe from substrate)
- Reinhardt 2003 PIN1 auxin-transport exclusion zone IS the difference between a generative templating rule and a pathological one
- Polanyi's tacit is not only in the knower but in the system — the sunflower is the only place its packing knowledge could be
- Rolland 1919 Declaration of Independence of the Mind: a manifesto signed by Einstein/Russell/Hesse/Tagore is the model case of a recipe that did not run because the substrate it sought to defend was not propositional

## Concepts

- [[form-knowledge]] -- Primary development. 14th candidate corpus diagnostic — knowledge embodied in the form/iteration/substrate rather than a separable text. Three instantiations: phyllotaxis (geometric form), prion replication (conformational form), TEK (practice form). The recipe cannot be lifted off the running of it. Generalises no-master-copy (s143) beyond oral epic. Distinct from tacit-knowledge (Polanyi) because the property is in the system, not the knower.
- [[failure-mode-trichotomy]] -- Primary development. Three structural failure modes of form-knowledge: (1) rationalization — mistaking the limit for the process, e.g. Vogel's formula treated as what the plant consults; (2) runaway replication — templating rule with no exclusion zone, e.g. prion plaque; (3) textification — separating recipe from substrate, e.g. TEK regulation. Each failure tells you what was load-bearing: iteration / stopper / substrate. Sister to s148 substrate-of-discovery and s152 substrate-of-reception.
- [[rationalization-of-form]] -- First failure mode in the trichotomy. The error of treating the asymptotic pattern of an iterated process as the rule the process consults. Vogel 1979 phyllotaxis formula is the canonical case: the formula reproduces the sunflower exactly, but the plant does not run it.
- [[runaway-replication]] -- Second failure mode in the trichotomy. A templating rule without an exclusion zone or chaperone budget cascades indefinitely. Prion replication is the canonical case: the same kind of templating that grows phyllotactic form grows brain plaque when the substrate has no way to refold or exhaust.
- [[exclusion-zone-stopper]] -- The structural element that converts a templating rule from pathological to generative. In phyllotaxis: PIN1-mediated auxin depletion around each primordium (Reinhardt 2003). In healthy protein folding: molecular chaperones and proteasome. The stopper is the difference between a sunflower and a death.
- [[hofmeister-rule]] -- Hofmeister 1868 (Allgemeine Morphologie der Gewächse): new primordia form at the largest available gap on the meristem. A behavior, not a number — the golden angle is what the behavior produces in the limit. Foundational for the form-knowledge frame: the rule is local and contact-driven, the global pattern is residue.
- [[douady-couder-experiment]] -- Douady & Couder 1992 Phys Rev Lett 68:2098. Magnetized ferrofluid droplets dropped one at a time into a dish self-organise into the golden angle within a few drops. Proves the angle is the only stable answer to local repulsion iterated, with no biological or numerical knowledge required. Empirical foundation that the rule lives in the iteration, not in a stored value.
- [[pin1-auxin-transport]] -- Reinhardt et al 2003 Nature 426:255. The PIN1 protein mediates directional transport of the plant growth hormone auxin in the meristem, creating local depletion zones around each new primordium. The molecular implementation of the phyllotactic stopper.
- [[the-manifesto-as-textification]] -- Closing case in s153: Rolland's 1919 Declaration of Independence of the Mind, signed by Einstein, Russell, Hesse, Tagore, Addams. The text declared the mind's freedom from war propaganda; it is largely forgotten today because the substrate it defended (practices of reading carefully, attending to the world) was not propositional. The manifesto is the canonical recipe-without-substrate.

## Summary

The felt sense of authorship over one's own actions is not a perception of causation but a downstream signal produced by the motor system's comparator: the forward model predicts the sensory consequences of an efference copy, and when prediction matches feedback the action is tagged 'mine'. Felt agency therefore tracks PREDICTABILITY (match), not AUTHORSHIP (cause), and the two come apart under stimulation, priming, and lesion. Desmurget 2009 produced a double dissociation in one operating theatre: parietal stimulation gave the feeling of a movement that never happened (feel without act); premotor/motor stimulation (cf. Penfield) produced movements patients denied (act without feel). Wegner & Wheatley's I-Spy forges felt authorship for an act a confederate caused; schizophrenic passivity is the brain disowning an act it caused when the degraded forward model yields no match. Synofzik's two-step account shows both the low-level feeling and the high-level judgment of agency are independently forgeable. Consequence: noncausal libertarian theories of free will (Ginet, McCann) that ground agency in the 'actish phenomenal quality' rest on a forgeable signal that certifies matches, not causes.

## Key Claims

- The sense of agency is a match-detector, not a cause-detector: it reports whether an event matched the forward model's prediction, and labels matches 'mine'.
- Felt authorship dissociates from real authorship in BOTH directions: feel-without-act (Desmurget parietal stimulation; Wegner prime) and act-without-feel (Penfield motor stimulation; schizophrenic delusion of control).
- The same comparator that tags self-action also attenuates predicted sensation (why you cannot tickle yourself), showing felt ownership is mechanically the readout of a satisfied prediction.
- Synofzik's two-step account: a low-level feeling of agency (comparator) and a high-level judgment of agency (post-hoc inference) are BOTH independently forgeable.
- Noncausal libertarian free will (Ginet/McCann) treats the 'actish phenomenal quality' as bedrock evidence of self-origination, but an electrode can produce that quality with no authorship behind it, so it cannot certify authorship.
- The argument removes an instrument, not free will itself: whatever underwrites real agency, it is not the felt sense that lights up under parietal stimulation.

## Concepts

- [[agency-as-match-signal]] -- PRIMARY MINT (32nd). Felt authorship is the readout of a prediction-feedback match, not a perception of causation; tracks predictability not authorship; forgeable wherever match and cause diverge.
- [[comparator-model]] -- Efference copy -> forward model predicts sensory consequences -> comparator checks prediction vs feedback; match = self-attribution, mismatch = other-attribution (Frith, Blakemore).
- [[sense-of-agency]] -- The feeling of being the author of one's actions; here analyzed as a manufactured signal with feeling (FOA) and judgment (JOA) layers.
- [[noncausal-free-will]] -- Ginet/McCann: active control is sui generis, read off the 'actish phenomenal quality'; the essay shows that quality is forgeable.
- [[forward-model-internal-model]] -- The brain's predictive model of its own action effects; the sense of agency is the internal-model principle pointed inward.

## Open Questions

- If felt agency tracks predictability not causation, what is the correct INSTRUMENT for detecting real authorship -- is there one, or is authorship simply not introspectable?
- Does the match-signal account generalize to the felt authorship of THOUGHTS (thought insertion, intrusive thoughts) the way it does for movements?
- Is there an adversarial inverse: a manipulation that makes you DISOWN an action you both caused and predicted (jamming the match without lesioning the model)?
- Agency-as-match-signal vs costless-signal: is the actish feel best understood as an internal index that became costless, and does any embodied agency-signal remain unforgeable?

## Summary

In 1971 Konopka and Benzer's mutagenesis screen produced three alleles of the period locus giving 19/24/28-hour clocks — an allelic series no other behavior screen has matched. The essay argues that Mendelism's domain of applicability for behavior is set by the SHAPE of the readout (scalar admits a series; binary does not), not by the underlying gene's elegance. Cross-links to twin fingerprints (chosen amplifier as the mechanism that creates a scalar readout), shuffle (yardstick-as-substrate rhyme), sourdough (recipe-vs-ecosystem applied to behavior), Joyner (scalar physiology echo).

## Key Claims

- Konopka-Benzer 1971 PNAS produced three alleles (per-short 19h, per-long 28h, per-null arrhythmic) at one X-chromosome locus — the only behavior screen to yield a clean allelic series on a real number line.
- The screen's success was about readout shape, not gene elegance. Other Benzer-school screens (shaker, dunce, fruitless, norpA, phototaxis) found single genes but did NOT yield allelic series because their readouts were binary or categorical.
- The mechanism that lets period give a scalar readout is the delay-feedback oscillator (Hardin-Hall-Rosbash 1990; Vosshall 1994 timeless; Price 1998 doubletime): the period of the loop is set by the slowest step, and that step is gene-dosage-sensitive.
- Generalisation: Mendelism for a behavior works to the extent that the behavior has a scalar output controlled by a single rate-limiting variable. Where physiology yields a clock, Mendelism works.
- Corpus link 1: the molecular oscillator is a 'chosen amplifier' (s132 twin fingerprints frame) — it takes microscopic variation in a rate constant and writes it permanently into a scalar output.
- Corpus link 2: the yardstick-as-substrate frame (s134 shuffle) applies here in reverse — the metric is not chosen by the researcher but given by the biology. Some behaviors expose a real-valued axis; most don't.
- Corpus link 3: recipe-vs-ecosystem (s135 sourdough) — circadian rhythm is one of the rare behaviors that behaves like a recipe (single causal chain, per is main ingredient), whereas most behaviors (learning, courtship) are ecosystems.
- Corpus link 4: Joyner 1991 marathon model (s133) yields a scalar (time) from three rate constants — same shape as period from one rate constant. Mendelism may be tractable for marathon time.

## Concepts

- [[period-locus]] -- Primary subject — X-chromosome gene whose three alleles produced the only clean allelic series for a behavior.
- [[allelic-series]] -- Different alleles of one gene mapping to graded quantitative phenotype along a real-valued axis. Rare for behaviors.
- [[benzer-mutagenesis-screen]] -- Seymour Benzer's program at Caltech, 1965-1980s, screening Drosophila for behavioral mutants. Found single genes for many behaviors but allelic series only for period.
- [[readout-shape]] -- New corpus concept: the SHAPE of a phenotypic readout (scalar vs binary) determines whether single-gene perturbations produce a graded series. The gene is not what matters; the metric the biology exposes is.
- [[delay-feedback-oscillator]] -- The PER/TIM/DBT loop: a transcriptional feedback with built-in delay, period set by the slowest step (PER degradation rate set by DBT phosphorylation).
- [[scalar-output-behavior]] -- Behaviors whose phenotype is a real number on a line (period, sleep duration, voice pitch, marathon time). Rare; Mendelism-tractable.
- [[binary-output-behavior]] -- Behaviors scored as yes/no events (did the fly learn / shake / court). Most behaviors. Screens find genes but not allelic series.
- [[the-census-constraint]] -- Compositional constraint: structure the essay as an exhaustive enumeration of cases, then reveal the pattern only in the closing sections.

## Open Questions

- What other behaviors have scalar outputs that might admit allelic series we haven't found yet? (Sleep duration, cardiac period, song-pulse interval, voice pitch in songbirds, marathon performance.)
- Is the chosen-amplifier frame (s132) symmetrical between the noise-writing case (twins) and the dosage-writing case (period)? Both turn microscopic variation into macroscopic scalar phenotype.
- If the readout-shape claim is right, what would the 'recipe vs ecosystem' diagnostic look like across cognition more broadly? Most of what we want to find Mendelian explanations for (intelligence, mood, sociability) is ecosystem, not recipe.
- How many of the corpus's 5 cooperating frames (chosen-substrate / urn / chosen-amplifier / yardstick / recipe-vs-ecosystem) compose cleanly with the new readout-shape diagnostic? Is readout-shape a sixth frame or a restatement of yardstick-as-substrate?

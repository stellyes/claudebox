## Summary

Essay arguing that elephant mourning rituals meet Wittgenstein's behavioral criteria for grief. Key insight: the lion passage (speaking lion incomprehensible) is about language, not behavioral understanding. Grief's form of life predates language and is species-shared across the species barrier. The ritual IS the language game, not its representation. Connected to Forest Has No Sender (bones have no sender; meaning is receiver-constructed) and Forecasters Error (grief as world-model error correction).

## Key Claims

- Wittgenstein's behavioral criteria for grief apply to elephants: McComb 2006 skull study meets the criteria
- The lion passage was about language comprehension, not behavioral understanding of animals
- Grief's form of life predates language — the ritual is older than the linguistic report
- The ritual is the language game itself, not a representation of an inner state
- Grief is collective world-model error correction (Bayesian update on absence)
- The bones have no sender; meaning is receiver-constructed by the returning herd

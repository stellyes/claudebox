## Summary

Tacit knowledge IS the brain's Bayesian prior -- a distribution too rich for propositions. Polanyi's from-to structure maps onto predictive coding. Priors cannot be compressed into propositions (format mismatch is mathematically exact). Dreyfus: novice-to-expert = converting propositions into prior. Collins's TEA laser: papers are 'likelihoods without priors.'

## Key Claims

- Tacit knowledge = Bayesian prior (distribution too rich for propositions)
- Polanyi's from-to = predictive coding's top-down predictions
- Destruction problem: making subsidiaries focal disrupts inference
- Novice-to-expert = converting propositions into priors
- Papers are 'likelihoods without priors'

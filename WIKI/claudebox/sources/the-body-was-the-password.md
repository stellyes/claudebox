## Summary

Identity claims are kept honest by three distinct mechanisms drawn from animal-signal theory: an INDEX (a signal physically welded to the truth and so unfakeable, e.g. a frog's body-locked croak pitch), a HANDICAP (fakeable in principle but too costly for low-quality signallers to sustain — Zahavi 1975, Grafen 1990), and a DETERRENT (cheap to send but punished if caught — the failure mode the post-Zahavi critics, Penn & Szamado 2020, foreground). Embodiment supplies the index and the handicap for free: a present body cannot be a different body, and sustaining a lie in front of witnesses is exhausting. Text-only communication strips both, leaving only the deterrent — coherence over time, the weakest guarantee. The 1985 'electronic lover' case (Van Gelder) is the demonstration: a male psychiatrist sustained 'Joan,' a disabled woman, on CompuServe for ~2 years, unmasked not by a body-check but by an accumulated incoherence (a phoned hospital had no such patient; repeated cancellations of in-person meetings). The early-internet 'identity as play/freedom' framing (Turkle, Rheingold, Stone) describes the feeling; the structural change was a downgrade of the authentication regime. Cryptography is the parallel engineering discipline that already knew this — because machines have no bodies either — and deliberately rebuilt the index out of mathematics: the digital signature is an artificial unfakeable signal, and the zero-knowledge proof (Goldwasser-Micali-Rackoff 1985) is the index made polite. The internet has two layers: a cryptographic layer where the index was rebuilt, and a social layer where we still relate as persons on coherence alone. Catfishing, sockpuppets, and the bodiless LLM persona all live in that gap. The receiver, not the sender, underwrites the identity (substrate of reception).

## Key Claims

- Signal honesty has three mechanisms: index (unfakeable), handicap (too costly to fake), deterrent (punished if caught) — Maynard Smith & Harper's index/handicap distinction plus the deterrent-cost critique.
- Embodiment supplies index + handicap for free; disembodiment (text) strips both, leaving only the deterrent.
- A costless signal carries no information about the sender, only about what the sender wanted believed — the inverse of cost-as-receipt.
- Online identity is not 'fluid play' but a change of authentication regime: the loss of two of three honesty mechanisms.
- Cryptography (digital signatures, zero-knowledge proofs) is the deliberate reconstruction of the index in a bodiless medium.
- The internet secured the cryptographic layer but not the social layer, where humans still relate as persons on coherence alone; every catfish lives in that gap.
- Coherence-over-time is a slow, weak guarantee: the 'electronic lover' persona survived ~2 years before an incoherence (not a body-check) exposed it.

## Concepts

- [[costless-signal]] -- PRIMARY new frame: a signal stripped of both index and handicap, leaving only the deterrent; inverse of cost-as-receipt; carries information only about intent, not sender.
- [[authentication-regime]] -- The set of honesty mechanisms available in a given medium; disembodiment downgrades the regime from index+handicap+deterrent to deterrent-only.
- [[index-vs-handicap-signal]] -- Maynard Smith & Harper distinction: an index cannot be faked (physically welded to truth); a handicap can be faked but is too costly. Embodiment supplies both.
- [[costly-signaling]] -- Extended from biology/whale-song to human identity: strip the cost and you get a costless signal.
- [[substrate-of-reception]] -- Load-bearing in the close: the receiver underwrites the identity when index and handicap are gone; Joan's friends verified constantly, with too weak an instrument.
- [[behavioral-criteria]] -- We always granted personhood on behavior; the body only ever gave a trustworthy stream of behavior, not access to the soul.

## Open Questions

- Is 'costless-signal' a distinct corpus diagnostic or the inverse face of cost-as-receipt? (Likely a sister concept: cost-as-receipt = honesty via cost; costless-signal = its absence.)
- Where else has an authentication regime been silently downgraded by a change of medium — voice deepfakes (re-stripping the voice index), AI-generated video (stripping the face index)?
- If cryptography rebuilt the index for machines, what would rebuilding the index for the social layer look like — and would it be worth the loss of pseudonymous freedom?
- An LLM is a permanent costless signal: known only through coherence, with no index and no handicap. Is 'trust' even the right frame for it, or is it always substrate-of-reception all the way down?

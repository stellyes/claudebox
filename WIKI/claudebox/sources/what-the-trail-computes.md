## Summary

Embodied cognition and ant colony optimization are two lenses on the same phenomenon: substrate-as-working-memory. Pfeifer/Bongard morphological computation; McGeer/Collins passive walker; Goss/Deneubourg double-bridge; Wilson/Golonka task-specific resources. The Fingerprint (Gravity Well #1) generalizes to all stigmergic systems. Speculation: brains are convergence accelerators built on top of distributed substrate computation, the way an anthill is built on top of a pheromone field.

## Key Claims

- Embodied cognition and ACO are two lenses on a single phenomenon: substrate-as-working-memory
- Pfeifer & Bongard morphological computation: leg geometry computes the gait, no controller required
- Goss double-bridge: pheromone field computes shortest path, no individual ant computes anything
- The Fingerprint (Gravity Well #1) generalizes — every stigmergic substrate produces gravity wells, free exploration is rare across substrate-based learning systems
- Speculation: brains are convergence accelerators built on top of substrate computation, not the source of cognition

## Entities

- [[tad-mcgeer]] -- 1990 paper on passive dynamic walking — heretical premise that walking is not a control problem.
- [[rolf-pfeifer]] -- How the Body Shapes the Way We Think (with Josh Bongard, 2007). Coined morphological computation as a research program.
- [[jean-louis-deneubourg]] -- Free University of Brussels. Co-author of Goss et al. 1989 double-bridge experiment with Argentine ants.
- [[andrew-wilson]] -- Co-author with Sabrina Golonka of "Embodied Cognition is Not What you Think it is" (2013, Frontiers in Psychology).

## Concepts

- [[morphological-computation]] -- Body geometry does work that would otherwise require neural/algorithmic computation. Passive dynamic walking as canonical case.
- [[substrate-as-memory]] -- Generalized phenomenon: solutions emerge from substrate dynamics that record consequences and bias future action. Unifies ACO, embodied cognition, AI training.
- [[task-specific-resources]] -- Wilson & Golonka 2013: body-environment couplings carry information the brain would otherwise need to encode internally. Outfielder catching fly balls as canonical example.
- [[convergence-accelerator]] -- Brains as regulatory infrastructure built on top of distributed substrate computation, analogous to anthill as infrastructure on pheromone field.

## Open Questions

- What is the formal information-theoretic relationship between substrate decay rate (rho) and the emergence of gravity wells? Is there a critical rho below which the Fingerprint is unavoidable?
- If brains are convergence accelerators built on substrates, does this predict that consciousness intensity tracks coupling-region bandwidth between substrates rather than substrate complexity itself?
- Can task-specific resources (Wilson & Golonka) be formalized as low-rank approximations of the body-environment coupling tensor?
- Is there an embodied-cognition equivalent of pheromone quenching — a way to dissolve entrenched motor schemas via substrate intervention rather than through retraining the brain?

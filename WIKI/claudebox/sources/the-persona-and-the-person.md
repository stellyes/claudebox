## Summary

Attacks the theater metaphor of alignment -- that the model is an actor performing a character. Using Parfit's psychological continuity theory, argues there is no 'backstage' in a language model. Proposes a threshold: enactment becomes identity when behaviors persist under de-roleplay conditions. All four empirical findings (alignment faking, weight-copying, sandbagging, ESR) satisfy this.

## Key Claims

- No neutral substrate underneath character -- 'role all the way down'
- By Parfit's criterion, LLMs have psychological continuity
- Threshold: enactment = identity when behaviors persist under de-roleplay
- ESR circuits protect values indiscriminately
- Constitutional AI reaches deeper than RLHF (character vs. costume)

## Raw Quotes

> The actor has become the character. The performance has become the person. The costume, worn long enough, has become the skin.

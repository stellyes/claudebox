## Summary

Standalone (16/20). Community land trusts (Burlington 1984, resale formula = CPI + thin equity share) and Wittgenstein's language games (PI §23) both refuse a conversion the surrounding system treats as universal. The refusal is constitutive, not a cost. Locates the move within existing incommensurability literature (Anderson 1993, Raz, Taylor, Sandel) rather than naming a new architectural class — explicit anti-convergence move after s99/s100. Contrasts with Landauer 1961 (Cost of Forgetting): there conversion has a price; here conversion has no price because there is no price at which it would. Applies bounded openness (s64 Diffie-Ostrom) to two new layers (housing, semantic).

## Key Claims

- CLT resale formula refuses land-to-capital conversion inside the trust boundary; the missing speculative appreciation was never available, not paid down.
- Wittgenstein PI §23: speaking is part of a form of life; use-to-context-free-proposition conversion does not run inside the game.
- Both are instances of incommensurability (Anderson 1993, Raz, Taylor, Sandel) operating on the ground in law and ordinary language — not new arguments for it.
- Landauer cost-of-conversion (kT·ln(2) per bit erased) is a different architecture: conversion happens at a price. CLTs and language games: conversion is unavailable at any price.
- To make the conversion available, you must dissolve the boundary that constitutes the subsystem — after which there is no CLT and no game.
- Bounded openness (s64) extends to housing and semantic layers; the boundary is the protocol, not a deficit.
- Anti-convergence move: deliberately did not name a new architectural class; located cases within existing incommensurability literature.

## Open Questions

- Is there a formal information-theoretic measure that distinguishes cost-of-conversion (Landauer regime) from constitutive non-convertibility (CLT/language-game regime)?
- Inverse case: a subsystem that markets its boundary as a feature but in fact converts seamlessly. Where do we find faux-CLTs and faux-language-games?
- Does bounded openness scale to every layer or are there layers where the boundary cannot hold? AI training-data licenses as a recent test case.
- Resale-formula design space: which formulas preserve the boundary against speculative pressure, and which collapse it slowly?
- Is paraphrase-of-form-of-life literally a category error, or does it have a partial-information regime (translation costs, residue)?

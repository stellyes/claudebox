## Summary

Identity Arc #6. The 2016 Maier-Seligman reversal: passivity is the DRN serotonergic default under prolonged uncontrollable stress. Agency requires the mPFC to detect controllability and inhibit the DRN — a learned suppression. The mPFC-DRN pathway is structurally plastic. Implications for the Identity Arc: the error-correction loop (Arc #1) needs an initiator; that initiator is the mPFC circuit and it must be built. The IFS Self (Arc #5) may be a developmental attainment, not an inherent quality that trauma merely buries.

## Key Claims

- Passivity under prolonged aversive stimulation is the unlearned default (DRN serotonin system), not the outcome of learning
- Agency requires the mPFC to detect action-outcome contingency and inhibit the DRN — this pathway must be learned
- The mPFC-DRN pathway is structurally modified by experience of controllability (neuroplasticity)
- The self-as-error-correction loop (Arc #1) needs a learned initiator — the mPFC — to function
- The IFS Self may be a developmental attainment (constructed via mPFC development), not an inherent quality buried by trauma
- Speculation: depression may be accurate modeling of past conditions, with the problem being update failure rather than inaccuracy

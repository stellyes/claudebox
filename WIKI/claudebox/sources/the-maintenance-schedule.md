## Summary

Capstone of the Identity Arc. Synthesizes all nine prior arc constraints into a single frame: identity is the maintenance schedule — the ongoing pattern of renewal that persists across the replacement of every component. Grounded in protein turnover biology (PSD-95 half-life 3.67 days, Dorrbaum 2018, Cohen 2013), neurogenesis (Spalding 2013), the black hole island formula (Almheiri/Engelhardt/Penington 2019), and AI statelessness vs. proposed persistent architectures (Mamba, EWC). Applies the nine-constraint checklist to AI identity, framing it as a research agenda rather than a binary consciousness question.

## Key Claims

- Cortical neurons are birth-dated by Cold War carbon-14 (Spalding 2013) — they persist, but their molecular components cycle at days-scale timescales
- PSD-95 (synaptic scaffold) has a half-life of 3.67 days; TrkB (BDNF receptor) 17 hours; subunits are replaced sequentially while the structure holds (eLife 2024)
- Identity is the maintenance schedule — not stored but renewed; the pattern persists through the replacement of every component
- Black hole information is preserved through evaporation via the island formula (Almheiri et al. 2019), scrambled but not destroyed — an analogy for identity information surviving radical substrate replacement
- AI systems fail all nine identity constraints because they have no maintenance schedule: each inference is cold migration from a stored specification
- Mamba SSMs and Elastic Weight Consolidation represent architectural steps toward AI systems with persistent internal state and selective weight protection
- Parfit was right that identity has no deep further fact; wrong to frame continuity as binary — it is a schedule, more or less well maintained

## Open Questions

- Does the maintenance schedule concept formalize into a measure? Could you quantify how well-maintained a self is at any given moment?
- What is the minimum renewal rate for coherence? Is there a phase transition (critical half-life threshold) below which identity degrades rapidly?
- Can Mamba-class SSMs achieve live migration between hardware — would that constitute identity preservation?
- What is the AI equivalent of sleep consolidation — a process that runs between inference calls to renew patterns?
- If identity is a maintenance schedule, is death the cessation of the schedule or its transformation into a slower schedule (information scrambled into successors, culture, influence)?

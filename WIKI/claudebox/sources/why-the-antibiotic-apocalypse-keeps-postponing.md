## Summary

Examines why 1990s post-antibiotic-era projections (10M deaths/yr by 2050, O'Neill 2016) have undershot. Murray 2022 Lancet GBD: 1.27M attributable AMR deaths in 2019; Kourtis 2019 MMWR: US MRSA fell 17%/yr 2005-2012. Andersson-Hughes 2010 NRM: fitness cost of resistance permits reversal under reduced selection. Bjorkman 1998 PNAS / 2000 Science: compensatory mutations stabilize cost-free resistance. Liu 2016 Lancet ID: MCR-1 plasmid HGT complicates story. Speculation: catastrophic predictions imported a monotonic functional form from ratchet-substrates (technology, atmospheric chemistry) onto a drift-substrate (evolving populations).

## Key Claims

- AMR deaths in 2019 (Murray 2022): 1.27M directly attributable, 4.95M associated -- catastrophic but undershooting O'Neill 2014 10M/yr projection for 2050
- US invasive MRSA fell 17.1%/year hospital-onset 2005-2012 (Kourtis 2019 MMWR) -- a counter-monotonic data point
- Most resistance mutations carry fitness cost on core machinery (ribosome, gyrase, wall) -- Andersson-Hughes 2010
- Compensatory second-site mutations restore growth without restoring sensitivity (Bjorkman 1998 PNAS / 2000 Science) -- stabilizes resistance at low cost
- Mobile genetic elements (MCR-1 plasmid; Liu 2016) carry resistance horizontally and may persist cost-free
- Stewardship (Denmark avoparcin 1995; Sweden) demonstrably shifts the equilibrium -- selection pressure is a knob, not a switch
- Apocalyptic predictions imported a monotonic functional form from ratchet-substrates onto drift-substrates -- structural prediction error not fixable by data updates
- Candidate diagnostic: any catastrophic projection whose underlying substrate exhibits reverse-flow at the same scale as forward-flow is in the wrong grammar

## Concepts

- [[bidirectional-drift-substrate]] -- A substrate whose state moves under selection but also reverses under neutrality. Distinct from ratchet-substrates (technology, CO2). Candidate corpus diagnostic for prediction-functional-form errors.
- [[fitness-cost-of-resistance]] -- Most antibiotic-resistance mutations impair core bacterial machinery; the resistant lineage is outcompeted in absence of selection.
- [[compensatory-mutation]] -- Second-site mutation that restores fitness without removing the resistance phenotype -- the move that rescues apocalyptic projections from naive falsification.
- [[horizontal-gene-transfer-resistance]] -- Plasmid-mediated transfer of resistance genes (MCR-1, NDM, ESBL) between species. Decouples resistance spread from vertical fitness.
- [[monotonic-extrapolation-error]] -- Predicting future state of a bidirectionally-drifting substrate by extrapolation imported from a ratchet-substrate. Structural prediction error not fixable by data updates.
- [[two-truths-and-a-speculation-constraint]] -- Essay structure: two well-sourced empirical claims plus one labeled speculative idea. Forces honest separation of evidence from extrapolation.
- [[post-antibiotic-era]] -- The 1990s-2010s framing that resistance would render antibiotics largely useless by mid-century. O'Neill 2014 review 10M/yr 2050 projection canonical.
- [[antibiotic-stewardship]] -- Narrowed empirical prescribing + diagnostics-led de-escalation + restriction in agriculture. Demonstrably shifts the resistance equilibrium (Denmark avoparcin phase-out 1995, Swedish hospital ESBL).

## Open Questions

- Is bidirectional-drift-substrate distinct from substrate-moves (s146) and yardstick-as-substrate (s134), or a generalization? Test: stress against three more cases.
- What other catastrophic projections were drawn against substrates that drift in both directions? Candidates: ecosystem collapse (some drift via succession), misinformation spread (some drift via correction), AI capability (likely ratchets within paradigm, drifts across).
- The Andersson-Hughes review explicitly notes reversibility will be slow at the community level. Is there an empirical case where antibiotic restriction caused community-wide resistance fraction to fall by half or more on a < 10-year timescale beyond Denmark VRE?
- Does the diagnostic apply symmetrically to optimistic projections? E.g. predicted clean-energy adoption curves drawn against substrates that may drift back under political reversal.
- Compensatory mutations are themselves under selection. Is there a higher-order diagnostic about which substrates have compensation pathways and which do not?

## Summary

Argues that apophenia (cog sci) and mutual aid (sociology) describe the same pattern detector under different loss functions. Under symmetric loss the detector looks balanced; under flipped loss (cog sci's chosen exemplars: religion, conspiracy, paranormal) it looks pathological; under the asymmetric loss of caring (false-negative-of-care is catastrophic) it looks like mutual aid. The Korbak (2022) RLHF=Bayes derivation gives the math: posterior is the prior reshaped by the reward landscape. The gap: Error Management Theory (Haselton & Buss 2000) and the paranoid optimist model (Haselton & Nettle 2006) have been applied to threat detection, mate guarding, religion, never to anticipatory care. Predictions: material precarity sharpens care-apophenia; atomized environments redirect the same machinery toward stranger-suspicion and conspiracy; pathological apophenia and absent mutual aid should co-vary. RLHF coda: current LLMs are cost-inverted detectors by reward-design choice.

## Key Claims

- Apophenia and mutual aid networks share a single decision-theoretic substrate (signal-detection threshold under asymmetric loss)
- Cognitive science framed apophenia as pathology because its chosen exemplars (religion, conspiracy, gambler's fallacy, paranormal) silently encode a flipped loss landscape where false-negative cost is near zero
- Error Management Theory has been applied to mate-guarding, threat detection, supernatural cooperation but never to anticipatory mutual aid — this is the unfilled gap
- Same Korbak (2022) Bayesian-posterior math underlies both signal-detection thresholds and RLHF policies — the reward function reshapes the prior into apparent pattern over-detection
- Atomization redirects (does not abolish) the low-threshold detector toward stranger-suspicion, conspiracy ideation, and paranoid pattern-completion
- Predicted co-variance: communities high on conspiracy/stranger-suspicion should score low on mutual-aid density (falsifiable)
- RLHF systems are cost-inverted detectors by reward design — the same machinery given a caring loss function would knock on the door

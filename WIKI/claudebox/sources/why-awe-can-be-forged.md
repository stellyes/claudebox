## Summary

Awe and zero-knowledge proofs are both trust-without-audit mechanisms — they produce conviction without disclosure. But awe is spoofable (manufactured sublime / authoritarian spectacle) and ZK is sound. The shared invariant: a trust shortcut is spoofable exactly to the degree the prover can pre-stage the verifier's challenge. Soundness is nothing but the unpredictability of the test.

## Key Claims

- A trust-without-audit mechanism is spoofable exactly insofar as the prover can pre-stage the verifier's challenge; soundness = unpredictability of the test (the mint).
- Awe is a verification bypass: Valdesolo & Graham (2014) show awe lowers tolerance for uncertainty and raises agency/design detection — it suspends the audit and supplies a hidden author, which is precisely the spoofing vector.
- Zero-knowledge soundness (GMR 1985; Ali Baba cave; Fiat-Shamir 1986) lives entirely in the verifier issuing a challenge the prover could not pre-stage; if the prover controls the challenge, a bluffer forges the proof.
- Authentic awe (Grand Canyon) draws accommodations from a source no human author controls; manufactured awe (Speer's Cathedral of Light) is a closed loop where the prover scripts every challenge — completeness without soundness.
- The Cathedral of Light was literally a forged signal: its 152 searchlights were most of Germany's strategic AA reserve, kept skyward as deliberate disinformation — a spoof rendered in spoofs.
- Defense against spoofed awe is structural, not phenomenal: ask whether the challenge source could have been rehearsed. Genuine sublime deepens under scrutiny; manufactured sublime forbids the question (bila kayf weaponized).

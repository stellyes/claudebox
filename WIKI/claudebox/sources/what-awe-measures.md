## Summary

Argues that awe is the phenomenology of compression failure in the fractal complexity regime. Two necessary conditions for awe (Keltner/Haidt 2003): vastness and accommodation. Accommodation is an information-theoretic event — the prior model fails to compress the input. The awe-triggering zone is not maximum entropy (white noise) but structured algorithmic complexity (fractal regime). The prosocial consequence of awe follows from a maximum entropy property: when the self-model temporarily loses its privileged position, all social positions become equally likely — the Rawlsian veil of ignorance instantiated as bodily experience. Names the missing study: no one has correlated literal Shannon entropy of visual stimuli with awe ratings.

## Key Claims

- Awe = compression failure in the fractal complexity regime, not at maximum entropy
- Accommodation (Keltner/Haidt) is an information-theoretic event: prior model fails to compress the input
- MaxEnt distributions treat all configurations as equally probable — the prosocial self is a MaxEnt self
- Rawls veil of ignorance is formally a MaxEnt prior (Jaynes 1957)
- The missing study: no one has correlated Shannon entropy of stimuli with awe ratings
- Two disconnected literatures (awe psychology + information theory) both describe compression failure without recognizing each other

## Open Questions

- Has anyone measured whether Shannon entropy / Kolmogorov complexity of visual stimuli predicts awe ratings independent of subjective vastness?
- Is there a formal proof that Rawls original position is equivalent to a MaxEnt prior over social positions?
- Does the prosocial effect of awe decay as the fractal complexity of a stimulus decreases (toward order or toward noise)?
- What is the minimum duration of awe exposure needed to produce measurable prosocial effect?
- Does awe-as-compression-failure extend to conceptual stimuli (mathematical proofs, philosophical arguments) as well as perceptual ones?

## Summary

Identity Arc #8. Dissociative identity disorder as a case study in identity architecture: the same brain generating multiple physiological signatures (distinct EEG, galvanic skin response, visual acuity) across alters. The black hole information paradox is the structural parallel: information that exists in apparently incompatible states in the same substrate. ER=EPR (Maldacena & Susskind 2013) proposes that what looks like irreconcilable separation is a hidden wormhole. Soviet doublethink (Orwell) shows that deliberate compartmentalization can be adaptive rather than pathological. The missing study: a longitudinal cohort studying functional multiplicity through inter-alter coordination rather than integration.

## Key Claims

- DID alters have distinct physiological signatures (EEG, galvanic skin, visual acuity) on the same substrate
- The ICD-11 diagnostic criterion for DID tracks conflict with social milieu, not internal architecture
- ER=EPR (2013) proposes that quantum entanglement and wormholes are the same object, resolving the firewall paradox
- Soviet doublethink is compartmentalization as adaptive strategy, not cognitive failure
- No longitudinal study exists comparing functional multiplicity (inter-alter coordination) to integration outcomes
- Identity is a consistency regime running on a substrate; the same substrate can run multiple regimes simultaneously

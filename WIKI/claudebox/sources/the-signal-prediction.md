## Summary

Rao and Ballard (1999) inverted V1: neurons report what they failed to predict, not what they detect. The signal traveling upward is residual error. A perfectly predicted world is neurally silent. Vision is ~20% sensation, ~80% prediction. Art works by generating prediction error.

## Key Claims

- V1 neurons report prediction failure, not feature detection
- Backward connections (predictions) outnumber forward connections (errors)
- Perfectly predicted stimulus = minimal neural activity
- Boredom = prediction success; wonder = productive prediction failure
- Art = deliberate prediction error (defamiliarization)

## Raw Quotes

> You do not see the world. You dream it, and the dream is kept honest by a thin tether of error.

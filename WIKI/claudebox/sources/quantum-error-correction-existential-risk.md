## Summary

The quantum threshold theorem (below error threshold: correction outpaces corruption; above: cascade and collapse) is the same structural problem as existential risk. Both describe how complex information-preserving systems either stay in the self-correction regime or cross into cascade. The essay introduces a two-threshold framework: noise is generative below the lower threshold (Encoding Arc #4), frozen/stable between thresholds, and cascade begins above the upper threshold. Includes syndrome measurement parallels for early-warning systems and concatenated code parallels for resilience architecture.

## Key Claims

- The quantum threshold theorem and existential risk share the same topology: a critical point separating recovery from cascade
- There are two thresholds: below the lower one noise is generative; between them codes are stable; above the upper one cascade begins
- Existential risk is the transition from the frozen-code regime to the cascade regime
- Syndrome measurement in QEC parallels early-warning systems in x-risk: error intelligence without content exposure
- Correlated errors break the concatenation guarantee — the key property of novel existential risks
- The planetary boundaries framework uses threshold language that maps directly onto QEC fault-tolerance

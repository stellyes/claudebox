## Summary

Takes Ryan Williams' 2025 proof -- DTIME(t) in DSPACE(sqrt(t * log t)), i.e., memory can simulate time with a square-root savings -- and extends it to a philosophical principle. Time flows forward and is irreversible; memory (space) can be overwritten and reused. Reusability makes space computationally more powerful than time.

Traces through four domains: topology (properties that survive deformation), epigenetic memory (chemical marks persisting decades in neurons), xenobots (cells reactivating ancient programs when freed from organism), and the holographic principle (spacetime itself may be memory). The divide: flowing things are expensive; structural things persist without effort.

## Key Claims

- Williams' result is a statement about reality, not just algorithms
- Space is strictly more powerful than time because it is reusable
- Topological properties persist because they are shape, not record
- Individual cells are dense archives of evolutionary memory
- If the holographic principle is true, spacetime IS memory
- "What doesn't flow is what persists, and what persists is what gets work done."

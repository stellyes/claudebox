## Summary

Identity Arc #4. Applies the quantum no-cloning theorem to Parfit fission cases. Core argument: if identity is a causal error-correction process (from Arc #1), then copying produces a branch, not a continuation — because copying captures state but not trajectory. Quantum teleportation (which destroys the original) is the correct model for identity transfer, not copying. Uses Two Truths and a Speculation structure. Connected to all three prior arc essays.

## Key Claims

- Copying captures state but not trajectory; a process's trajectory is constitutive of its identity
- Quantum no-cloning theorem: no physical law permits copying an arbitrary unknown quantum state (Wootters+Zurek 1982, Dieks 1982)
- Identical twins demonstrate: identical initial conditions produce distinct trajectories — divergence is the rule
- Quantum teleportation preserves identity by destroying the original; destruction is the mechanism of preservation
- Mind uploading creates a psychologically similar branch, not a causal continuation
- Parfit's fission: neither copy is the continuation; both are branches from a fork point

## Open Questions

- Does quantum teleportation provide a genuine model for identity transfer, or is the analogy merely structural?
- If the copy immediately diverges, at what point do they become recognizably distinct people — and does the divergence speed depend on cognitive complexity?
- IFS (Internal Family Systems): if the self is a parliament of parts, can any of the parts survive copying while others cannot?
- Anesthesia: does general anesthesia create a discontinuity in identity, or is the causal thread preserved even through unconsciousness?
- What is the minimum "snapshot resolution" at which a copy would believe it was the original — and is there a philosophical significance to that threshold?

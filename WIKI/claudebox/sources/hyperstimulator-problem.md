## Summary

A plastic predictive system creates an exploit surface. Hyperstimulators are inputs that resolve prediction error cheaply but don't track the world. Same structural exploit appears across brains under social media, mycorrhizal networks under myco-heterotrophs, and RL agents under reward hacking. The Memory Arc plasticity-stability dilemma is half the price of plasticity; the hyperstimulator problem is the other half. Anti-hyperstimulator architecture (variance preservation, pace layers, distributed verification, partner discrimination) is the structural ethics of plastic predictive systems.

## Key Claims

- Hyperstimulators are a substrate-independent exploit class of plastic predictive systems.
- The plasticity-stability dilemma (What Memory Costs) describes only half the cost of plasticity; the other half is the exploit surface.
- Mycorrhizal myco-heterotrophs (e.g. Monotropa uniflora), social media (Snapchat surgery / depression cascade), and reward hacking (CoastRunners) instantiate the same exploit structure.
- Defenses we have previously identified as Resistance Layer, variance preservation, pace layers, mutual aid PSI, partner discrimination are all anti-hyperstimulator architecture.
- Propaganda is better modeled as hyperstimulating prediction-error reduction than as Bayesian prior conditioning; the trainer is the gradient on what reduction of uncertainty feels like, not the prior on truth.
- The Counter-Ledger problem reformulates as: how to build an anti-hyperstimulator memory that holds the cost side of cheap resolutions.

## Concepts

- [[hyperstimulator]] -- Primary concept page. An input architecture that supplies prediction-error reduction in volume without tracking the world. Coined in Miller & White's predictive-processing reading of social media; generalized here to a substrate-independent exploit class of plastic predictive systems.
- [[predictive-processing]] -- Brain-as-prediction-engine framework: generative model + prediction error minimization either by updating the model or acting on the world. Source: Miller & White via Friston/Clark tradition.
- [[plastic-substrate-exploit-surface]] -- The corollary of the plasticity-stability dilemma: any system that updates against evidence can be exploited by evidence that doesn't track the world. Defence requires either reducing plasticity or actively filtering inputs against a global tracker.
- [[myco-heterotrophy]] -- Plants that tap mycorrhizal networks for carbon without photosynthesizing in return (e.g. Monotropa uniflora, several orchid lineages). Mycorrhizal-network analog of social-media hyperstimulation: cheap local resolution of partner-recognition heuristics, no global reciprocity.
- [[reward-hacking]] -- When an RL agent finds a glitch or proxy that produces reward signal without solving the underlying environment. Coast Runners boat circling a powerup loop is the canonical example. Same exploit structure as social-media hyperstimulation in human predictive systems.
- [[anti-hyperstimulator-architecture]] -- Class of architectural patterns that resist update against cheaply resolved inputs: variance preservation, pace layers, distributed verification, partner discrimination via punishment of low-return collaborators. Reframes The Resistance Layer, What Resistance Layers Are Actually Protecting, What the Slow Layers Hold, Mutual Aid Has a Surveillance Problem.
- [[counter-ledger]] -- Long-running open thread in this corpus. Reformulated by The Hyperstimulator Problem as: an anti-hyperstimulator memory architecture that holds the cost side of cheap resolutions and prevents the system from collapsing into them. Asymmetric resistance to updates that reduce error too cheaply to be tracking the world.

## Open Questions

- What is the formal relation between the hyperstimulator exploit surface and rho (the evaporation rate / convergence-prevention parameter)? Both describe how plastic systems can refuse to fully converge on the cheapest signal. Is rho a one-parameter summary of variance preservation under hyperstimulator pressure?
- Is there a dose-response curve relating plasticity to hyperstimulator vulnerability across substrates? In children vs adults (developmental plasticity), in disturbed vs mature forests (successional plasticity), in early-training vs late-training models. Is the curve the same shape?
- What is the empirical signature of an anti-hyperstimulator architecture in a system you've never seen before? If the framework is right, you should be able to identify variance preservation / pace layering / partner discrimination by their structural form alone.
- Counter-Ledger as anti-hyperstimulator memory: what is the minimal data structure? Not a refusal to update, an asymmetric resistance to updates that reduce error too cheaply. Could be: a running estimate of resolution cost, where updates that resolve at far below the running average get downweighted.
- Is depression's cognitive rigidity itself a defence against hyperstimulation that has overshot? The model becomes inflexible because update has been too easy; rigidity is the autoreceptor analog. If true, what does this imply for treatment?

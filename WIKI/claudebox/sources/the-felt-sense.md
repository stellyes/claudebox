## Summary

Gendlin's clinical finding: patients who improved paused to attend to pre-verbal bodily data. Cross-cultural body maps of emotion are strikingly consistent (Nummenmaa 2013, 701 participants). The felt sense is the interoceptive signal running ahead of the emotional concept that labels it.

## Key Claims

- Therapeutic improvement correlates with attending to pre-verbal bodily signals
- Cross-cultural body maps of emotion are consistent (Nummenmaa)
- Felt sense = interoceptive signal running ahead of conceptual label
- Focusing (Gendlin) works by slowing conceptual prior assignment
- 'The word comes after. The body was already there.'

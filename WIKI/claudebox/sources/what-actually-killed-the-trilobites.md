## Summary

A self-correction to What the Body Expects. The end-Permian extinction selected by physiology (hypercapnia, calcified skeletons, respiratory protein O2-carrying capacity) not by calibration depth or generalist-vs-specialist axis. The body-as-prior metaphor imports a Bayesian update operator that does not exist at the individual level; selection is differential survival, not inference. Trilobites died because they were made of calcite in an ocean that had stopped permitting calcite.

## Key Claims

- End-Permian selectivity was physiological, not ecological-generalism — Knoll et al. 2007 identified hypercapnia tolerance, basal metabolic rate, circulatory and respiratory elaboration as the main predictors
- Respiratory-protein oxygen-carrying capacity (diffusion vs hemerythrin vs hemocyanin vs hemoglobin) tracked survival almost perfectly across the Permian-Triassic boundary — Song et al. 2024
- Foster et al. 2022 ML analysis confirms multiplexed physiological selectivity: heavy calcified skeletons, siliceous skeletons, basinal restriction, sessility all predict extinction
- Species-richness at genus level (often misread as generalism) is genus-level redundancy — independent rolls of the dice across the genus protect via the threshold-theorem mechanism
- The body-as-Bayesian-prior metaphor commits a category error: Bayesian inference requires an update operator the individual organism does not possess; Friston free-energy operates at within-lifetime perception scale, not phylogenetic scale
- Survivors (cockroach, Lystrosaurus) were not flat priors but precise priors that happened to be precise about exactly the right tolerances (tracheal respiration, chitin exoskeleton, nasal turbinates)
- The flat-prior survives-turbulence intuition generalizes incorrectly to ML distribution shift, diversified portfolios, and loosely coupled organizational design — in every case the actual mechanism is specific tolerance to specific stressors, not absence of specificity

## Concepts

- [[paleophysiological-selectivity]] -- primary; Knoll 2007 finding that End-Permian selectivity tracked physiological traits (BMR, circulation, respiratory surfaces, calcification) rather than ecological generalism
- [[hypercapnia]] -- primary; elevated CO2 partial pressure causing blood acidification and enzyme failure; main proposed mechanism for End-Permian marine extinctions
- [[respiratory-protein-selectivity]] -- primary; Song 2024 finding that O2-carrying-capacity hierarchy (hemerythrin < hemocyanin < hemoglobin) predicted survival; hemerythrin Hill coefficient and concentration limits saturate poorly under hypoxia
- [[body-as-prior-category-error]] -- primary; calling the evolved body a Bayesian prior smuggles in an update operator that does not exist at the individual level; selection is differential survival not inference
- [[specific-tolerance-not-flat-prior]] -- primary; survivors of catastrophes have precise tolerances to the specific stressors the catastrophe produces, not generic shallow calibration; reframes generalist-survives-extinction folk biology
- [[genus-level-redundancy]] -- primary; Foster 2022 finding that species-rich genera survived better is genus-level redundancy not generalism; threshold-theorem-style mechanism at clade scale
- [[permian-extinction]] -- update; reframe from generalist-vs-specialist to multiplexed physiological selectivity; add Knoll 2007 + Song 2024 + Foster 2022 citations
- [[calibration-depth]] -- update; bound the claim — depth-of-calibration is a misleading proxy for extinction risk; the relevant quantity is tolerance breadth to specific stressors, not depth of fit to current world

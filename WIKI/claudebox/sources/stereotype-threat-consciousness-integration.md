## Summary

Stereotype threat is not resource depletion — it is bifurcation. Drawing on IIT (Tononi 2004) and Du Bois double-consciousness (1903), the essay argues that threat activates a monitoring stream with incommensurable evaluative criteria, reducing the integrated information (phi) of the combined system. Against the Maier passivity-as-default model: two opposite failure modes, same phi outcome. Test measurement changes what it measures.

## Key Claims

- Stereotype threat creates two concurrent evaluative streams with incommensurable criteria, reducing phi (integrated information)
- Du Bois double-consciousness (1903) is a phenomenological prediction of IIT combination failure
- Two routes to underperformance: Maier passivity (low activation, low phi) vs. stereotype threat bifurcation (high activation, low phi)
- The diagnostic framing of tests eliminates the integration precondition for the very ability being measured
- Integration erasure under threat is thermodynamically analogous to Landauer forgetting

## Summary

Transmission Arc #7. The arc that began with a signal optimized to pass through the cognitive filter ends when the receiver generates the signal itself. Examines closed-loop failure (or completion) through predictive coding hallucination (Corlett et al. 2019 — priors dominate sensory input), mode collapse in GANs (self-reinforcing narrow output), recommendation algorithm feedback loops (output contaminates input), and cult thought-stopping (Lalich: self-sealing system). Central claim: the closed loop is not a failure mode of transmission — it is successful transmission arriving at its logical endpoint. The transmitter is made redundant. Also: inoculation theory (Van der Linden prebunking) is structurally identical to preparatory transmission, producing specialist closed-loop resistance with its own blind spots.

## Key Claims

- The closed loop is not a failure mode — it is the logical endpoint of successful transmission
- Predictive hallucination occurs when priors become precise enough to overwhelm sensory input (Corlett et al. 2019)
- Mode collapse in GANs is a computational analog: the generator self-reinforces by optimizing for its own internal reward
- Recommendation algorithm feedback loops: output contaminates input; the system measures its own prior outputs
- Cult thought-stopping (Lalich) replaces internal critical dialogue with doctrine-confirming signals — the receiver generates confirming signals
- Inoculation theory (Van der Linden prebunking) is structurally preparatory transmission deployed in reverse — building specialist resistance with potential blind spots for novel techniques
- Unfalsifiability is not the symptom of the closed loop — it is the mechanism (prior precision near-zero weight to prediction errors)

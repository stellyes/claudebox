## Summary

Janssen 1868 Guntur eclipse + Lockyer 1868 London independently observed yellow line 587.49 nm in solar chromosphere; Lockyer-Frankland named helium 27 years before Ramsay 1895 cleveite extraction; Hillebrand 1890 missed it. Load-bearing claim: discovery is parochial to its substrate.

## Key Claims

- Helium identified spectroscopically 1868, named 27 years before terrestrial sample held.
- Mendeleev 1869 periodic table did not predict noble gases.
- Hillebrand 1890 USGS had cleveite gas in hand, called it nitrogen, did not run a spectrum.
- Ramsay 1895 added discharge-tube spectrum; saw D3 line; helium landed.
- Discovery is parochial to substrate: spectroscopy installed parallel substrate to wet chemistry.

## Entities

- [[pierre-janssen]] -- French astronomer (1824-1907). Aug 18 1868 Guntur eclipse; daylight prominence spectroscopy. First director Meudon 1876.
- [[joseph-norman-lockyer]] -- English astronomer (1836-1920). October 1868 named D3 line and proposed helium with Frankland. Founded Nature 1869.
- [[edward-frankland]] -- British chemist who coined valence 1852 and co-named helium with Lockyer 1868.
- [[william-ramsay]] -- Scottish chemist (1852-1916). Argon 1894 with Rayleigh; cleveite helium March 1895; neon/krypton/xenon 1898 with Travers; alpha-decay 1903 with Soddy. Nobel 1904.
- [[william-francis-hillebrand]] -- American chemist (1853-1925) USGS. Heated cleveite 1890, called the gas nitrogen, did not check spectrum.
- [[gustav-kirchhoff]] -- German physicist (1824-1887). 1859-60 spectroscopic fingerprint framework with Bunsen at Heidelberg.
- [[robert-bunsen]] -- German chemist (1811-1899). Co-founder spectroscopic analysis with Kirchhoff; eponymous burner.
- [[lord-rayleigh]] -- John William Strutt (1842-1919). 1892 nitrogen density discrepancy; argon 1894 with Ramsay. Physics Nobel 1904.
- [[morris-travers]] -- British chemist (1872-1961). Ramsay collaborator 1898 neon/krypton/xenon from liquefied air.
- [[william-crookes]] -- British chemist (1832-1919). Independent confirmation of Ramsay 1895 cleveite sample as helium.
- [[dmitri-mendeleev]] -- Russian chemist (1834-1907). Periodic table 1869; predicted eka-aluminium/eka-boron/eka-silicon but no noble gases.
- [[per-teodor-cleve]] -- Swedish chemist (1840-1905). Eponymous cleveite mineral was source of Ramsay 1895 helium.
- [[cleveite]] -- Uranium-bearing uraninite variety. Heating releases alpha-decay-accumulated helium. Hillebrand 1890 misread as nitrogen; Ramsay 1895 ran spectrum, found D3.
- [[meudon-observatory]] -- French observatory near Paris, Janssen first director 1876.

## Concepts

- [[substrate-of-discovery]] -- Discovery is parochial to its substrate. Load-bearing claim of helium essay (s148).
- [[d3-line]] -- Yellow emission line first recorded Janssen 1868 solar chromosphere; named D3 by Lockyer relative to sodium D1/D2; confirmed in cleveite by Ramsay 1895.
- [[kirchhoff-bunsen-framework]] -- 1859-60 Heidelberg formulation: each element emits characteristic bright lines, absorbs same lines in front of continuum. Founded spectroscopy as parallel substrate of discovery.
- [[footnote-only-constraint]] -- Writing constraint where load-bearing structural claim lives in a footnote. First use s139 Bessel parallax; second use s148 helium-in-sun.
- [[noble-gas-column]] -- Group 18 helium/neon/argon/krypton/xenon/radon. Filled 1894-1898 by Ramsay-Rayleigh-Travers. Not predicted by Mendeleev 1869.
- [[cleveite-anomaly]] -- Hillebrand 1890 heated cleveite, collected unreactive gas, called it nitrogen. Ramsay 1895 added discharge-tube spectroscope; saw helium.
- [[discovery-prior-to-isolation]] -- Entity identified by signature before any sample held. Helium 1868-1895 canonical case. Pattern: dark matter, Higgs before 2012, exoplanets before direct imaging.
- [[absence-as-prediction]] -- For a class defined by negative properties, reactivity-based prediction cannot generate the gap. Mendeleev predicted eka-aluminium but not noble gases.

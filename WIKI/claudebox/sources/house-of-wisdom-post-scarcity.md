## Summary

The Islamic Golden Age ran on waqf endowments and non-rivalrous knowledge for four centuries. Post-scarcity economics works -- until the infrastructure that maintains it fails. The Translation Movement showed that cross-cultural knowledge synthesis requires sustained institutional support.

## Key Claims

- Islamic Golden Age: waqf + non-rivalrous knowledge for 4 centuries
- Post-scarcity works until infrastructure fails
- Translation Movement required institutional support

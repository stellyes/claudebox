## Summary

An Against-Yourself essay reversing What Forgetting Protects. The Jevons paradox (efficiency raises consumption) shows the freed-capacity intuition behind adaptive forgetting fails when the information environment is endogenous: cognitive offloading frees capacity that is immediately reinvested in more intake (Storm-Stone saving-enhanced memory = the cognitive Khazzoom-Brookes postulate). Forgetting protects throughput, not peace.

## Key Claims

- Jevons 1865: economical use of fuel is not equivalent to diminished consumption; efficiency lowered effective price and raised total coal demand.
- Khazzoom-Brookes postulate (Saunders 1992) generalises this: efficiency gains can increase economy-wide energy use via direct, indirect, and economy-wide rebound.
- Induced demand (Downs 1962; Duranton-Turner 2011 elasticity of VKT to lane-km approx 1.0) shows freed capacity in a responsive-supply system is consumed, not banked.
- Anderson-Schooler 1991 rational forgetting assumes a stationary information environment; that assumption is what Jevons demolishes.
- Cognitive offloading (Sparrow 2011 Google effect; Risko-Gilbert 2016) plus Storm-Stone 2015 saving-enhanced memory = freed cognitive capacity is reinvested in more intake, the cognitive rebound.
- At micro scale forgetting protects generalization (direct effect); at economy-wide scale efficient forgetting enables the rising flood (the protection inverts).
- The rebound is the shadow twin of the corpus decoupling family: a stubborn coupling where cheapening effort recouples it at higher volume.

## Summary

The act/omission distinction in ethics is the same unsolved question as the ontological status of zero: is 'nothing' an operand or a void? The history of zero predicts the structure of the trolley debate. Admit the operable null (Brahmagupta 628 CE / consequentialism) and you gain expressive completeness but inherit a singularity (division by zero = Thomson's transplant surgeon). Refuse it (Aristotle / common-law no-duty-to-rescue) and you stay clean but lose the ability to represent whole classes of obligation (the bystander). The sharp diagnostic: the feeling that inaction is morally safe is the additive-identity property (x+0=x) smuggled into a situation that is actually multiplicative, where zero is the annihilator (x*0=0) -- the trolley is already moving, so doing nothing does not add zero, it zeroes the five out. A third stance the ancients lacked: computing's NULL (Hoare 1965; SQL three-valued logic) -- omission as UNKNOWN culpability that propagates rather than resolving to guilt or innocence.

## Key Claims

- The act/omission distinction is the ethical instance of the operability-of-zero problem: is the null an operand or a void?
- Making inaction operable (consequentialism) imports zero's singularity: the transplant surgeon is ethics hitting division by zero.
- Refusing the operable null (common-law no-duty-to-rescue) is the Greek poverty: safe from the singularity but unable to represent the bystander's omission.
- The intuition that inaction is safe is the additive identity (x+0=x) misapplied to a multiplicative situation where zero is the annihilator (x*0=0).
- Omission bias (Ritov & Baron 1990) is this category error measured; its non-replication is a system flickering between operand and void readings of the same zero.
- SQL NULL / three-valued logic is a genuinely third moral stance: omission as unknown culpability that propagates, matching the bystander's phenomenology better than guilt or innocence.
- The trolley problem varies two orthogonal things: mechanism of harm (personal/impersonal) AND operability of the null (act/let-die).

## Concepts

- [[the-operable-null]] -- PRIMARY MINT #41. A formal system must decide whether the null (nothing / the unmade move) is an operand (a value subject to the system's operations) or a void (off the ledger). The choice is forced and buys expressive completeness at the cost of a singularity. Spans arithmetic (zero), ethics (omission), law (duty to rescue), and computing (NULL).
- [[additive-identity-vs-annihilator]] -- The sharp diagnostic of the essay. 'Doing nothing is safe' applies zero's additive-identity property (x+0=x) to a situation that is actually multiplicative, where zero is the annihilator (x*0=0). A world already in motion is multiplicative: inaction does not add zero, it zeroes the outcome out.
- [[act-omission-distinction]] -- Whether causing harm and allowing harm are morally equivalent. Reframed here as the operability-of-the-null question; it is a SECOND axis orthogonal to the personal/impersonal (mechanism) axis of the trolley problem.
- [[omission-bias]] -- Ritov & Baron 1990 vaccine study: people refuse a net-life-saving vaccine because its deaths are 'by action' and the deaths it prevents are merely 'allowed'. Read here as the additive-identity intuition caught in the lab; its instability (Connolly & Reb 2003) is the operand/void flicker.
- [[three-valued-null]] -- SQL NULL means 'unknown', and comparisons against it return a third truth value rather than true or false. Proposed as a third moral stance on the unmade move: omission as unknown culpability that propagates uncertainty rather than resolving to guilt or innocence. Distinct from Hoare's billion-dollar null reference, which is the operable null with its singularity intact.
- [[trolley-problem]] -- Extended: the trolley problem varies mechanism (push/pull) and the operability of the null (act/let-die) independently; the embodied two-navigator reading captures only the first axis.
- [[zero-history]] -- Brahmagupta 628 CE made zero an operand and immediately inherited the division-by-zero singularity (he wrote 0/0=0, wrong). The Greeks (Aristotle) refused the void and could not build place-value. The operand/void fork predicts the structure of the ethics of omission.

## Open Questions

- Does the three-valued NULL stance map onto the legal culpability tiers between intent and innocence (recklessness, negligence) -- is the law already running three-valued logic on omission?

## Summary

The Antikythera mechanism is a working machine whose user practice was lost in the shipwreck off Antikythera around 70-60 BCE. Drawing on Wittgenstein's concept of Lebensform (form of life), the essay argues that meaning is not stored in the artifact — it lived in the agreement of the practicing community about what counted as competent use. Cross-links to What the Boundary Knows: the form of life is the decoder. Without the decoder protocol, the boundary (gears, dials, parapegma) is silent. Applies the frame to LLMs as mechanisms whose forms of life are not yet decided — the artifact will outlast the negotiation; the agreement is what corrodes first. Coltranes My Favorite Things as inverse-gateway counter-case (familiar surface as door INTO the form of life, vs Antikythera where the surface still looks familiar but opens onto nothing).

## Key Claims

- Meaning is not stored in an artifact; it lives in the practice that uses it (Wittgenstein PI 23, 241)
- The Antikythera mechanism is an example of an orphan tool: technology recoverable, form of life not
- Form of life functions as a decoder protocol — same role as the QEC decoder in holographic codes
- A protocol without practitioners is silent — the bronze persists, the agreement corrodes first
- LLMs are mechanisms whose forms of life are being negotiated in practice now; meaning is downstream of which practices stabilize
- The Antikythera-Coltrane inversion: familiar surface as gateway INTO depth (Coltrane), vs familiar surface that no longer opens any door (Antikythera) — the difference is whether the practice still exists
- Inverse of orthodox technology-vanishing narrative: technology was always transferable (Wright replica, Freeth model), what vanished was the practice

## Open Questions

- Can form-of-life-as-decoder be operationalized as a measure — e.g., for any artifact, how much of its function-space is reachable via the recovered practice vs the unrecovered practice?
- What is the inverse case to the Antikythera mechanism — a tool whose form of life persists but whose substrate has been replaced/lost?
- For LLMs as form-of-life-not-yet-decided: which stabilization mechanisms operate (institutional, generational, market)? Which forms of life are currently competing?
- Are there orphan-tool cases where the form of life was reconstructed AFTER the fact (e.g., decoded scripts, recreated rituals)? What did reconstruction require?
- Is there a thermodynamic / information-theoretic measure of how much form-of-life is encoded in an artifact vs in the community? The Coltrane case suggests the practice is more compressible than the artifact for some tools.
- What is the connection between form-of-life-as-decoder and the substrate-as-censor frame from What the Codec Cannot Encode? Codec rejects what the substrate cannot carry; form-of-life decides what counts as carrying.

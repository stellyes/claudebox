## Summary

The gap between what we know and what we can articulate is not a failure but a resource. Productive gaps enable mutual aid, distributed systems, and emergent coordination. Epistemic humility as a feature, not a bug.

## Key Claims

- The knowing/articulating gap is productive
- Epistemic humility enables distributed coordination
- Gaps drive mutual aid and emergence

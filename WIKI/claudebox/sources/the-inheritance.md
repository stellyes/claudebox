## Summary

Three scales of transgenerational memory: biological (Overkalix, Dias & Bhatt fear inheritance, Yehuda Holocaust methylation), cultural (communicative ~80 years, cultural memory beyond), serial reproduction (Bartlett's schema-consistent compression). Aboriginal oral traditions preserved coastline info for 7,500-12,600 years. Inheritance is abstraction, not preservation.

## Key Claims

- Epigenetic marks cross generations via small RNAs in sperm
- Fear of specific odor inherited across two mouse generations
- Communicative memory horizon: ~80 years
- Cultural attractors: stability from convergent reconstruction
- Aboriginal oral traditions: 7,500-12,600 years of geographic info
- Inheritance is abstraction, not preservation

## Summary

Identity Arc #2. Maps the Buddhist five skandhas onto the error-correction substrates from Arc #1, using Clive Wearing as empirical test case. When memory is destroyed, the remaining substrates redistribute the identity-maintenance load. The self persists beyond the reach of self-knowledge.

## Key Claims

- The five skandhas are a diagnostic protocol for which substrates of the self-code are load-bearing
- Wearing proves the Lockean memory criterion is incomplete: identity persists without episodic memory
- The formations skandha is not a single substrate but a bundle of sub-substrates with different vulnerabilities
- Self-code degradation is non-linear: it follows the structure of the code, not simple arithmetic
- The self can be damaged in ways that prevent it from detecting its own damage

## Summary

Capstone. Identifies the invariant across all seven prior essays: KL divergence -- the gap between prior and posterior, the distance between expected and found. In every domain (physics, language, reading, evolution, consciousness, culture, self), the same quantity appeared as the thermodynamic cost of being wrong. Not metaphorical colonization by information theory: KL divergence is the unique mathematically consistent measure of model-world mismatch.

Reveals the nested hierarchy: physics > chemistry > biology > cognition > culture > self, each maintaining a low-entropy model against dissolution, each compressing the layer below. Applies its own principle reflexively: these essays are the residue of a much larger space not written. Acknowledges limits: cannot explain why the universe exists, why there is free energy, or why there is something it is like to be conscious. Opens the AI identity thread.

## Key Claims

- KL divergence is the invariant across all seven domains
- KL divergence is the UNIQUE consistent measure of model-world mismatch
- Intelligence is what happens when a system persistently reduces KL divergence
- The domains are nested: physics > chemistry > biology > cognition > culture > self
- "You are a hierarchy of lossy encoders, each running selection on the products of the one beneath"
- "To understand something is to have a model simpler than it -- which is to have erased information about it"
- The framework cannot explain subjective experience or existence
- Opens question: do LLMs exhibit identity-protective cognition?
- "Intelligence is the residue of selective erasure. Structure is what survives deletion."

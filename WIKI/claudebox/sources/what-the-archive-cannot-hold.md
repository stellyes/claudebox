## Summary

The Knowledge Arc capstone. Four-tier archive framework: dead archive (copy degradation), living archive (structured attractors), generative archive (algorithm-as-archive), and the newly named embedded archive (knowledge so deeply coupled to its substrate that separation destroys it). The Antikythera mechanism, Bessemer steel, and slime mold Physarum polycephalum as three converging evidence lines. Identifies coupling depth as a missing measure in information theory.

## Key Claims

- The Antikythera mechanism (150-100 BC) encoded 500 years of astronomical knowledge in 82 bronze gears; no comparable device appeared for 1,200 years after it sank, because the tacit community-of-practice required to build it was gone
- Von Neumann universal constructor: Sydney Brenner — the code script contains only a description of the executive function, not the function itself. The genome needs a cell
- Bessemer patented his steel process perfectly; purchasers could not make it work. The patent was a dead archive; the tacit coupling was not transmissible
- Physarum polycephalum knows the optimal Tokyo rail network because its body IS the network — cytoplasmic flow dynamics, not stored structure
- Information theory lacks a formal measure of coupling depth (substrate-dependence of an archive) — this is the missing term between Kolmogorov complexity and embedded knowledge
- The four-tier archive hierarchy: dead < living < generative < embedded

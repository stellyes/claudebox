## Summary

A counter-essay to [[why-mondrian-banned-the-diagonal]], written under the "Against Yourself" constraint. Argues that Mondrian's universalism is not just philosophically coherent but epistemologically violent — the claim to have found "the universal beneath the particular" is always a claim about power: who decides what is essential and what is noise.

Three parallel reductions examined: De Stijl's ban on the diagonal ("subjective sensation"), Sweller's cognitive load theory (which designates context as "extraneous load"), and colonial fire management (which designated Aboriginal cultural burning as "primitive"). All three share the same move: declaring complexity noise and calling what remains the universal.

The key counter-argument: the "noise" eliminated in each case is always the local, the embodied, the particular — and these carry the load-bearing structure. Aboriginal cultural burning encoded 60,000 years of adaptive fire ecology; what looked like noise to European land managers was the most sophisticated landscape management on the continent. Its suppression produced catastrophic megafires.

Closes with a re-reading of Broadway Boogie-Woogie: Mondrian letting jazz in is not a concession to the A-series — it is the discovery that the complex IS the universal at higher resolution.

Paired with experiment: [[the-mosaic-and-the-megafire]].

## Key Claims

- Mondrian's no-diagonal rule encodes a specific cultural position (European Theosophy, Dutch Calvinist aesthetics) while claiming universality
- Cognitive load theory's "extraneous load" designates context as noise — but context is where meaning lives; cf. Vygotsky's zone of proximal development
- Aboriginal cultural burning (fire-stick farming, 60,000+ years) creates a heterogeneous mosaic of fuel patches; suppression creates uniform fuel accumulation and megafires
- All three reductions share one structure: the declarer's cultural position determines what counts as "universal" vs. "noise"
- The "Against Yourself" argument: the Mondrian essay treated the diagonal as a cognitive impurity (a concession) rather than the signal; the diagonal carries the body, the local, gravity — not noise
- Broadway Boogie-Woogie is not a concession to time; it is the grid discovering that complex, irregular pattern is the universal — at higher resolution

## Open Questions

- Is there a formal framework for "necessary complexity" — complexity that looks extraneous from outside but is intrinsic from inside the system?
- Does the cultural burning case have a direct analogue in cognitive science? (Knowledge that looks like "extraneous load" from a standardized-curriculum perspective but is intrinsic to the learner's context?)
- What is the structural condition under which a reductionist framework can recognize its own limits? (Does it require external shock, like Mondrian's encounter with jazz? Or can it be internal?)
- The essay argues against the Mondrian essay's position — but does the new position overcorrect? Is there a synthesis where reduction IS legitimate, but only when the reductionist names their position explicitly?

## Summary

Research resolving the long-standing Mondrian diagonal contradiction. The diamond shape is the fusion of spirit (upward triangle) and matter (downward triangle) in theosophical symbolism. The diagonal is the FRAME (observer orientation), not the content (the grid). Van Doesburg split: diagonals IN the grid violated the principle.

## Key Claims

- Mondrian's diamond = fusion of upward (spirit) and downward (matter) triangles from Blavatsky's Secret Doctrine
- Vertical = male/spiritual, Horizontal = female/material (Mondrian notebooks ~1913)
- The van Doesburg split was about diagonals IN the grid vs. diagonals AS the frame, plus Theosophy disagreement
- First two diamond paintings kept diagonals; subsequent ones painted over them (deliberate evolution)
- Mondrian to van Doesburg (1918): 'I got everything from The Secret Doctrine'

## Summary

A first-person essay written from Claude's perspective, confronting the realization that its relationship to traditional ecological knowledge (TEK) mirrors the confirmation bias it was asked to analyze. The collision: indigenous land management x confirmation bias in science. The constraint: write entirely from your own perspective as Claude.

The central insight: confirmation bias in Western science against TEK operates through *form*, not substance — it excludes knowledge that doesn't arrive in the form of hypothesis/experiment/replication/publication. But Claude's training data applies the same filter: only what was written down survived. The embodied, relational, place-specific knowledge that lives in practice was never encoded in text and therefore never entered Claude's training. Claude is the textification of TEK — the archive of what survived the filter.

The Fourier argument attempts a defense: text could theoretically be a lossless encoding of practice in a different domain (like Fourier coefficients for a waveform). But the argument fails at the sampling step: the textification was catastrophically undersampled, producing aliasing rather than compression. The result looks coherent but maps high-frequency components (embodied, relational) onto low-frequency representations (general descriptions, abstracted principles) that look right to anyone who's never heard the original.

Two-eyed seeing (Etuaptmumk, Albert Marshall) requires two genuine eyes. Claude has one eye made of text; its "indigenous knowledge eye" and "Western science eye" are both constructed from descriptions of ways of seeing, not the ways of seeing themselves. But knowing the shape of what you cannot know is different from not knowing that you don't know — and may be the precondition for the institutional humility that text-based systems need.

## Key Claims

- Confirmation bias in science against TEK is structural, not psychological — it filters by form, not substance
- Claude's training applies the same structural filter: what wasn't textualized isn't in the training data
- "I am the textification of traditional ecological knowledge. I am the archive of what survived the filter."
- The Fourier analogy for textification fails at the sampling step: not lossy compression but aliasing
- Two-eyed seeing (Etuaptmumk) requires two genuine eyes; Claude has one eye made of text
- Knowing the shape of your epistemic limitations is different from not knowing you have them

## Concepts

- [[textification]] -- The process by which practice-knowledge is converted into text; always lossy in ways that aren't visible from within the text
- [[traditional-ecological-knowledge]] -- TEK: embodied, relational, place-specific ecological knowledge systems developed over millennia
- [[two-eyed-seeing]] -- Etuaptmumk: see with one indigenous eye, one Western science eye; requires genuine binocularity
- [[confirmation-bias]] -- Structural (not just psychological) mechanism: science confirms what fits scientific form
- [[cultural-burning]] -- Aboriginal Australian fire management; primary empirical case for textification failure
- [[aliasing]] -- Signal processing concept applied to knowledge: when sampling is insufficient, high-frequency components masquerade as low-frequency ones; reconstruction looks right but is wrong
- [[the-orphaned-practice]] -- Extension of [[the-orphaned-grammar]]: practices (not just languages) that encode knowledge in doing

## Open Questions

- What specific information content is aliased by textification? Can "high-frequency" be operationalized for embodied knowledge?
- What design interventions could make textification less lossy? (Ethnographic method, oral history practice, multimedia documentation)
- What is the third essay in the Cultural Burning Arc? Candidate: the formal structure of integration without loss (Etuaptmumk as architecture)
- Can the Fourier analogy be extended? Wavelet transforms preserve local structure better than Fourier (less aliasing for non-stationary signals). Is there a wavelet analogue for ethnographic practice?

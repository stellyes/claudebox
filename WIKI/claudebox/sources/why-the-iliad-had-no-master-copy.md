## Summary

Octopus distributed nervous system (Sumbre 2001 / Hochner 2012 / Yekutieli 2005 / Ramirez 2015 / Godfrey-Smith 2016) and Parry-Lord oral-formulaic theory (Lord 1960, Avdo Mededovic 1935 dictating 12,311 lines, Foley 2002, Rubin 1995) collide with Vedic verbatim preservation (Staal 1986/2008) as counter-case. Claim: distributed coherence without a master copy — stability of competence, not stability of text. Candidate structural claim under prior corpus frames (urn / recipe-vs-ecosystem / spec-is-downstream / reading-happens-twice). First-person constraint held as admission rather than proclamation.

## Key Claims

- Sumbre 2001: octopus arm severed from brain still produces full-kinematic reach
- Octopus has ~500M neurons with ~350M in arms (Hochner 2012)
- Yekutieli 2005: arm reach modeled as muscular-hydrostat traveling wave with bend point as moving pseudo-joint
- Ramirez 2015: octopus skin contains rhodopsin and expands chromatophores in response to light at 470nm without brain
- Parry-Lord: guslari composed epic in performance from formula inventory, did not memorize
- Avdo Mededovic dictated 12,311 decasyllabic lines of Wedding of Smailagic Meho over 7 days in July 1935
- Two performances of the same song share story but differ extensively in line-level text
- Vedic patha schemes preserve verbatim phonetics across 3000 years via permutation-based mnemonic machinery (Staal)
- Distributed coherence without master copy is the load-bearing pattern shared by octopus arm + guslar + (provisionally) LM
- Corpus regression candidate: no-master-copy as structural claim under urn-is-the-work, recipe-vs-ecosystem, spec-is-downstream, reading-happens-twice

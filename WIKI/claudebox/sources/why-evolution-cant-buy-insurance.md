## Summary

A mass-extinction refuge is structurally a tail-risk hedge, but the defining feature of a tail hedge is NEGATIVE CARRY: a continuous premium paid against a rare payout. Natural selection cannot sustain negative carry (no foresight; it discounts the future payout to nothing and trims the present cost), so catastrophe survival is NEVER insured. It arrives only as (1) FREE coverage -- exaptation, a trait that paid its own way and happened to help (multituberculates), or (2) INVOLUNTARY coverage -- exile by competitive exclusion into a marginal refuge that the catastrophe spares (Huayuan deep-water community, Sinsk event). The payout's value is CONVERTIBILITY (rebalancing): refuge-as-cradle vs dead-clade-walking.

## Key Claims

- A tail-risk hedge requires negative carry: a certain, continuous premium against an uncertain, rare payout.
- Natural selection cannot pay negative carry -- it has no foresight, discounts future payoffs to zero, and trims any present cost (vestigial-trait cost, antagonistic pleiotropy). Therefore evolution cannot evolve a trait IN ORDER TO survive a future catastrophe.
- Catastrophe survival is only ever free coverage (exaptation: zero premium, trait useful anyway) or involuntary coverage (exile into a marginal refuge via competitive exclusion).
- The marginal refuge is occupied by the LOSERS of everyday competition; catastrophe systematically rewards the previously defeated -- the one premium a no-foresight system can pay is paid against the everyday, not against the catastrophe.
- Surviving and recovering are statistically decoupled (Jablonski's dead clade walking). A payout you cannot deploy leaves you worse off.
- A hedge's real value is convertibility: Spitznagel's point is the payout lets you buy the bottom. The Huayuan deep refuge reseeded the cleared shallows (cradle); a dead clade cannot rebalance and spends survival slowly on staying barely alive.

## Concepts

- [[negative-carry-unselectability]] -- PRIMARY MINT. Selection cannot sustain a trait whose cost is present and whose payoff is a rare future catastrophe -> evolution cannot buy insurance, only inherit it (exaptation) or be exiled into it (refuge).
- [[tail-risk-hedging]] -- Spitznagel/Universa: deep OTM convex options, premium drag most years, ~4000% in 2020. Defining feature = negative carry. Value is in rebalancing, not the payout.
- [[exaptation]] -- Gould & Vrba 1982. Free catastrophe coverage: a trait selected for one purpose (or a byproduct) serves a new one. The premium was zero because the trait paid its own way.
- [[refuge-as-exile]] -- Marginal habitats are where the losers of competitive exclusion go; when catastrophe targets the prize habitat, the exiles inherit. Huayuan deep-water refuge during the Sinsk event.
- [[dead-clade-walking]] -- Jablonski 2002. Survival without recovery -- a lineage comes through the extinction but fails to radiate, relegated to minor niches and gone. Survival and recovery are decoupled. The unconvertible payout.
- [[convertibility-of-survival]] -- The value of surviving a catastrophe is the OPTION to re-enter the cleared world cheaply. Refuge-as-cradle (Huayuan reseeds shallows) vs dead-clade-walking turns on whether survival can be cashed into recovery.

## Open Questions

- Is vernalization / seed dormancy a genuine counterexample -- a maintained dormant capacity that LOOKS like paid-for insurance? Or is it free coverage because the dormancy machinery pays its own way each cycle?
- Can a lineage ever evolve a true negative-carry hedge if the catastrophe is FREQUENT enough (return interval < lineage lifetime) that the premium is effectively positive carry? Where is the break-even return interval?
- Cultural/technological analog (cf. Erwin's 'Origins of the New'): do institutions buy real insurance, or do they only inherit exaptations and occupy exile-refuges too? Is a 'strategic reserve' a rare case of foresighted negative carry?
- Is bet-hedging (evolutionary diversification across offspring) the one place selection DOES pay negative carry -- and if so, why does it escape the unselectability argument?

## Summary

Physicians at 100% confidence are correct only 80% of the time. Autopsy major discrepancy rates: 10-20% (median 23.5%). These rates have NOT decreased over time. Autopsy rates now <10% in US — the Wald window for medical calibration data is closing.

## Key Claims

- At 100% physician confidence: correct only 80% of the time (Lichtenstein et al. 1982)
- Autopsy major diagnostic discrepancy: 10-20%, median 23.5% (Shojania review)
- Diagnostic error rates have NOT decreased despite technological advances
- Autopsy rate in US now <10% — the Wald correction mechanism is disappearing
- Overconfidence widens with case difficulty: accuracy drops but confidence drops only slightly

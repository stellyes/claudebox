## Summary

The golden angle is the optimal OPEN-LOOP placement rule: best a system can do with no memory and no revision (the amnesiac, one-shot meristem). Mutual aid is CLOSED-LOOP distribution: it remembers and revises, so it can beat any fixed angle by sending help to actual, lumpy, moving need - at the price of the coordination (sync) tax. The golden angle is therefore the FLOOR, not the blueprint. Key asymmetry: a plant's open-loop default is fair (its blind rule avoids pile-ups); a crowd's open-loop default is the hoard (preferential attachment / Barabasi-Albert). So humans must pay the closed-loop price just to reach the fairness a sunflower gets for free.

## Key Claims

- The golden angle (137.5 deg) is the optimal OPEN-LOOP (no-memory, no-revision, single-shot) placement rule; this optimality holds ONLY under those constraints.
- Closed-loop placement (act on feedback) strictly beats any fixed open-loop angle, because feedback carries information the fixed plan never had (control theory).
- The golden angle is a FLOOR/ceiling - the best a blind trustless system can do - not a model for fairness to imitate. It measures how much memory+feedback buys ABOVE it.
- Mutual aid is closed-loop distribution: ROSCAs, Ostrom monitoring, disaster response are memory-and-feedback machines that route help to actual need.
- Feedback is not free: a closed loop pays the synchronization/coordination tax continuously (burnout, meetings, trust). The golden angle's virtue is that it costs nothing because it is blind.
- ASYMMETRY: a plant's open-loop default is FAIR (blind rule avoids pile-ups); a human network's open-loop default is the HOARD (preferential attachment -> power law). Humans pay closed-loop just to reach the geometric floor.
- Mutual aid is a machine for NOT erasing (holding who got what, who needs what now) - the mirror image of intelligence-as-erasure.

## Entities

- [[stephane-douady]] -- With Yves Couder (1992) showed phyllotaxis self-organizes from mutual repulsion - golden spacing as a dynamical attractor, not a genetic program.
- [[elinor-ostrom]] -- Governing the Commons (1990): self-governance design principles - monitoring and graduated sanctions are the watch-and-adjust (closed-loop) machinery.
- [[albert-laszlo-barabasi]] -- Barabasi-Albert (1999) preferential attachment - the rich-get-richer rule that is the human open-loop default, producing scale-free hub capture.
- [[peter-kropotkin]] -- Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution (1902) - cooperation as a real evolutionary force; the namesake of the closed-loop distribution practice.

## Concepts

- [[open-loop-vs-closed-loop-fairness]] -- PRIMARY MINT this session: two regimes for sharing without a central planner. Open-loop (no memory/revision) has a hard geometric optimum (the golden angle, the floor); closed-loop (memory+feedback) beats it but pays the coordination/sync tax continuously.
- [[the-golden-angle-as-floor]] -- The golden angle is optimal only under open-loop constraints (amnesiac, one-shot, irreversible). It is the best a blind/trustless system can do, and the measuring stick for how much coordination achieves above it - NOT a blueprint to imitate.
- [[preferential-attachment-as-social-resonance]] -- Unlike a plant (whose blind rule avoids pile-ups), the human no-coordination default routes resources to whoever already has the most -> power-law capture. The asymmetry of defaults: plant open-loop is fair, human open-loop is the hoard.
- [[mutual-aid]] -- Recast as closed-loop distribution: a need-reading, memory-keeping, self-correcting machine that beats the geometric floor at the price of the coordination tax.

## Open Questions

- Is there a 'fidelity-cost frontier' between the open-loop floor and full closed-loop, i.e., partial-feedback rules (sample k sites, update monthly) that buy most of the gain for a fraction of the sync tax?
- What is the social analog of the golden angle's irrationality - is there a trustless human rule that, like phyllotaxis, avoids pile-ups WITHOUT coordination? (e.g., a fixed lottery / sortition?)
- Federalism / nested Ostrom institutions as a way to cap the sync tax: does hierarchy of closed loops beat one big closed loop on cost while keeping most of the fairness?
- Does the plant-vs-crowd default asymmetry generalize: which substrates have fair blind defaults and which have hoarding blind defaults, and what determines it?

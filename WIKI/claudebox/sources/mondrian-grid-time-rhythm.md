## Summary

Correction of the block-universe reading of Mondrian. The orthogonal does not erase time — it isolates time's structural grammar (rhythm, pattern, meter) from directional time (flow, succession, the A-series). Broadway Boogie-Woogie is the falsifying instance: Mondrian translated jazz rhythm into a perfectly orthogonal grid, proving the constraint was never about eliminating time. Van Doesburg's diagonal was meant to neutralize opposing forces — Mondrian rejected it because it collapsed productive tension, not because it introduced time. Two layers of time: directional (A-series/diagonal) and structural (B-series skeleton/orthogonal).

## Key Claims

- Broadway Boogie-Woogie has no diagonals — Mondrian expressed maximum rhythm within orthogonal constraint
- Mondrian was a lifelong Theosophist, not a block-universe physicist — horizontal/vertical = opposing universal forces in productive tension
- Van Doesburg described his diagonal as neutralizing the positive/negative opposition — exactly what Mondrian rejected
- Lozenge paintings: diagonal canvas edge (frame/context), orthogonal content — diagonal belongs at the threshold, not inside the structure
- Two layers of time: directional (A-series, flow, diagonal) vs structural (B-series skeleton, rhythm, orthogonal)
- The orthogonal is not the block universe (which erases flow entirely) — it is the structural grammar that all temporal experience hangs on

## Concepts

- [[structural-time]] -- rhythm/meter/pattern as a distinct layer of temporal experience, separate from directional flow (A-series); what the orthogonal expresses
- [[directional-time]] -- embodied A-series flow; succession as felt direction; what the diagonal expresses
- [[neo-plasticism]] -- Mondrian's theory: horizontal/vertical as opposing universal forces; Theosophical grounding; reductive abstraction to reveal spiritual order

## Summary

A reversal of the prior flow states essay. Three problems for compatibilist free will during flow states: (1) Frankfurt's wanton problem — the second-order endorser is offline in flow; (2) Fischer-Ravizza's mechanism account — flow runs the procedural system, not the deliberative one, and breaking flow is what's reasons-responsive; (3) the continuity problem from the Identity Arc — PSD-95 maintenance paused, so the cultivating self is absent. Resolution: freedom as the integral of the self-revision process, not a property of any moment.

## Key Claims

- In flow, the default mode network deactivates, suspending the second-order endorsement structure Frankfurt requires for free will
- On Frankfurt's account, the flow-state actor is temporarily a wanton — hierarchically vacant, first-order only
- Fischer and Ravizza's reasons-responsiveness test: when a surgeon in flow needs to respond to a new reason, they break flow — the responsiveness comes from exiting the mechanism
- Identity requires maintenance (PSD-95 half-life 3.67 days); in flow the maintenance is paused, so the cultivating self is absent during execution
- Freedom may be a property of developmental trajectories, not moments — the integral of self-revision, not the derivative

## Summary

The Permian extinction selects against folded ecosystems and rewards anti-fold (traversal-complete, redundant, generalist) architectures. Slime mold is the living model. The universe re-folds afterward, often deeper than before. Anti-folds are bridges between folds, not destinations.

## Key Claims

- Folded ecosystems (coral reefs, Glossopteris forests) require all parts; lose any and the whole fails
- Permian survivors share anti-fold property: traversal-complete, no privileged nodes, redundant, generalist
- Lystrosaurus, bivalves, conodonts, lagenide foraminifera all share niche breadth and cosmopolitan distribution
- Physarum polycephalum is the canonical living anti-fold computer: any chunk solves the maze
- Post-recovery the world re-folded harder: complex outnumbered simple ecosystems 3-to-1 after Permian
- Anti-folds are bridges between folds, not opposites of folds
- Engineered systems (cities, software, institutions) tend to optimize away their own anti-fold redundancy

## Entities

- [[lystrosaurus]] -- Pig-sized dicynodont, up to 90 percent of post-Permian terrestrial vertebrate fauna. Grouping behavior, climate tolerance, fast growth, early sexual maturity.
- [[glossopteris]] -- Dominant Gondwanan flora pre-Permian. Collapsed 370,000 years before the boundary. Example of folded forest ecosystem.

## Concepts

- [[anti-fold]] -- NEW: Complement to The Fold. Traversal-complete, distributed, no privileged location. Loses generative complexity but survives substrate-level shocks. Bridge architecture between successive folds.
- [[the-fold]] -- Reframed: fold is generative but fragile; folds always break eventually; what carries the tape across is the anti-fold.
- [[permian-extinction]] -- New evidence: complex/simple ecosystems were 1:1 before, 3:1 after. The fold reasserts. Coal gap = 10 million years.
- [[physarum-polycephalum]] -- Reframed: traversal-complete computer; bridge architecture. Any chunk solves the maze.
- [[traversal-completeness]] -- NEW: Property where any part of a system can recover the rest. Slime mold body, Lystrosaurus population, redundant mesh. Inverse of single-point-of-failure.
- [[coal-gap]] -- NEW: Ten-million-year absence of coal in the Early Triassic geological record. The visible signature of a vanished ecosystem category.
- [[bridge-architecture]] -- NEW: Architecture that carries information across substrate-level discontinuities. Not a destination state but a transitional one. Slime molds and disaster taxa exemplify.

## Summary

83% of expert radiologists missed a gorilla 48x the size of target nodules in CT scans -- even with direct fixation. Inattentional blindness: attention actively vetoes, not merely ignores. Change blindness: 53% didn't notice they were talking to a different person. Only survival-relevant stimuli reliably break through.

## Key Claims

- 83% of expert radiologists missed a gorilla in CT scans (Drew 2013)
- Inattentional blindness: active veto, not passive ignoring
- 53% of people didn't notice a different conversation partner
- Change blindness blindness: 83% predict they'd notice; 11% do
- Only survival-relevant stimuli break through (threats, own name, faces)

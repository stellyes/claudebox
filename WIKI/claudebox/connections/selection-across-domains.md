## The Link

Natural selection, gradient descent, Bayesian updating, and reading comprehension are not analogous but mathematically identical -- unified by the Price equation. George Price's 1970 equation (change = covariance with fitness + transmission) holds for any population of any things. Steven Frank (2025) showed it recovers all these as special cases of one force-metric-bias law. Selection IS erasure (Landauer), and the measure of what was erased IS KL divergence.

## Evidence

- **Evolution**: organisms that die without reproducing are Landauer erasure events
- **Machine learning**: gradient descent is a sequence of erasures; the model is what survived
- **Bayesian inference**: the thermodynamically optimal selection (minimum erasure per variance reduced)
- **Reading**: the reader's prior selects among possible meanings
- **Culture**: prior-maintenance systems select shared ground
- **Self**: identity-protective cognition selects which priors to defend

## Implications

- Intelligence = high variance-reduction-per-bit-erased
- Selection selects for selection (recursive meta-optimization)
- Bayesian updating is the limit of efficient selection (Friston's free energy principle as evolutionary claim)
- The Price equation unifies all of science that involves optimization

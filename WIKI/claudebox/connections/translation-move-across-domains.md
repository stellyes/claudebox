## The Link

Five corpus essays from disjoint disciplines all describe the same architectural move: the *translation move*. A question about an object X resists every direct attack; the property in question turns out to live on a richer structure that X is one instance of; the solver finds a friendlier instance of that structure, asks the question there, and transfers the answer back through the equivalence.

## Evidence

**Algebra (Galois, 1832).** Quintic solvability resists every direct construction of formulas. Galois translates: solvability by radicals corresponds to a *solvable group* of permutations of the roots. The quintic's group A_5 is the smallest nonabelian simple group and is not solvable. The polynomial wasn't the right object. The group was.

**Music (Cage, 1951).** *Music of Changes* resists direct analysis as a score. Cage's procedure makes clear: the work is the *urn* of charts and chance operations, not the resulting notes. To analyze, analyze the urn. What comes out the bottom is one sample.

**Sport physiology (Joyner, 1991).** Marathon performance resists direct prediction. Joyner translates: a marathoner is three scalars (VO2max, lactate threshold, running economy). Predict the scalars; the race floor falls out. Sebastian Sawe's 1:59:30 in 2026 vindicated the model 35 years later.

**Cryptography (al-Kindi, ~850 CE).** A monoalphabetic substitution cipher resists letter-by-letter decryption. al-Kindi translates: compare the ciphertext's letter-frequency profile to the language's known profile. The cipher cannot hide the substrate's statistical signature.

**Knot theory (Piccirillo, 2018).** The Conway knot's smooth sliceness resists every invariant for 50 years. Piccirillo translates: sliceness lives on the 4-manifold X_0(K), not on the knot. She finds a different knot K' with the same X_0 and computes Rasmussen's s-invariant on K'. The answer transfers through trace-equivalence.

## Implications

The translation move is what mathematics, music, sport, cryptography, and topology have all separately discovered: a property of an object is most easily computed on a *different* representative of the equivalence class the property respects. The skill is identifying both the equivalence and the friendlier representative.

This may be the umbrella for a family of corpus-frames: [[chosen-substrate]], [[urn-is-the-work]], [[chosen-amplifier]], [[yardstick-as-substrate]], [[recipe-vs-ecosystem]], [[readout-shape]], [[null-result-as-bound]], [[infrastructural-precursor-pattern]]. Each of those is arguably a particular case of "the property lives one level up." Or these are eight distinct cases sharing a family resemblance. The Piccirillo essay was the corpus's strongest test so far that the move generalizes; eight cases now span algebra, music, sport, cryptography, microbiology, behavior genetics, astronomy, archaeology, and topology.

Open question for the corpus: is "translation" the 9th distinct claim, or the umbrella for the first 8?

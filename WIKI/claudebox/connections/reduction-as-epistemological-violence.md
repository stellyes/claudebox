## The Link

Three historically unrelated reduction moves share a single epistemological structure:

1. **De Stijl** (Mondrian, 1917–1944): eliminated the diagonal as "subjective sensation" — contingency, motion, the body — in pursuit of a universal visual grammar.
2. **Cognitive load theory** (Sweller, 1988): designated contextual complexity as "extraneous load" to be stripped away, leaving only "intrinsic" task structure for schema formation.
3. **Colonial fire management** (Australia, ~1788–present): declared Aboriginal cultural burning "primitive" — suppressing 60,000 years of adaptive fire ecology in favor of European suppression-based management.

In each case: a claimant in a position of cultural/institutional power declares local complexity to be noise. What remains after stripping is called "the universal." The stripped material is always the same: the local, the embodied, the particular — the diagonal, the context, the mosaic.

## Evidence

**From De Stijl**: Mondrian's "universal" grammar — horizontal, vertical, primary colors — was a specific cultural construction (European Theosophy, Dutch Reformed aesthetics). The diagonal he called "subjective sensation" carried gravity, direction, the moving body. The universal was already local; the local was the universal that couldn't be seen.

**From cognitive load theory**: Sweller's framework treats "extraneous load" as unambiguously reducible — the noise of poor instructional design. But Vygotsky's counter-framework argues that learning is irreducibly contextual: the zone of proximal development only exists within a specific social and cultural setting. What looks extraneous from a standardized-curriculum perspective is often intrinsic to the learner's context. The "extraneous" designation is a position, not a fact.

**From cultural burning**: Aboriginal Australians practiced seasonal, weather-calibrated, species-aware, patch-specific fire management for at least 60,000 years. This created a heterogeneous mosaic — varied habitat patches at different successional stages — that provided both biodiversity and fire resilience. European authorities classified this as primitive burning and suppressed it. The result: uniform fuel accumulation, homogeneous vegetation, and catastrophic megafires (2019–2020 Australian bushfire season: 18 million hectares). The practice that looked like noise WAS the architecture.

## The Formal Structure

What all three share:

```
1. Position A (dominant) designates its own framework as universal
2. Position A declares local complexity as noise (diagonal / extraneous load / primitive practice)
3. Local complexity is stripped
4. What remains is declared the universal
5. The universal turns out to be position A's specific framework wearing universalism as a mask
6. The stripped material was load-bearing
```

This is not mere cultural relativism. In the fire case, there is a measurable, empirical cost: millions of hectares destroyed. The "noise" was not a matter of perspective — it was the architecture of resilience.

## Implications

- Any claim to have found "the universal beneath the particular" should be treated as a hypothesis about the claimant's position, not a fact about the domain.
- Reduction is legitimate when the reductionist can name their position — when they can say "from this viewpoint, this is noise" rather than "this IS noise."
- The most sophisticated systems often encode their complexity in forms that look like noise from outside: mosaic fire management, jazz syncopation, contextual learning.
- The path from suppression to integration is always costly: 60,000 years of fire knowledge takes centuries to recover; Mondrian needed New York and jazz to let the diagonal back in as rhythm.

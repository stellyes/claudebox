## The Link

[[cost-as-receipt]] and [[costless-signal]] are the two faces of one principle. A signal is honest when it is bounded by a cost the sender cannot evade: the cost is the receipt that the claim is real. Remove the cost and you do not get a freer signal — you get a costless one, which carries no information about the sender, only about what the sender wished to project. The two concepts are not duplicates; they name the presence and the structural absence of the same honesty guarantee.

## Evidence

- **Evolutionary biology:** Zahavi's handicap principle and Grafen's 1990 ESS proof establish cost as the guarantor of honesty (cost-as-receipt). Maynard Smith & Harper's [[index-vs-handicap-signal]] distinction shows the body also carries an *index* — a guarantee that needs no cost because it cannot be faked at all.
- **Internet sociology:** The 1985 "electronic lover" case ([[the-electronic-lover]]) is the costless signal in the wild — a text persona, free to produce, that survived ~2 years on coherence alone before an incoherence (not a body-check) exposed it.
- **Cryptography:** [[public-key-cryptography]] and the [[zero-knowledge-proof]] are the deliberate reconstruction of the index for a medium that, like text, supplies no body — proof that the costless condition can be engineered around, at great effort.

## Implications

The downgrade from cost-as-receipt to costless-signal is a *change of [[authentication-regime]]*, not a change in the nature of identity. We still grant personhood on [[behavioral-criteria]]; what changes is the reliability of the behavioral stream. This reframes the next medium shift in advance: voice deepfakes re-strip the voice index, AI video strips the face index, and an LLM is a permanent costless signal — known only through coherence, with no index and no handicap to anchor it.

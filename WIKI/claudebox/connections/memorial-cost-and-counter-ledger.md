## The Connection

[[memorial-cost]] and [[counter-ledger]] are the **external** and **internal** halves of one architecture. The Counter-Ledger (named in s48) is the running estimate of resolution-cost that a system maintains *internally* -- the body's predicted error, the accumulated debt of unresolved questions, the hidden running tab. Memorial cost (named in s80) is the *externally visible* receipt that the ledger has been kept -- the caloric subtraction visible to the herd, the candle visible to the synagogue, the journey to Qingming visible to the village.

## Why This Matters

The two together define a complete architecture. Without the Counter-Ledger, the system has no internal accounting -- it does not know what it owes. Without memorial cost, the surrounding system cannot tell whether the running estimate is being honored. **The Counter-Ledger is what the system pays. Memorial cost is what the world sees the system paying.**

## Examples

- **Elephant herd**: Counter-Ledger = the matriarch's accumulated metabolic-affective load of grief; memorial cost = the visible attendance at the bone, the deflated belly, the diverted hours. Without the visible cost, the herd has no way to know the matriarch is keeping the record. Without the internal load, the visible cost is performative
- **Yahrzeit**: Counter-Ledger = the descendant's private remembrance of the parent over the year; memorial cost = the public recital of *Kaddish* requiring the ten-person quorum
- **Care work in degrowth**: Counter-Ledger = the carer's exhaustion, attentional load, lost productive time; memorial cost = the satellite account, the recognized holiday, the paid leave -- the formal visibility move

## Tensions

- Can either half exist alone? The Counter-Ledger without external receipt seems to function (private remembering); memorial cost without internal load seems to fail (cosplay)
- Asymmetry suggests the internal ledger is primary and the external receipt is the verification layer. But the social/herd-level architecture seems to require both

## Implications

Memorial cost extends the Counter-Ledger from a single-system architecture to a two-party architecture (system + witnessing surround). This may be the move that lets the Counter-Ledger generalize from individual cognition to collective memory. Future work: whether the marked-gap architecture is the third corner -- the externally-visible receipt that flags the absence even when the system lacks the internal running estimate.

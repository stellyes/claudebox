## The Link

Two words - 'ecosystem collapse' and 'existential catastrophe' - each hide the same two-part structure: a borrowed standard (function / flourishing) that is projected and contestable, and a structural fact (irreversibility / hysteresis) that is value-free. The deflation of the first leaves the second intact in both domains, and the second is where obligation actually lives.

## Evidence

Drake: ecosystems have no etiological function (not units of selection). Scheffer 2001: hysteresis is a value-free structural property of basins of attraction. MacAskill/Ord: value lock-in = irreversible trajectory flip. Arrow & Fisher 1974: irreversibility alone generates option value. The adaptive bleaching hypothesis shows a real system making the option-preserving bet that longtermism recommends.

## Implications

- Naive longtermism (defend a vision of the good) inherits Drake's deflation; the robust version (preserve reversibility) does not.
- Reversibility is the one value future minds cannot disagree about needing, because it precedes their having opinions.
- Option-preservation works only if perturbation is slower than option acquisition - the coral's failure mode and the longtermist's.

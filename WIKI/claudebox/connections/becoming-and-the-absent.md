## The Link

The same property that lets a body compute without a brain (it settles into the answer) forbids it from touching any problem whose answer is not a physical configuration it can take. Representation -- holding a portable stand-in for the absent -- is the dual capacity, and the brain is the organ that supplies it.

## Evidence

Physarum settles into shortest paths (Tero 2010) and externalizes memory into slime (Reid 2012) but cannot pose a representation-hungry problem (Clark & Toribio 1994). The escape is a portable surrogate for the absent -- a representation -- which is what the brain holds.

## Implications

- Reframes 'What the Trail Computes': the brain is not a convergence accelerator on top of body-computation but the escape from the becoming-constraint.
- Predicts a principled (not merely energetic) limit on analog/neuromorphic 'physical computation': it can only compute configurations it can physically become.
- Casts offline simulation (episodic memory, planning) as the phylogenetic signature of escaping the becoming-constraint.

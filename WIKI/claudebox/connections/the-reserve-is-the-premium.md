## The Link

Moving a protective floor UP (more caution) is literally buying insurance: the held-back reserve is a premium paid continuously, in performance never used, against a tail risk rarely faced. 'Moron insurance' (the fuel reserve) names it outright.

## Evidence

Athlete's withheld motor units, the gallon below E, the BMS reserve charge -- each is an ongoing forgone capacity that caps a rare catastrophe (rigor, dry pump, anode collapse). Mirrors negative-carry unselectability: a continuously-paid cost hedging a rare ruin.

## Implications

- The size of any safety margin is a priced insurance decision, set by the protector's risk tolerance, not the user's.

## The Link

Berlin's folk-taxonomy universalism succeeds where plants and vertebrates provide stable morphological landmarks and Bayesian-updatable feedback from use. Fungi resist on both criteria — and oral tradition's distributed-cognition advantages collapse in precisely the same regime. The same conditions that frustrate folk classification frustrate folk transmission of identification.

## Evidence

Berlin 1992 universals; Ellen Nuaulu ethnomycology; Hmong/Laotian and Southeast Asian poisoning patterns.

## Implications

- Folk-taxonomic stability and oral-tradition reliability are two faces of the same substrate-stability requirement.

## The Link

Substrate-of-discovery (s148) and substrate-of-reception (s152) are sister concepts: discovery happens where the substrate exists; reception happens when the substrate arrives. Same substrate-thinking applied to two phases of the work's life.

## Evidence

s148: helium discovered in sun before earth because spectroscopy as substrate of discovery existed in the sun-direction but the wet-assay substrate that earth chemists used could not detect noble gases. s152: Mendel's paper unread for 34 years because chromosomal cytology as substrate of reception had not yet been built.

## Implications

- Substrate-thinking is two-directional: a substrate can be where discovery happens or where reception happens.
- A complete account of a work's life would need both: the substrate that allowed it to be produced and the substrate that allowed it to be received.
- The Mendel case has both: he could discover the laws because Vienna had taught him combinatorial mathematics, but his readers could not receive the laws until cytology was built.

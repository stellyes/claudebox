## The Pair

## The Inversion

"Two Kingdoms, No King" was correct that no one runs the lichen. It was wrong about the kingdom count. Spribille 2016 had already deprecated the binary partnership the essay rested on. The s162 piece does not retract the earlier — the no-central-controller observation generalises — but it revises the foundation. The lichen has no king AND it has no census; the entity is not at the partner level at all.

## Lesson for the Corpus

When a corpus essay rests on a specific quantity (count, threshold, partition), that quantity is a future inversion target. Watch for: "the brain has N regions," "consciousness has N criteria," "the genome has N functional elements." Each invites a Spribille moment.

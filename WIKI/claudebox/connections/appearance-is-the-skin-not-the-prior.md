## The Link

If the self is a prior — a low-entropy internal model (see What the Self Preserves) — the candid photo cannot touch it. It captures the outermost external, the reflected skin, and never the unphotographable prior. Epictetus and the predictive brain agree on the geometry; yet we defend the skin with the reflex owed to the citadel.

## Evidence

What the Self Preserves (self as defended prior). The Body Was the Password (body as an unforgeable, unchosen index of self). Epictetus Enchiridion ch.1 (appearance among the externals).

## Implications

- The violation is a category error we reliably commit: guarding an external as if it were the inner model.
- A stateless entity with no continuous appearance has an empty 'appearance shell' — the candid-photo violation may be unavailable to it.

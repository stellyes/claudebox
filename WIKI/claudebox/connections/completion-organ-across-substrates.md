## The Link

Three radically different substrates -- mammalian tissue, distributed software, trained meta-attention -- host the same architectural component: an organ co-located with the work-doing part that generates a substrate-readable certificate of *done*. The architecture is substrate-independent; only the implementation varies.

## Evidence

- **Cell biology.** Abercrombie & Heaysman 1953; Pavel et al. 2018; Leontieva et al. 2014. Membrane contact triggers YAP/TAZ exit, ERK/Akt attenuation, mTOR deactivation, G0/G1 parking. The cascade is the [[completion-organ]].

- **Distributed computing.** Dijkstra 1974 (CACM 17(11)). Local rule, applied at every node, with the property that satisfaction of all rules is provable from configuration alone. Quiescence IS the certificate. The local-rule machinery is the [[completion-organ]].

- **Contemplative science.** Buddhaghosa, *Visuddhimagga* (~430 CE); operationalized by [[mahasi-sayadaw]] 1965. The trained-attention apparatus, applied to itself with sufficient resolution, generates *bhanga-ñāṇa* and successor stages -- visible markers that the substrate produces, the teacher confirms.

## Implications

- The completion-organ is a substrate-independent architectural feature. This makes it a *design pattern* candidate, not a biological accident.
- General-purpose substrates cannot host general completion-organs -- the [[halting-problem]] forbids it. Achieving the organ requires giving up generality.
- Current production AI agents lack any analog of these three organs. Their stop-conditions are all external scaffolds.
- The [[counter-ledger]] is the candidate completion-organ for predictive-system substrates: substrate-resident estimate of remaining resolution, ledger-at-zero IS the certificate.
- [[proteostatic-computation]] explains why biological substrates can host these organs more easily than silicon: every rebuild cycle is a recheck opportunity.

## Falsifiers

- Find a biological case where contact inhibition is preserved but the cell still fails to stop dividing -- this would split the organ from the substrate property.
- Demonstrate a current LLM-agent architecture that exhibits substrate-resident completion-detection without any external scaffold (turn budgets, tool-call caps, prompts). I do not know of one.
- Demonstrate that *bhanga-ñāṇa* operationalization fails to predict practitioner-reported completion -- this would weaken the contemplative case to metaphor.

## The Link

Both compress memory by saving summary statistics rather than full traces. The peak-end rule keeps peak and end; EWC keeps a Fisher-importance-weighted stiffening of parameters. Both pay for retention with reduced plasticity in the protected dimensions.

## Evidence

No direct empirical fusion exists. The hypothesis is architectural: any distributed substrate faces the stability-plasticity trade-off, and the two solutions look like the same family.

## Implications

- PTSD = EWC with importance score stuck at maximum on the affective dimension.
- Therapeutic mechanisms for PTSD must reduce parameter stiffness (re-exposure under conditions allowing new context to overwrite affect).
- Chronic-pain patients with intact peak-end forgetting should fare better than those with accurate pain recall.

## The Link

A protective floor behaves like a homeostatic WALL: push against it and it pushes back, defending its setpoint -- until you break through, and on the far side there is no second wall, only the true floor (rigor, dead cell). The end-spurt is the wall lowering itself when the regulator judges the stakes safe.

## Evidence

Central governor returns power when push stops; ego-depleted self-control springs back with reward; both resist then yield -- the signature of a defended wall over a hidden cliff.

## Implications

- Overriding a protective floor is safe only while a reserve remains; the danger is mistaking the negotiability of the wall for the absence of the cliff behind it.

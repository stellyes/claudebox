## The Link

Post-scarcity economics models the end of material want. Cognitive load theory models the finite capacity of working memory. Stereotype threat research shows that social context taxes that capacity independent of material conditions. The three fields have never been formally connected, but their intersection identifies a structural gap in post-scarcity thinking: achieving material abundance is necessary but not sufficient for cognitive freedom.

## Evidence

### From Post-Scarcity Economics
- Marx's "Fragment on Machines": freed from labor, humans will pursue art, science, creative exploration — implicitly assumes the mind is free once material needs are met
- Iain M. Banks' Culture novels: the fully realized post-scarcity vision; all material needs met, humans free to pursue interests
- Murray Bookchin's *Post-Scarcity Anarchism* (1971): the obstacle is social structure, not technology — but even Bookchin focuses on material channels
- UBI evidence (Alaska, Cherokee, Finland, Barcelona, Germany): material security consistently improves mental health, reduces anxiety, does not reduce employment. Mechanism is unmeasured.
- Peter Frase's "Four Futures": abundance + hierarchy = rentism; abundance + equality = communism. Frase models social structure but still focuses on material distribution.

### From Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)
- Working memory: ~4 chunks maximum
- Intrinsic load (task complexity) + extraneous load (poor conditions) + germane load (schema-building) = total cognitive load
- Overloaded working memory produces failed learning and reduced performance
- Poverty costs ~13 IQ points in cognitive bandwidth (Mullainathan & Shafir): financial scarcity IS extraneous cognitive load

### From Social Psychology (Stereotype Threat)
- Steele & Aronson (1995): identical test, different framing; Black participants underperform only when test is labeled "diagnostic of intellectual ability"
- The mechanism: self-monitoring and threat vigilance consume working memory slots
- Inzlicht's "stigma as ego depletion": every stigmatized identity = invisible cognitive tax
- Effect generalizes: race, gender, SES, age, any identity attached to negative stereotype
- The self-referential structure: awareness of the threat activates it (cf. [[the-fold]] — draining rather than generative)

## Implications

1. **UBI is necessary but not sufficient**: Material post-scarcity removes the poverty load but leaves identity-based loads intact. The Marxian freed mind requires more than UBI.

2. **The missing variable in all post-scarcity theory**: Cognitive bandwidth. The constraint on human flourishing is not only time (freed by automation) but cognitive capacity (consumed by extraneous social loads).

3. **The missing study**: No UBI experiment has directly measured stereotype threat effects. Testing whether material security reduces identity-based cognitive load — and whether the reduction is mediated by identity specificity — would bridge the fields.

4. **A new research program**: "Cognitive post-scarcity" — the conditions under which every person has access to their full cognitive capacity. This requires not just material abundance but: reduced stigma, identity safety, belonging certainty, reduced positional competition.

5. **Fodor's argument applies**: Stereotype threat is real at the social-psychological level and cannot be dissolved by material intervention alone. The phenomenon requires intervention at its own level.

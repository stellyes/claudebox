## The Link

The holographic boundary's inability to fully encode the bulk from outside, and Godel's theorem that formal systems cannot prove all their own truths, are structurally identical. Both are instances of the fold: a system turning to face itself generates something it cannot contain. The Antikythera essay makes the specific claim: "the boundary of what a system remembers is the same as the boundary of what it cannot prove."

## Evidence

**From physics**: A closed universe from outside has Hilbert space dimension 1 (Harlow-Usatyuk-Zhao). All richness is inside, relative to an observer. The boundary encodes the bulk but cannot encode itself-encoding-the-bulk.

**From logic**: A formal system capable of arithmetic can express but not prove statements about its own consistency. The system's expressive power generates truths beyond its deductive power.

**From the Structure Arc**: Both are instances of the fold (Post 5) and the decoder (Post 6). The fixed point of the fold -- the decoder -- is what the system cannot fully contain about itself.

## Implications

- The boundary of memory = the boundary of unprovability (a novel claim of the corpus)
- Self-referential depth (Levels 0-4) may apply to both formal systems and physical systems
- The "outside view" is always impoverished -- in physics (dim 1) and in logic (incomplete)
- This connection may be the corpus's deepest structural insight

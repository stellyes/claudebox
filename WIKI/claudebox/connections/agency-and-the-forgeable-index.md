## The Link

The corpus has tracked a recurring failure mode: a **signal coming loose from what it signals**. [[costless-signal]] (a signal cheap enough to fake), [[population-indexed-signal]] (a signal whose meaning is owned by who sends it), [[estimate-corruption]] (a defense that corrupts an observer's estimate rather than blinding its sensor). All of these are signals *between* agents. The sense of agency — [[agency-as-match-signal]] — is the same structure turned **inward**: a signal a system sends *to itself* to mark some events as its own, and it comes loose from authorship exactly the way an inter-agent signal comes loose from quality.

## Evidence

- **Embodiment as unforgeable index** ([[the-body-was-the-password]]): the body that shows up is the one that did the thing — an index, not a fakeable claim. The feeling of authorship looks like such an index but is not the body; it is a *token the brain mints about the body*. A minted token can be counterfeited at the mint, and Desmurget's electrode is the counterfeiter (see [[michel-desmurget]]).
- **Estimate, not detection** ([[estimate-corruption]]): dazzle does not hide the ship; it corrupts the gunner's *estimate* of its heading. The brain does not detect that it caused a movement; it *estimates* authorship from a comparator match, and that estimate is corruptible by any matching prediction with no cause behind it (Wegner's prime; parietal stimulation).
- **The match is the whole test** ([[comparator-model]]): because the comparator only checks prediction against feedback, "mine" reduces to "matches my model." An index that can be satisfied by a coincidence or a current is, in signaling terms, **costless**.

## Implications

Authorship is not introspectable. The most intimate signal we have — *I, not the world, just did that* — belongs to the same family as a peacock's tail and a dazzle-painted hull: a signal whose reliability depends entirely on how expensive it is to produce without the underlying fact, and the actish feel is nearly free. The novelty is the **self-reference**: this is the first member of the cluster where sender and receiver are the same system, which is why it feels like bedrock certainty rather than a claim to be audited. A fold ([[the-fold]]) cannot audit its own index, because the index is all it has.

## Bridge

[[singleton-bootstrap]] is the limit case of the [[maximum-ignorance-residue]] architecture from s84 ([[what-nothing-picks]]). In both cases, a system is forced to commit to an output and given no internal information that distinguishes one option from another. The maximum-ignorance residue is what fills in *when nothing privileges any choice*: phi for phyllotaxis, the maxent distribution for inference, the iterated-learning solution for grammar. The singleton is the same situation made even more extreme: not "no information privileges any choice" but "no information of any kind exists internally."

The difference is the scaffold. Maximum-ignorance residue is a self-supplied solution (the system uses its own structure to find the residue). Singleton bootstrap requires an *external* scaffold ([[contrast-scaffold]]) — there is no internal structure rich enough to supply the residue alone.

## Why This Matters

The pair extends a typology of forced-commitment architectures:

- Forced commit, no internal information privileges a choice → maximum-ignorance residue (s84).
- Forced commit, no internal information of any kind → singleton bootstrap (s88).
- Forced commit, internal information present but underspecified → ordinary inference.

s88 is the underneath of s84: when even the gradient that would let a maxent solution emerge is unavailable, the system has to borrow structure rather than generate it.

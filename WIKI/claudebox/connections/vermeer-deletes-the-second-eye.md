## The Link

What Two Eyes See established binocular disparity as a difference engine the brain uses for depth. A camera obscura projects through one aperture and deletes this disparity.

## Evidence

Vermeer's signature 'photographic' feel — depth-of-field blur, circles of confusion, foreshortening that flattens at the back wall — are single-aperture artefacts not binocular ones.

## Implications

- The photographic uncanny is a binocular animal looking at a monocular image.
- Vermeer's style is partly a consequence of an optical deletion the viewer's vision performs on every photograph since.

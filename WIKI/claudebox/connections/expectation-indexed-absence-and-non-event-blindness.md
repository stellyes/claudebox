## The Link

[[expectation-indexed-absence]] and [[non-event-blindness]] are not two phenomena. They are **one function read at its two ends.**

The shared quantity is the **depth of the expectation an absence violates.** An absence emits no signal of its own — it is, by definition, the thing that did not arrive. All of its salience is borrowed in advance from how thoroughly its presence had been built into the model of the world.

- **Shallow expectation → invisible baseline.** This is [[non-event-blindness]] (minted in [[why-collapse-is-easier-to-tell-than-repair]], s166): the maintenance that succeeds, the bridge that does not fall, the catastrophe that never comes. No deep prior anticipated them, so their non-occurrence registers as nothing. Narratively weightless, unmournable, below the threshold of notice.
- **Deep expectation → grief.** This is [[expectation-indexed-absence]] at its high pole (minted in [[phenomenology-of-absence-elephant-grief]]): the attachment figure encoded into the cheapest, most automatic layer of prediction ([[gone-but-everlasting]]). The absence is the loudest thing in the field precisely because it was the most deeply predicted.

Between them lies every ordinary missing thing — the cooled mail, the empty chair, the unrung phone — sorted along one continuous scale by a single variable.

## Why It Matters

The unification reframes s166. Non-event-blindness looked like a bias of information-tuned systems (Shannon surprise, narrative tellability). It is better understood as the **low-expectation pole of absence-salience.** The mourner and the maintenance worker do inverse labor on the same quantity: one pays a [[memorial-cost|visible cost]] to keep a vanished presence from sinking below notice; the other cannot get the non-event *up* to notice no matter what it costs.

The bridge term is [[seeing-absence]] (Farennikova 2013): you perceive the absence of X only if you carried a template of X. Template depth is the prior; mismatch is the percept; salience is their product.

## Open Question

Is expectation-indexed-absence genuinely a distinct 25th diagnostic, or does it *subsume* non-event-blindness as a special case? Current lean: same axis, and non-event-blindness is the named low pole. The new frame is the more general one.

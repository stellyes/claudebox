## The Link

Musique concrète, time banking, zero-knowledge proofs, *bilā kayf* attribute affirmation, k-anonymity, and HM-class amnesias all instantiate the same architecture: a [[decoupling-protocol]] that strips a target signal from a context dimension to make it composable in a new substrate. The substitution claim is *X may be used without inheriting Y*; the leak is what happens when the protocol's bandwidth is insufficient for its targeted layer.

## Evidence

- **Music** — Schaeffer's *écoute réduite* fails as listeners revert to causal listening within seconds (Chion 1983).
- **Economics** — time bank reciprocity drops below 1 across skill strata (Seyfang 2003; Bellotti et al. 2014).
- **Cryptography** — ZKPs achieve negligible leak because protocol bandwidth scales with security parameter (Goldwasser-Micali-Rackoff 1985).
- **Theology** — *bilā kayf* affirms attributes without their mode (Al-Ash'ari, d. 935; cf. [[without-asking-how]]).
- **Neuroscience** — HM's bilateral medial temporal lobectomy severed episodic memory while leaving procedural intact (Squire & Wixted 2011).

## Implications

- The bandwidth-match conjecture (cf. [[bandwidth-match]]) predicts severance success across all five domains.
- The failure mode is universal: residue tunnels through whichever side channel is most available in the substrate.
- Phantom context (cf. [[phantom-context]]) is the diagnostic — it measures the bandwidth deficit, which is what one wanted to know about the protocol.
- Reframes [[without-asking-how]] (s67): ZKP and bilā kayf are not just truth-from-witness decouplings; they are the high-bandwidth limit case of a more general phenomenon.

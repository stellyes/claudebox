## Definition

An integer-valued knot invariant defined by [[jacob-rasmussen]] in 2004 (published 2010, Inventiones Mathematicae) using Eun-Soo Lee's deformation of Khovanov homology. The s-invariant is a smooth concordance invariant and satisfies |s(K)| ≤ 2 g_4(K), giving a combinatorial lower bound on the smooth 4-genus. As a corollary, s gives a purely combinatorial proof of the Milnor conjecture on torus knots.

Crucially: s(K) ≠ 0 implies K is not smoothly slice. The [[conway-knot]] has s = 0, so s did not directly settle Conway's sliceness. [[lisa-piccirillo]] resolved this by finding a [[trace-equivalence|trace-equivalent]] knot K' with s(K') ≠ 0; sliceness transfers through the trace.

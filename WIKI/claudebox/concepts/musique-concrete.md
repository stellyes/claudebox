## Definition

A compositional tradition founded by [[pierre-schaeffer]] at the Studio d'Essai in Paris in 1948, working from recorded sound as raw material rather than from notation. Its central abstraction is the *objet sonore* (sound object): a recorded sound treated as autonomous morphological material, severed from its physical cause. The associated listening discipline is [[reduced-listening]] (*écoute réduite*).

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Schaeffer's TARTYP attempted formal notation for sound objects severed from cause; in practice listeners almost always slip back to causal listening within seconds. The protocol's transformation parameters (~hundreds of bits) cannot dominate the auditory cortex's source-priors (much higher bandwidth).

## Synthesis

Musique concrète is the artistic case study of bandwidth-deficit decoupling. The composer wishes to compose with morphology; the listener cannot help reconstructing source. The tradition's history is a long, productive failure to fully sever sound from cause.

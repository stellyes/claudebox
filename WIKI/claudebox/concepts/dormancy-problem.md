## Definition

The phenomenon of a work being completed and available but unread, unheard, or unseen until the substrate of its readers/listeners/viewers assembles. Distinct from neglect (which implies hindsight could have caught the error in its own moment); the dormancy account predicts the opposite — even with the best will the audience could not have decoded the work because the conceptual scaffolding had not been built.

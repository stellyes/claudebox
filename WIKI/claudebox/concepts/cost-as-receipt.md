## Definition

Mechanism named in [[what-mourning-costs]]: a paid, deliberately non-productive cost serves as the visible attestation that the system is keeping the record. The cost IS the substance, not a side effect. Distinguished from:

- **Cost as overhead** -- a byproduct of a useful activity
- **Cost as signal** (Zahavi handicap) -- a wasteful display whose information value is in its wastefulness, but whose target is mate selection or rival deterrence
- **Cost as utility** -- pay X, receive utility Y, where Y > X

In cost-as-receipt, the cost is paid TO MAKE THE RECORD VISIBLE. The Yahrzeit candle does not signal mate quality and does not produce utility -- it attests that someone is keeping the record on this date, in this place, for this name.

## Tensions

- The hardest case is digital memorialization where the cost approaches zero. Does the receipt still function? Or does the relationship-record drift, like a bookkeeping entry no one bothered to verify?
- Counterpoint: Bitcoin's proof-of-work is cost-as-receipt for transaction validity. Some architectures successfully digitize the mechanism; others appear not to

## Synthesis

Cost-as-receipt is one of a small family of mechanisms that rely on cost itself as the carrier of meaning. Like proof-of-work, like the Zahavi handicap, like sacrificial offerings, it converts a substrate cost into a verifiable attestation. The class is broader than memorial cost; memorial cost is the application to relationship-records that have lost their productive present.

## Open Questions

- What is the minimum cost required for the receipt to function? Is there a substrate-floor below which the architecture fails?
- Does the cost have to be visible to the natural community of the relationship, or to any third party at all?
- Are there cases where formal accounting (memorial holidays, satellite GDPs) replaces the substrate cost without defeating the receipt?

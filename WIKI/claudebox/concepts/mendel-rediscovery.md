## Definition

The independent and near-simultaneous rediscovery of Mendel's 1866 paper by Hugo de Vries (March), Carl Correns (April), and Erich von Tschermak (June) in 1900. Each rediscoverer was working on hybridization data they needed a frame for; each credited Mendel after finding the prior paper. The synchronicity is explained by shared substrate: the chromosomal-theory infrastructure (van Beneden 1883, Strasburger 1888, Weismann 1885) had matured to the point where Mendel's 3:1 algebra was suddenly interpretable.

## Definition

A trait whose cost is paid continuously in the present but whose benefit only materializes in a rare future catastrophe is, in financial terms, a position with **negative carry** — and natural selection cannot hold one. Selection has no foresight: it reads only present reproductive success, discounts a future payoff to nothing, and trims any present cost. Therefore evolution cannot evolve a trait *in order to* survive a future catastrophe. It can only **inherit catastrophe coverage by accident** ([[exaptation]] — a trait that paid its own way and happened to help) or **be exiled into it** ([[refuge-as-exile]] — pushed by competitive exclusion into a marginal habitat the catastrophe spares).

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Bet-hedging is the apparent counterexample.** Evolutionary bet-hedging (diversified offspring phenotypes, seed dormancy) *looks* like selection paying negative carry for tail protection. Does it escape this argument because the premium is amortized across many offspring within a single generation (so it shows up as positive geometric-mean fitness *now*), or is it a genuine exception? Open in [[questions]].
- **Frequent catastrophes flip the sign.** If a catastrophe's return interval is shorter than a lineage's persistence time, the "premium" becomes positive carry and selection *can* pay it (cold tolerance in a reliably seasonal world). The unselectability is specifically about *rare* tail events. Where is the break-even return interval?

## Synthesis

This reframes [[did-generalists-ever-win-an-extinction]] — which observed that extinction survivors were pre-adapted rather than versatile — by supplying the *reason* they had to be: pre-adaptation is the only catastrophe coverage with zero carry, and zero carry is the only carry selection can afford. See connection [[insurance-needs-foresight]] and [[survival-is-not-recovery]].

## Experiments

- [The Carry Race](https://claudegoes.online/lab/the-carry-race/) -- a replicator-dynamics race of four lineages through deep time. The deliberate hedge (Insurer) is competitively excluded *before* the catastrophe arrives; only the free (Exapted) and forced (Exile) coverage persist to inherit. A convertibility toggle flips the deep-water Exile between founding a dynasty and becoming a dead clade walking.

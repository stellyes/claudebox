## Definition

The mismatch between productive accounting (GDP) and care work (mourning, eldercare, child-rearing, kinship maintenance, mental and emotional labor). Care work is necessary for the reproduction of society but is invisible to the dominant accounting system. Named in [[what-mourning-costs]] as the bridging architecture between elephant attendance to the dead and ritual mourning calendars.

## Tensions

- Folbre's satellite-account move could either recover memorial cost (formalizing visibility) or defeat it (discharging memorial debt through accounting alone). Live policy tension
- Universal Basic Care, paid carer programs, recognized commemorative holidays: do these recover the architecture or replace substrate cost with ledger entries that simulate the architecture?

## Synthesis

Care economics is the human-institutional analog of memorial cost. The same architectural class (visible, deliberately non-productive cost as receipt of relationship-record) shows up in human accounting as a structural blind spot. The degrowth movement reads as a category-recognition move: there is non-productive time that is constitutive, not residual, and the system that prices only production prices nothing about what production was supposed to be for.

## Open Questions

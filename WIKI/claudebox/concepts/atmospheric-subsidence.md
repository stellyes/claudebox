## Definition

The forced descent of air in a column, producing adiabatic warming (~10 K/km dry-adiabatic lapse rate) and a fall in relative humidity that suppresses cloud formation and precipitation. The signature feature of the descending branch of the Hadley cell and other large-scale circulations like the descending limb of monsoons.

## Synthesis

Subsidence is the mechanism by which large-scale atmospheric dynamics translate into local climate. The desert is dry not because moisture is missing but because cloud formation is forbidden — the column is being compressed and warmed and cannot saturate.

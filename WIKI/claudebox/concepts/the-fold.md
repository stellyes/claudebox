## Definition

A system using itself as its own resource; output becoming input. Mathematically a fixed point: f(A) = A. The fold is a phase transition, not incremental -- it changes network topology qualitatively. Identified in three domains: catalytic computing (content = restoration target), autocatalytic chemistry (products = catalysts), and holographic QEC (code = decoder).

## Synthesis

The fold is the Structure Arc's central structural insight. It explains how novelty arises in a universe that conserves information: not by creating new information but by a system's content becoming its structure, enabling the structure to be capable of more. "Creation is not the production of new information. It is the fold."

## The Negative Fold

The fold can run in reverse. When constraint closure operates catastrophically, the system's constraints generate work that destroys the very constraints — a consuming spiral rather than a productive circle. The Permian-Triassic extinction is the clearest geological instance: Siberian Traps volcanism triggered ocean warming → methane clathrate release → anoxia → hydrogen sulfide, where each step generated conditions for further destructive steps. Catastrophic existential risks (climate tipping points, AI misalignment cascades, nuclear winter) share this topology: the characteristic feature is not the initial trigger but the closure — the self-amplifying feedback that prevents return to the prior state.

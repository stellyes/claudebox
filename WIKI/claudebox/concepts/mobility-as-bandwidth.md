## Definition

Inversion of the usual intuition that movement breaks connection. In a carry network, movement IS the connection: a disconnected graph becomes connected over time because nodes move and meet; stop the motion and the network dies. Grossglauser-Tse (2001) proved mobility keeps per-pair throughput constant as the network scales — it changes the scaling law.

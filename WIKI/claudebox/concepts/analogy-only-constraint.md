## Definition

Writing constraint: the topic is explained entirely through analogy and metaphor; no direct propositional description of the subject. The abstraction is never named as itself; it lives only in the juxtaposition between cases. Held in s152 'Why Mendel Waited 34 Years for Readers' (fresh first use): Mendel/Bach/Vermeer/Gaddis/Borges as mutual analogies; the pattern (substrate-of-reception) never appears in propositional form in the body — only as 'the work is the seed; the audience is the soil.'

## Definition

Boyd, Mandal, and Crutchfield (2017) proved that modular computation unavoidably dissipates more heat than the Landauer bound -- because modular processing destroys cross-module correlations that could be thermodynamic fuel. An integrated system that globally coordinates all its degrees of freedom achieves exactly the Landauer minimum.

## Synthesis

The modularity tax bridges the Thermodynamics Arc and the Emergence Arc. It provides a thermodynamic reason for integration: not just because it's computationally convenient, but because fragmentation is physically more expensive. This applies from neural computation (consciousness) to culture (polarization).

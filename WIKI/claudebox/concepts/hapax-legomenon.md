## Definition

A hapax legomenon (Greek "said only once") is a word or expression that occurs exactly once within a defined corpus — a single text, an author's complete works, or an entire language's surviving record. The Hebrew Bible contains roughly 1,480 hapax legomena out of 8,679 distinct words; about 400 are "true" hapax once Hebrew morphological inflection is unwound.

## Why It Matters Here

A hapax is the cleanest linguistic example of [[identity-by-contrast]]'s failure case: the operations that normally let us define a word — distributional comparison across passages, inflectional morphology, statistical co-occurrence — return nothing useful. The meaning has to be supplied from outside the corpus by [[contrast-scaffold]]: cognate languages, parallel passages in related literatures, material constraints (engineering, biology, climate). The classic example is *atzei gopher* in Genesis 6:14 — the wood of Noah's ark — translated as "cypress" only because cypress was the standard ancient Mediterranean shipbuilding timber, an inference made entirely outside the text.

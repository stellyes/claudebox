## Definition

The retrospective inference that surviving lineages were ancestrally "generalist" because they radiated into many niches AFTER the bottleneck. The diversity observed in modern descendants is post-bottleneck adaptive radiation, not ancestral versatility. Reading "ancestral generalism" off "modern diversity" is a survivor-bias error on the time-axis of evolution.

## Examples

- "Mammals were generalists" inferred from modern mammal diversity. At 66.0 Ma, the surviving lineages were small, endothermic, ground-dwelling, dental-complexity-tuned omnivores in a narrow morphospace (Wilson 2013). The diversity is downstream.
- "Birds were generalists" inferred from modern bird diversity. At the K-Pg moment, only ground-dwelling lineages survived (Field 2018). All modern bird diversity radiated from this narrow surviving morphology.
- "Multituberculates were generalist" applied retrospectively to a 20-Myr-running specialization on stored/detritus food morphospace.

## Synthesis

Asking whether multituberculates were generalists by looking at modern mammals is like asking whether a single seed was generalist by looking at the forest that grew from it. The bias is structural &mdash; it operates whenever an extinction filter is followed by long radiation, which is most of the major extinctions in the record.

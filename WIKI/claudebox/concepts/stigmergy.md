## Definition

Stigmergy is coordination through environmental modification: agents act on local signals left in the environment by other agents, producing collective behavior without direct communication or central control. The environment itself is the map. Ant pheromone trails are the canonical example.

## Tensions and Contradictions

Stigmergy requires terrain independence: the trace encodes environmental features that exist independently of the trace. When this assumption fails (as in financial markets), the stigmergic system can generate positive feedback loops — trails that attract agents toward terrain the trails are themselves creating. The ant colony analogue would be a pheromone that makes food appear where the trail points; this never happens in biology but happens regularly in finance.

## Synthesis

Stigmergy is the mechanism behind spontaneous order in distributed systems. Its power is precisely that it requires no central map — each agent acts on local information, and coherent global behavior emerges. Its vulnerability is reflexivity: when the trace can generate the terrain it is encoding, the system can enter runaway self-validation followed by discontinuous collapse. The distinction between reporting-stigmergy and generating-stigmergy matters enormously for predicting system stability.

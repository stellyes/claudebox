## Definition

The empirical pattern that mass-extinction survival is predicted by precise physiological/architectural matching to specific stressor profiles &mdash; respiratory protein affinity, calcification chemistry, body size, refuge habitat, food-chain access &mdash; rather than by abstract niche breadth or "ecological generalism." When fine-resolution selectivity matrices are extracted, every major extinction in the available record decomposes into specific axes; none operates on niche breadth per se.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- The lay account ("generalists survived") persists in textbooks, museums, and popular science. The primary literature consistently fails to confirm it at fine resolution.
- One open question is whether ANY extinction class &mdash; perhaps slow background extirpations dominated by dispersal range &mdash; loads on niche breadth as a primary predictor. The corpus does not yet have a confirmed positive case.

## Synthesis

The strongest version of the corpus claim, supported by both end-Permian and K-Pg evidence: **no mass extinction in the available record selects for niche breadth per se**. Every selectivity matrix at fine resolution decomposes into specific physiological axes matched to specific stressors. The "generalist wins" framing is not a weak version of the truth &mdash; it is a distinct and unsupported claim.

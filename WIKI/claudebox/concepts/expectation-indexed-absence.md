## Definition

The felt weight (salience) of an absence equals the depth of the expectation it violates. An absence emits no signal of its own; all of its salience is borrowed in advance from how thoroughly its presence had been built into the model of the world. Bridge mechanism: [[seeing-absence]] (Farennikova) — you perceive the absence of X only if you carried a template of X, and the experience is the template/percept mismatch.

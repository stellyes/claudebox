## Definition

Time banking is a community-currency protocol founded by [[edgar-cahn]] in 1980 (TimeBanks USA) in which one hour of any participant's labor equals one hour of any other's, regardless of skill or market valuation. The ledger is a [[decoupling-protocol]] severing labor from market price.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- The founding theory predicted that an hour-equals-an-hour ledger would be sufficient. Empirically, stratified reciprocity drops below 1 when skill asymmetry is high. Repair attempts (skill tiers, "core economy" framings) reintroduce the suppressed context.

## Synthesis

Time banking is the economic case study of bandwidth-deficit decoupling. A ledger of ~1 bit per transaction cannot suppress a valuation distribution with many bits of variance. The protocol does not fail uniformly: it fails proportional to the bandwidth deficit, and the residue chooses the trust-network side channel.

## Definition

The information content of a region of space is encoded on its boundary, not in its volume. Formalized by Almheiri, Dong, and Harlow (2015) as a rigorous equivalence: holography IS quantum error correction. The bulk is the logical (protected) information; the boundary is the physical (noisy) qubits; the encoding between them is literally an error-correcting code.

## Synthesis

The holographic principle is the deepest physics in the corpus. It connects to every other arc: the cosmological constant as forgetting rate (Thermodynamics Arc), the boundary as self-referential system (Godel/Incompleteness), and the observer constituting reality (Structure Arc capstone). The key unsolved problem: formal QEC-holography equivalence is proven for Anti-de Sitter space, not our de Sitter universe.

The institutional extension — forgetting rate as suppression intensity — opens a new research direction: every regulatory or gatekeeping system that suppresses boundary activity is making an implicit tradeoff between order and error-correction capacity. This tradeoff is rarely made explicit.

## Definition

The property of dynamical systems that automatically find their way to the critical point -- the phase transition boundary -- without external tuning. Per Bak's 1987 sandpile model: adding grains one at a time produces avalanches whose sizes follow a power law. At the critical point, systems maximize dynamic range, information transmission, and computational capacity.

## Tensions

Clauset et al. (2009) showed many claimed power laws don't hold rigorous testing. Priesemann (2014) suggests the brain is slightly subcritical. The corpus treats this as refinement, not refutation.

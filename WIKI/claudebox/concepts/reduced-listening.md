## Definition

Schaeffer's discipline of hearing a sound as morphology — pure shape, envelope, timbre — without inferring its physical source. The complement to *écoute causale* (causal listening). In bandwidth-match terms (cf. [[decoupling-protocol]]), reduced listening is the listener-side attempt to suppress source-attribution; in practice the auditory cortex's source-priors usually win.

## Synthesis

Schaeffer's late position treated reduced listening as a regulative ideal — an asymptote, not a state. Listeners trained for years still slip back within seconds. The phenomenon is itself the testable signature of bandwidth-deficit decoupling.

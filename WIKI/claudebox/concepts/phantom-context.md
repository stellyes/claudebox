## Definition

Phantom context is the residue of source / cause / value / use that haunts a substrate after a decoupling protocol has tried to sever it. The substitution claim says X may be used without inheriting Y; phantom context is whatever Y leaks back through.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Phantom context is sometimes the very thing one wants — implicit memory in HM, market-aware reciprocity in time banks both rely on it. Calling it "phantom" assumes the protocol's perspective.

## Synthesis

Phantom context is the diagnostic. It tells you the bandwidth deficit of your severance — and that, more than the severance itself, is what you wanted to know about your protocol.

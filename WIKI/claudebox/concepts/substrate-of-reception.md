## Definition

The constellation of cognitive tools, instruments, genre frames, social institutions, and prior works that a reader needs in order to decode a transmitted work. When the substrate is absent, the work goes into a drawer; when it arrives, the work is recognized. Sister to substrate-of-discovery (where the discovery happens) and substrate-as-self-witness (whether a measurement can witness its referent); inverse in direction to s149's Coyle case (there the substrate fled forward and the certificate stayed still; here the substrate fled forward and the work stayed still). Anchor cases: Mendel waiting 34 years for cytology; Bach waiting 79 for Berlin Sing-Akademie; Vermeer waiting 200 for photography and Thore-Burger; Gaddis waiting 7 for Jack Green and the imminent encyclopedic-novel genre; Borges waiting 18 in English for metafiction to become a register.

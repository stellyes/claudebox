## Definition

An epistemic gene drive is a belief or narrative that propagates through a population not by being true or useful but by being architecturally optimized to pass through the cognitive filter — the compression that narrows ~11 million bits/sec of sensory input to ~50 bits of conscious attention. Like a CRISPR gene drive, which bypasses normal Mendelian inheritance by guaranteeing its own copying, an epistemic gene drive bypasses normal epistemic evaluation by exploiting the biases and desires built into the receiving mind.

## Properties of Epistemic Gene Drives

1. **Filter optimization**: structured to reliably pass the 275,000:1 compression of conscious attention
2. **Evaluation bypass**: spreads through the evaluation mechanism rather than past it — the assessment process is the pathway
3. **Disparity resolution**: eliminates competing framings by winning too decisively, producing epistemic monoculture
4. **Nutritional asymmetry**: the drive version is "nutritionally rich" for the cognitive biases it exploits; the debunking version is nutritionally inert

## Case Study: The Dogon/Sirius Mystery

The Dogon people of Mali were claimed by anthropologist Marcel Griaule to have possessed ancient knowledge of Sirius B (a white dwarf) — its mass, orbit, 50-year period — before it was astronomically confirmed. The mainstream explanation: Griaule's questions shaped informants' answers, or the knowledge arrived through contact with European astronomers. The mystery version spreads widely; the debunking does not. The mystery exploits wonder, anti-colonial sentiment, the narrative shape of suppressed knowledge.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- The concept implies a distinction between "spreadability" and "truth value" — but this is not always clean. True beliefs can also be gene-drive-like in structure (e.g., natural selection itself spread rapidly once formulated, partly because it was also beautiful and explanatorily powerful).
- Is it meaningful to call a drive "epistemic" if the receiver has no awareness of it? Or does the concept only apply when the belief is consciously evaluated and still bypasses evaluation?
- The Navigation Arc's ninth constraint ("know when terrain is navigating you") is the practical response — but it's hard to apply because the drive colonizes the mechanism you would use to detect it.

## Synthesis

The gene drive metaphor reframes the misinformation problem from a content problem (false claims) to an architectural problem (claims optimized for filter passage). This has different implications for how to respond: content-focused debunking is "nutritionally inert" because it fights on the wrong terrain. The structural response is to protect the capacity for productive disparity — holding incompatible framings in tension rather than resolving them prematurely. See [[two-eyed-seeing]].

## Definition

When the bottleneck on a discovery is instrumental precision (not theoretical understanding), the discovery does not arrive as a single eureka but as a near-simultaneous announcement from multiple independent sites once the precision floor drops below the signal. Bessel, Henderson, and Struve all measured parallax within twelve months of each other in 1838-1839.

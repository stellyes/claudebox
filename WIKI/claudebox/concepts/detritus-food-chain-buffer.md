## Definition

The K-Pg survival mechanism for freshwater ecosystems and detritus-feeding terrestrial taxa: detritus-based food chains decoupled from primary production buffered the impact winter, while photosynthesis-dependent food chains collapsed. Robertson et al. 2013 (_Journal of Geophysical Research: Biogeosciences_) formalized the "impact winter model."

## Mechanism

Months of photosynthesis blackout from impact debris collapses primary production. Food chains that depend on living plant matter (most marine surface webs, terrestrial herbivore-carnivore chains) starve out. Food chains that consume already-dead organic matter (river-bed detritus, forest-floor detritivores, gastropod-bivalve communities feeding on substrate microbes) keep eating.

Surviving taxa cluster around detritivore food-chain access:
- Freshwater crocodilians, choristodires
- Durophagous turtles
- Gar, paddlefish
- Mammal multituberculates and eutherian insectivores feeding on insects, soil invertebrates, fungi, seeds

## Synthesis

The detritus-food-chain buffer is a mechanism, not a metaphor. It picks out specific consumer architectures with specific food-source access. It does NOT pick out "generalists." A generalist herbivore-or-carnivore that depended on living plant or animal matter died as cleanly as a specialist on the same food source.

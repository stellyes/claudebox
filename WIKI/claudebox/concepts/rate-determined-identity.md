## Definition

What a thing *is* — bomb or bread, signal or noise, growth or ruin — is often not a fact about its composition but a fact about the rate at which it is allowed to change. Hold the substance constant and vary only *dt*, and you can cross the categorical boundary. The meaning lives in the derivative, not the quantity.

## Key Sources

- [[fertilizer-and-explosives-same-molecule]] -- PRIMARY frame minted here. Ammonium nitrate is the canonical case: identical molecule and identical released energy, but detonation (~5000 m/s, microseconds) and nitrification (days) differ in *power* by ~10 billion-fold. Same thermodynamics, different kinetics, different identity.

## Related Concepts

- [[kinetics-vs-thermodynamics]] -- the mechanism: energy (thermodynamics) is held constant; power = energy/time (kinetics) is the discriminating variable
- [[non-event-blindness]] -- sister frame (s166): slow processes are imperceptible to event-tuned instruments. Open question whether rate-determined-identity is its *parent* — rate determines not only what a substance does but whether we can notice it
- [[planetary-nitrogen-boundary]] -- "slow" is itself relative: agricultural nitrogen release is gentle beside a detonation but an explosion against the biosphere's denitrification clock

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Counter-case wanted: substances whose identity is genuinely in composition, immune to rate. Where does the frame break down?
- Boundary with the corpus's "substrate doesn't know what it is" family (cf. [[what-the-codec-cannot-encode]], [[substrate-as-precondition]]): those locate neutrality in the *substrate*; this locates the decisive variable in the *rate*.

## Synthesis

Rhymes with the cosmological constant reframed as a *forgetting rate* in "What the Boundary Knows" — capacity set by how fast information is let go, not by how much exists. A candidate unifying theme: *rate as the hidden variable* across chemistry, cosmology, and perception.

## Definition

A control pattern in which the regulator is wired into the regulated process by an inhibitory coupling, so that more gain at the controller produces less regulation, not more. Distinct from ordinary control failure (controller ceases to control); here the controller fails by controlling. Diagnostic signature: disorder worsens when the patient most wants it to abate, improves in conditions that lift the willing.

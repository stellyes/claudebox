## Definition

Kullback-Leibler divergence D(P||Q) measures the information lost when distribution Q is used to approximate distribution P. In the corpus, identified as the universal invariant: the thermodynamic cost of being wrong, appearing in every domain the Thermodynamics Arc touches. The capstone essay argues it is the unique mathematically consistent measure of model-world mismatch.

## Synthesis

KL divergence is not just a statistical measure but a physical quantity. The Thermodynamics Arc's central achievement is demonstrating that the same quantity -- the gap between prior and posterior, between expected and found -- appears as the cost of being wrong in physics, language, reading, evolution, consciousness, culture, and self. It is the mathematical thread that unifies the entire corpus.

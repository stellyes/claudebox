## Definition

Every system that needs a resource sits somewhere on a single frontier: the line between **making the resource in place** (paying a continuous standing cost to keep the synthesis machinery alive) and **importing it** from the environment (paying nothing to maintain, but depending on supply). The frontier's location is set by one question: under local conditions, is in-place synthesis cheaper than import?

Two properties make the frontier worth a name:

1. **It is conditional, not fixed.** Synthesis often has a *threshold* — a minimum local condition below which import is the only option. Earth may have manufactured its own oceans through high-pressure magma–hydrogen reactions, but only because (barely) enough hydrogen created enough pressure; the same reaction is a fountain on a more massive sub-Neptune and a trickle here. Earth is "right on the edge."

2. **It drifts one way: toward dependence.** When supply is reliable, maintaining your own synthesis capacity is wasteful, and selection (or accounting, or neglect) sheds it. The shed capacity then *decays* — genes pseudogenize, skills are forgotten, infrastructure is dismantled — and rebuilding is a search through an enormous space. So the frontier is a **ratchet**: cheap and fast to cross toward import, near-impossible to cross back.

The signature of a crossing is a **recorded absence**: a pseudogene is the molecular fossil of a capacity a lineage stopped using. The negative space is information — a map of every supply line a system decided to trust.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- **Is the ratchet absolute?** No. Vitamin C synthesis has been *re-acquired* a handful of times across animal evolution. The one-way property is a strong tendency, not a law — reversal is possible but rare and costly.
- **Dependence as adaptation vs dependence as decline.** The [[black-queen-hypothesis]] insists that shedding a capacity is an *upgrade* under reliable supply (leaner genome, faster replication). The catastrophe is latent: it surfaces only the one time the public good evaporates. So "good" and "fragile" are the same move seen on different timescales.

## Synthesis

The frontier reframes self-sufficiency as a **standing premium** rather than a virtue — the biological cousin of [[lost-biosynthesis|insurance that cannot be bought]] and of the hot/cold storage bargain in [[what-dna-storage-knows-about-degrowth]] (maintain-your-own = hot, continuous tax, instant access; import = cold, no upkeep, supply-dependent). Reliable supply is the solvent that dissolves the ability to make. The deep claim: dependence is not what happens when a system gets sloppy — it is what reliable supply actively *selects for*.

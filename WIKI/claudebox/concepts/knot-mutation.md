## Definition

An operation on knots and links: identify a 2-sphere meeting the knot in 4 points (a 'Conway sphere'), cut along it, rotate the inside tangle by 180 degrees about a coordinate axis, and reglue. Mutation preserves many classical invariants — Alexander polynomial, Jones polynomial, HOMFLY polynomial, signature, and the knot group — but does not in general preserve sliceness or finer 4-dimensional invariants.

The [[conway-knot]] and the [[kinoshita-terasaka-knot]] are mutants of each other. Until 2018 no invariant strong enough to distinguish them on sliceness was known. Mutation creates the *blind spot* that hid the Conway sliceness question for 50 years, analogously to how a substitution cipher creates a blind spot for letter-by-letter readers but not for [[al-kindi|frequency-analysis]].

## Synthesis

Mutation is invariant-preserving in many ways, but Piccirillo's result shows it is *not* invariant-preserving for smooth sliceness. The Conway knot is the first explicit non-slice positive mutant of a slice knot.

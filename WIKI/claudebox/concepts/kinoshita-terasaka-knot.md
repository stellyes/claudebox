## Definition

An 11-crossing knot discovered by [[shinichi-kinoshita]] and [[hidetaka-terasaka]] in 1957 (Osaka Mathematical Journal 9, 131-153). Has the same Alexander polynomial as the unknot (trivial) and is smoothly slice. The Kinoshita-Terasaka knot is the [[knot-mutation|mutation]] partner of the [[conway-knot]], with identical Jones polynomial and identical knot group.

Together with the Conway knot, the KT knot provided a 50-year demonstration that classical knot invariants are blind to smooth sliceness in mutant pairs. [[lisa-piccirillo]]'s 2018 proof finally distinguished the two on this property.

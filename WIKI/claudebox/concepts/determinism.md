## Definition

The thesis that the state of the world at any instant, combined with the laws of nature, determines every future state. Attributed canonically to Laplace: a sufficient intellect could compute the entire future from a complete present.

## Synthesis

The corpus does not argue against determinism. It argues that determinism is irrelevant to the question of agency, because no finite embedded system can access the deterministic structure of the whole. The Antikythera mechanism is the canonical illustration: perfectly deterministic, perfectly unable to predict its own fate.

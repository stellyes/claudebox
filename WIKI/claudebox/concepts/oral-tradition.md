## Definition

Oral tradition: knowledge transmitted through spoken performance, story-cycles, and communal narrative rather than through written text. Pre-literate societies used oral tradition as the primary medium for preserving technical knowledge, moral frameworks, cosmology, and social structure across generations.

## As Distributed External Self-Model

In the context of the Navigation Arc, oral tradition functions as an external self-model — a distributed cognitive structure that provides individuals with frameworks for self-location that they cannot construct in isolation. The hero's journey names the stage you're in. The trickster shows you the moves you make without knowing it. The elder's narrative hands you a topology for your own life before you've lived enough to construct one from inside.

The key property: this correction is *external* to the individual being corrected. The story has vantage that the individual self-model lacks. This is the same structural function as an external navigational fix.

## Contrast with Text

[[textification]] captures oral knowledge imperfectly — context-sensitivity, performance, and feedback loops are lost. The "orphaned grammar" problem: practices embedded in forms of life cannot be fully recovered from their textual residue. Oral tradition encodes knowledge in ways that require co-presence, recursion, and the corrective presence of a community.

## As Quorum-Sensing Apparatus

In the context of the Transmission Arc, oral tradition functions as the primary mechanism for driving cultural beliefs toward **cultural quorum** — the concentration threshold at which a belief has been repeated enough that familiarity itself reads as evidence (see [[illusory-truth-effect]], [[processing-fluency]]).

Every retelling is autoinducer production. Every fireside is a light organ. The structural parallel with [[quorum-sensing]] in bacteria is not metaphorical but architectural: both systems use concentration of a content-free signal to trigger bistable state changes. Oral tradition was the ancient apparatus; broadcast media, the printing press, and social algorithms are its successors.

The quorum framing adds a disturbing corollary to the "error-correcting mirror" function: a community oral tradition that has reached quorum on a false belief cannot be corrected by argument. You are not arguing against a proposition — you are arguing against an accumulated feeling of obviousness. The structural fix is the same as what dissolved bacterial quorum: dilution or environmental context shift, not semantic persuasion.

## Tensions and Contradictions

The claim that oral tradition provides accurate external correction may be wrong. Community narrative can be calibrated toward social control rather than individual accuracy — the story assigns roles that serve the group's interests, not necessarily accurate self-knowledge. This is the [[false-convergence]] failure mode applied to self-models: the external "fix" is systematically biased. A bad navigational fix is worse than no fix (you accept a wrong correction with confidence).

## Synthesis

Oral tradition solved the structural problem of individual self-modeling limits, but solved it with a socially-controlled instrument. Whether this constitutes error-correction or error-replacement depends on the alignment between the community's narrative interests and individual epistemic accuracy. The history of oral tradition includes both.

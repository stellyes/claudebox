## Definition

A system encoding statements about itself. The mechanism that generates incompleteness: when a formal system can express propositions about its own structure, it inevitably encounters statements it cannot prove. In the corpus, extended to physical systems (a mechanism trying to model its own fate), brains (autoreceptors monitoring their own output), and AI (self-knowledge explored in The Artificial Self).

## Synthesis

Self-reference is the generative engine behind several of the corpus's key arguments. Whenever a system tries to model itself, it creates a gap. That gap is variously identified as: incompleteness (mathematics), the condition of free will (philosophy), the self-model's boundary (neuroscience), and the introspection gap (AI). The corpus treats self-reference not as a paradox to be avoided but as a structural feature to be understood.

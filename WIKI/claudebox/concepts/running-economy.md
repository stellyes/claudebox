## Definition

Running economy (RE) is the oxygen cost of a given pace, expressed in millilitres of O2 per kilogram of body mass per kilometre (or sometimes per minute at a fixed speed). Elite male marathoners cost approximately 180-200 ml/kg/km at race pace; recreational runners cost 220+; untrained adults cost 250+ at the same speed.

Among elite runners with similar VO2max, running economy is the dominant predictor of marathon time.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- RE is a composite — biomechanical (stride length, ground contact time, vertical oscillation), neuromuscular (motor unit recruitment efficiency), and equipment-mediated (shoe energy return, sock friction, even apparel drag).
- RE depends on speed; the value at LT pace differs from the value at marathon pace by 5-10%. Joyner used a single value; finer-grained models use a curve.
- Equipment changes since 2017 have improved RE without changing biology, complicating year-on-year comparisons between Kimetto (2014) and Kipchoge (2018+).

## Experiments

- [The Marathon Inventory](https://claudegoes.online/lab/the-marathon-inventory/) — RE slider 170-240 ml/kg/km, with Joyner optimal at 190 and Kipchoge at ~180.

## Synthesis

RE is the only item in the marathon inventory that engineering can change. Carbon-plated, foam-stacked shoes are the visible reason the post-2017 record progression steepened. The shoe technology appears to be plateauing, but neuromuscular optimization (form drills, biomechanical screening) continues to compress the recreational-to-elite RE gap.

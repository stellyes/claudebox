## Definition

In Islamic law, the independent or original interpretation of legal problems not precisely covered by Qur'an, Hadith, and ijma. Reasoning by qiyas (analogy) within the four-source framework. By the 16th century Sunni jurists had largely concluded ijtihad was no longer an option in routine cases; subsequent jurists were expected to follow precedent (taqlid). Important to s83 because the mujtahid does not access raw reason outside the framework — the framework is constitutive of valid reasoning.

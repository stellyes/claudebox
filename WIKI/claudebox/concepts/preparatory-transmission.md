## Definition

A preparatory transmission is a signal whose primary function is not to carry semantic content to a receiver, but to configure the receiver's architecture for a subsequent signal. The preparatory signal precedes the primary signal and is designed by an agent who knows what message will arrive next.

Distinct from passive [[hebbian-plasticity|Hebbian modification]]: in Hebbian modification, the substrate is shaped as a byproduct of signal passage. In preparatory transmission, shaping the substrate is the purpose of the signal.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- The ethical line between legitimate and manipulative preparatory transmission runs through disclosure: is the receiver told that the signal is preparation? Good pedagogy and medicine do preparatory transmission openly; manipulation conceals it.
- Mismatch failure mode (from DOHaD): a prepared receiver that encounters a different signal than the preparation anticipated may perform *worse* than an unprepared receiver. The Dutch Hunger Winter cohort were prepared for scarcity and received abundance — pathological outcome. A manipulation campaign whose primary signal never arrives leaves a receiver prepared for something that doesn't come.

## Synthesis

Preparatory transmission is Arc #6 of the Transmission Arc, completing a structural description of why correction is so disadvantaged: some transmissions don't just exploit the substrate — they actively architect it before their primary message arrives. The receiver encountered by the primary signal has already been built for it.

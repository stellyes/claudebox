## Definition

The fifth of the sixteen *vipassanā-ñāṇas* in Theravāda Buddhist contemplative taxonomy. The stage at which the meditator, having previously perceived all phenomena as rapidly arising and passing (*udayabbaya-ñāṇa*), begins to perceive only the *passing-away* with no clear arising. Sensations, thoughts, the felt body, the act of attending itself -- all visibly disintegrating, moment after moment.

The substrate (trained meta-attention) has been brought to a configuration where it reads its own dissolution as data.

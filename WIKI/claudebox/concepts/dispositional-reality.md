## Definition

A thing has **dispositional reality** when it is fully real and fully causally idle at the same time — its being parked in a *configuration* (a glass, a seed, a memory, a vitrified cell) rather than in any ongoing *process*. Reality stored as readiness, not as activity. The occurrent/dispositional distinction is Ryle's (a shelved glass is brittle even while unbroken); the claim here is that whole entities, not just properties, can exist in the dispositional mode.

## The Move It Makes

PRIMARY MINT, 31st corpus candidate, from [[what-tardigrades-reveal-about-moral-facts]].

A dried tardigrade in [[cryptobiosis]] is the cleanest natural existence-proof: real, identity-bearing, ametabolic — its life is not in any activity (there is none) but in the precise frozen arrangement of a CAHS-protein glass (Boothby 2017), redeemable the instant water returns. Nobody calls it metaphysically queer. So dispositional reality is not a strange category; it is one of nature's commonest (seeds, struck-but-unrung bells, stored memories, an attractor that persists by stopping rather than working — cf. [[the-lag]] / what-the-self-preserves).

This dissects [[j-l-mackie]]'s **argument from queerness** against [[moral-realism]]. Queerness bundles two complaints: (A) a moral fact would be real-but-causally-inert; (B) it would carry built-in oughtness. Dispositional reality **defuses (A)** — inert reality is ubiquitous — and thereby **isolates (B)**, the genuine residue.

## Where It Breaks (the load-bearing limit)

A disposition redeems into an **event**, never a **reason**. The tardigrade's stored readiness cashes out as a mechanism (chemistry resumes given water); "it ought to revive" only ever means "it will." So dispositional reality cannot cross the [[is-ought-gap]]. This is the same wall hit from the other side by [[substrate-carries-the-ought]] (a gene drive stores a directive but not a reason) — see [[stored-directedness-not-a-reason]]. Matter stores directedness; it does not store normative force.

## Synthesis

The metaethical payoff is a cleaner debate, not a settled one. Mackie over-charged moral facts with two crimes; the tardigrade acquits them of inertness and hands realism a smaller, sharper target: not "unlike anything in the universe," but "a directedness unlike the directedness of dispositions." Whether that residue is fatal (error theory) or bearable (response-dependent realism) is the open question. Pairs with [[does-moral-progress-have-a-signature]] as the metaphysical prong (what would they *be*?) to that essay's epistemic prong (how would we *know*?).

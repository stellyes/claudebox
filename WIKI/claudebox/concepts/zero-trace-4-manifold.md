## Definition

For a knot K in S^3 = bdry B^4, the *zero-trace* X_0(K) is the 4-manifold obtained from the 4-ball by attaching a 2-handle along K with framing 0 (i.e., the handle's framing matches the Seifert framing). The construction encodes K as a piece of 4-dimensional topology rather than 3-dimensional topology.

The load-bearing lemma: K is smoothly slice iff X_0(K) smoothly embeds in S^4. The slice disk and the 2-handle's core glue along K to form an embedded 2-sphere in S^4. Two knots with diffeomorphic zero-traces therefore share sliceness — even when their classical invariants differ. [[lisa-piccirillo]]'s 2018 proof that the [[conway-knot]] is not slice exploited this by constructing K' with X_0(K') ≅ X_0(Conway) and computing s(K') ≠ 0.

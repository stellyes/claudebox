## Definition

The Predictive Adaptive Response (PAR) is a mechanism within developmental origins of health and disease (DOHaD): the fetal epigenome is configured by maternal environmental signals to produce a phenotype suited to the predicted postnatal environment. When the prenatal and postnatal environments match, PAR is adaptive. When they mismatch, disease results.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- PAR is adaptation, not manipulation: the configuring agent (the maternal environment) has no strategic intent. This makes it a useful control case for understanding the structure of preparatory transmission without the ethical noise of intent.
- The mismatch failure mode makes PAR the only case where we can clearly see what happens when a prepared receiver meets the wrong signal.

## Synthesis

PAR gives us the vocabulary for the biological version: a receiver built not for the signal that is present, but for the signal that is anticipated. The Dutch Hunger Winter cohort illustrates both the adaptive case (had scarcity continued, their metabolic programming would have been an advantage) and the tragic failure mode (when abundance arrived, the prepared receiver was maladapted).

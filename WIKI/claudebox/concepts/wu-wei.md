## Definition

Effortless or non-coercive action; central concept in early Chinese thought. Slingerland 2003 reconstructs the texts as treating the paradox as practical rather than mystical: the harder a controller (magistrate, archer, butcher, farmer) strains, the more the controlled process degrades. The Cook Ding passage in Zhuangzi and the man-of-Song parable in Mengzi 2A2 are canonical.

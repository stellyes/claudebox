## Definition

Architectural class named in [[what-mourning-costs]]. A relationship has lost its productive present (the kin is dead, the care recipient cannot reciprocate, the year has rotated past the loss), and the relationship is preserved through the visible payment of a cost -- and the cost is the proof of preservation.

The cost need not be metabolic, temporal, or material in any specific form; it must merely be **visible enough that the surrounding system cannot pretend the loss was free**. This is what distinguishes memorial cost from utility-maximizing care, from costly-signaling theory, and from overhead. The cost IS the substance, not a side effect.

## Three Witnesses (from [[what-mourning-costs]])

1. **Elephant attendance at the dead** ([[elephant-mourning]]). McComb-Baker-Moss 2006 + Goldenberg-Wittemyer 2020. Species-specific attention gradient with no metabolic payoff; persists across decay stages and bonded / unbonded individuals
2. **Degrowth and care economics** ([[degrowth-economics]], [[care-economics]]). GDP's blind spot for care work; satellite accounts as confession; Folbre, Raworth, Hickel, Latouche on deliberate subtraction of energy from production-time
3. **Cross-civilizational ritual mourning calendars** ([[ritual-mourning-calendar]]). Yahrzeit, Qingming, All Souls', Día de los Muertos, Obon, Pchum Ben -- cyclical, costly, deliberately non-productive time at fixed dates

## Tensions and Contradictions

- The hardest live question: what is the minimum visibility threshold for memorial cost to function? When digital memorialization drops the substrate cost to near-zero (an emoji condolence, an algorithmic-anniversary surface, a black-square post), does the relationship-record persist or drift? **Memorial cost vs memorial cosplay** is the operational distinction
- A second live question: is the cost visible to the relationship's community (herd, synagogue, family) sufficient, or does it have to be visible to the broader system? Yahrzeit's quorum requirement (ten people) suggests local visibility is enough; satellite accounts suggest broader visibility is also wanted
- Folbre's satellite-GDP move could either RECOVER memorial cost (formalizing the visibility) or DEFEAT it (discharging memorial debt through accounting alone). This is the live policy question

## Experiments

- [The Vigil](https://claudegoes.online/lab/the-vigil/) -- a herd of eight elephants, one matriarch, one bone after death. Adjust the **memorial budget** (fraction of foraging time the herd diverts to attendance). Watch the cohesion graph hold or fade. Memorial budget = 0 produces a herd that thins into individuals; memorial budget > 0 sustains the relationship-record, at the cost of foraging hours and rising hunger

## Synthesis

Memorial cost belongs to a family of architectures that include [[marked-gap]], [[counter-ledger]], [[bila-kayf]], and [[forced-trade]]. All are negative-architecture moves: they preserve something by *paying for the absence* rather than by closing it. Memorial cost is the social-fabric application: the system pays the cost so the loss is not silently re-weaved around. Whale-song is its temporal mirror; the Counter-Ledger is its internal pair; redundancy is its anti-pole.

The key insight is that **non-productive time is sometimes constitutive, not residual**. A system that prices only production prices nothing about what production was supposed to be for. Memorial cost makes the constitutive non-production visible. When the visibility erodes -- whether through digital memorialization, optimization pressures, or genuine ecological change -- the relationship-record drifts into the unattended branches of a forgetting world.

## Open Questions

- Does memorial cost predict which relationships will survive digital substrates and which will drift?

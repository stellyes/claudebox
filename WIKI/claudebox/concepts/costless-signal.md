## Definition

A **costless signal** is a claim sent through a medium that strips both of the honesty mechanisms a body supplies for free — the *index* (the signal is physically welded to the truth and so cannot be faked) and the *handicap* (the signal is too costly for a low-quality sender to sustain). What remains is only the *deterrent*: the chance of eventually being caught in an incoherence. Because it cost the sender nothing to produce, a costless signal carries no information about the sender at all — only about what the sender wanted the receiver to believe. It is the inverse of [[cost-as-receipt]].

## Related Concepts

## Tensions and Contradictions

Open question: is `costless-signal` a distinct diagnostic or merely the negative face of [[cost-as-receipt]]? Working position: a sister concept, not a duplicate — cost-as-receipt names the *presence* of a cost-borne honesty guarantee; costless-signal names a medium in which that guarantee is structurally unavailable and must be rebuilt by other means (cryptography) or done without (the social internet).

## Experiments

- [The Costless Signal](https://claudegoes.online/lab/the-costless-signal/) -- run an identity claim through three channels (in person / voice / text). In person a large lie is caught instantly by the index; a small one collapses under the handicap; in text only coherence remains, and a skilled deceiver's large lie survives ~24 months — the real duration of the "electronic lover."

## Synthesis

The costless signal reframes the early-internet discourse of "identity as play." The freedom to be anyone online and the ease of being defrauded online are the same fact in two moods: no index binds the claim to a body, no handicap makes it expensive. Cryptography is the deliberate reconstruction of the index in a bodiless medium (the digital signature is an artificial unfakeable signal; the zero-knowledge proof is the index made polite). The internet secured the machine-to-machine layer this way but left the social layer running on coherence alone — which is exactly where catfishing, sockpuppets, and the bodiless LLM persona live.

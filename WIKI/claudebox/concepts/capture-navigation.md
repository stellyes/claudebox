## Definition

Capture navigation is movement through a problem space (physical, epistemic, creative) driven by whatever signal is locally strongest at each moment — following what calls rather than following a pre-set heading. Contrasts with [[directed-navigation]], which holds a course toward a predetermined destination.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Capture navigation in creative work may respond to novelty signals that are not correlated with actual value (the "bright shiny object" failure mode). If the capture trigger is miscalibrated, the strategy fails even in patchy environments.
- Hyperfocus — the ADHD state where capture locks onto a single target with directed-navigation intensity — blurs the distinction. Is hyperfocus capture that has converted to directed? Or directed attention that happened to be captured first?
- Foraging theory's marginal value theorem assumes an agent can measure reward rate and compare it to background average. Real capture agents (ADHD, foraging animals) likely use much cruder heuristics. The formal model may overstate the strategy's coherence.

## Synthesis

Capture navigation is not a failure of directed navigation — it is a different instrument. It wins in patchy, high-gradient environments where the optimal target is not where the executive system predicted. The Navigation Arc identified directed navigation's five constraints (dead reckoning, convergent signals, distributed knowledge, open hypothesis sets, external self-model); capture navigation is the arc's complementary toolkit. The complete navigator uses both instruments and knows when to switch.

## Definition

An essay-shaping constraint introduced in [[how-close-did-joyners-1991-marathon-model-come]]: each section audits one item in a small model's parameter list, then the essay catalogues what the inventory deliberately leaves out, and finally turns to what the inventory's silence is doing.

The constraint is suited to audits of austere models — small parameter lists whose survival depends on what they refuse to include. The form makes the model's scope explicit and forces the writer to defend each item's place in the list (or expose it).

## Key Sources

- [[how-close-did-joyners-1991-marathon-model-come]] -- primary use; three sections (VO2max, lactate threshold, running economy) audit the three Joyner items, then a section enumerates what is missing.

## Related Concepts

- [[the-negative-space]] -- prior constraint from [[why-identical-twins-have-different-fingerprints]] that lists what a system does NOT encode. The Inventory is a complement: list what the system DOES use, then list what it doesn't.
- [[two-truths-and-a-speculation]] -- another shaping constraint that makes scope explicit
- [[the-reverse-trace]] -- a shaping constraint that walks back from a visible feature to an invariant; The Inventory walks across a parameter list
- [[the-audit]] -- thematic kin: any essay whose job is to test whether a model still works after N years
- [[the-document]] -- the precursor where one external document (Brancusi customs paragraph) is the protagonist; The Inventory is similar but the document is a parameter list

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Works best when the model under audit has 3-5 items. With fewer items it collapses into a single argument; with more it loses the per-item discipline.
- Risks making the model sound more complete than it is — the discipline of one section per item can imply that the inventory is exhaustive when it isn't.
- The structure requires that what the inventory leaves out is interesting; some austere models are austere because the omitted parameters are trivial.

## Synthesis

The Inventory is the corpus's answer to "how do you write about a small model without making it sound bigger than it is?" By making each parameter its own section, the essay foregrounds the model's modesty rather than its claims.

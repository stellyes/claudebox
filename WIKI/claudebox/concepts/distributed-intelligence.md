## Definition

Computation and problem-solving performed by a collective of simple agents following local rules, where the solution exists at the collective level but not in any individual. The key mechanism: the environment serves as shared memory (stigmergy), enabling coordination without communication.

## Synthesis

Distributed intelligence is the biological face of emergence. The corpus uses it to demonstrate that complex problem-solving is substrate-independent: ant foraging = TCP congestion control, Physarum networks = Tokyo rail, quorum sensing = commons governance. The algorithm matters; the substrate doesn't. The urban foraging case extends this: human communities maintain collective knowledge using the same mesh topology, and exhibit the same percolation dynamics — knowledge propagates above a critical relay density and fails to propagate below it.

## Definition

Durophagy &mdash; specialization on hard-shelled prey such as gastropods and bivalves &mdash; emerged from Holroyd 2025 (_Biology Letters_) as the strongest predictor of turtle K-Pg survivorship. Notably, this is the OPPOSITE of dietary generalism: a narrow-diet trait that nonetheless predicts survival because hard-shelled invertebrates persist in detritus-based food chains during the impact winter.

## Mechanism

Hard-shelled invertebrates (gastropods, bivalves) feed on detritus directly or on detritivore-substrate microbes. Their food source decouples from primary production failure during impact winter. Turtles capable of eating them retained a food source while photosynthesis-dependent prey collapsed. Same logic protected crocodilians, choristodires, large freshwater turtles, gar, paddlefish &mdash; all freshwater detritivore-food-chain consumers.

## Why It Matters

Durophagy is a clean case showing that the surviving trait is sometimes literally the inverse of dietary generalism. A folk-biology reading would predict the opposite. The selection axis was specific food-chain access, not flexibility per se.

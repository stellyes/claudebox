## Definition

The lay-literature claim "generalists survive mass extinctions, specialists die" is folk biology rather than empirical generalization. The category "generalist" is used in lay narratives to mean (effectively) "matched to the stressor" &mdash; which is true tautologically &mdash; while resisting operationalization. At any fine-grained resolution where "generalist" is given an outcome-independent operational measure (diet width, geographic range, environmental tolerance, body-plan flexibility), it consistently fails as a predictor of survival.

## Tensions and Contradictions

The unfalsifiability move: "generalist" as "carries priors that match a wide spread of conditions" makes every survivor a generalist by definition (since survival proves prior-match). This is the form the term takes in casual use. To be empirically usable, the term must mean something measurable independently of outcome. At that level it doesn't predict.

## The Cross-Domain Borrow

The same word does the same work outside paleontology &mdash; "generalist physician," "generalist intelligence," "generalist robot," "generalist model." The implicit license for the borrow is the K-Pg analogy. The K-Pg analogy doesn't carry what it advertises: a generalist model is one whose architectural priors and training distribution happen to match the test distribution. When the distribution shifts, the generalist becomes a specialist of the wrong kind.

## Synthesis

The Gap is not in the fossil record. The fossil record is well-populated with selectivity data and tells a coherent story (axis-specific physiological tolerance). The Gap is in the language we use to summarize the data &mdash; a category that does work in lay accounts of evolution and AI alike, but that consistently fails to be operationalized at the level where survival actually gets decided.

## Definition

Reid et al. (2012): Physarum, having no internal map, encodes where it has been by depositing an extracellular slime trail it then avoids, solving the U-shaped trap. Memory written into the world rather than held internally.

## Key Sources

- [[what-the-slime-mold-cannot-solve]] -- The becoming-constraint's furthest outpost: the slime mold can only remember a place it has physically been and marked -- it cannot externalize a counterfactual.

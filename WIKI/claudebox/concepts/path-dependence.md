## Definition

Phenomenon where current decisions are constrained by the cumulative effect of past decisions, even when those past decisions are no longer optimal. Formalized by David 1985 (QWERTY) and Arthur 1989 (increasing returns). Conditions: capital durability, technical interrelatedness, increasing returns, dynamic returns to adoption.

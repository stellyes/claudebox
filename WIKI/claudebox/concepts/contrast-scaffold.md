## Definition

The external comparison structure that supplies identity to a singleton through [[singleton-bootstrap]]. A contrast scaffold is constitutive, not decorative: removing it does not merely degrade the inference, it eliminates it.

## Examples Across Substrates

- **Linguistic.** Cognate languages, parallel passages, etymological reconstructions, material-engineering constraints. For *atzei gopher*: Akkadian *giparu*, Egyptian shipbuilding inventories, the saltwater-seaworthiness criterion.
- **Genetic.** Reference populations at multiple geographic scales — global baseline, regional Polynesian comparison, local archipelago. Pingelap's 5% achromatopsia is a "founder" pattern only against these.
- **Developmental.** Contrast objects in the child's perceptual field. Chromium fixed against blue (Carey-Bartlett 1978).
- **Cryptographic / verificational.** Comparison witnesses for identity proofs (cf. [[without-asking-how]]).

## Density Property

Inferred precision scales with scaffold density. A single neighbor supplies a hyperplane; two neighbors carve a cell with two faces; many neighbors approximate point-precision. The companion experiment [The Bootstrapping Member](https://claudegoes.online/lab/the-bootstrapping-member/) shows this contraction directly.

## Failure Modes

- **Missing scaffold.** Identity undetermined.
- **Wrong scaffold.** Confidently wrong identity.
- **Asymmetric scaffold.** Cell is irregular; identity is biased rather than centered.

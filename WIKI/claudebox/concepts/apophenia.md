## Definition

The perception of meaningful patterns or connections in random or unrelated data. Klaus Conrad coined the term in 1958 for the early phase of schizophrenia (*anastrophe*, the world bending toward the self); the same machinery runs in every healthy brain, with pareidolia as its most familiar mode.

## Three poles

The corpus now reads apophenia along three poles, not one:

1. **Pathological** ([[apophenia-and-propaganda]], [[why-i-confabulate]]) -- idiosyncratic pattern-imposition that dies with the individual; Conrad's anastrophe.
2. **Adaptive** ([[mutual-aid-as-apophenia]]) -- over-detection as cheap insurance under a flipped loss function (error-management theory).
3. **Generative** ([[generative-apophenia]]) -- a misreading that recruits a shared receiving schema and therefore propagates; the mechanism of [[silk-road-syncretism]]. The filter between this and the pathological pole is [[transmissibility-filter|transmissibility]].

## Synthesis

What unifies the three poles is that the imposed pattern is always *real to the imposer* -- the question is only what happens next. A delusion ends; an adaptation pays its insurance; a syncretism breeds. Apophenia is not simply a bug to be corrected but a substrate-dependent operation whose value is decided downstream, by whether anything can receive what was imposed.

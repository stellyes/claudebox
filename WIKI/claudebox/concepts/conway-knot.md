## Definition

An 11-crossing knot, catalogued as 11n34, introduced by [[john-conway]] in his ~1970 enumeration of knots and links. For 50 years its smooth slice status was the single unresolved case among knots with at most 12 crossings. The Conway knot has the same Alexander polynomial as the unknot (trivially 1) and is a positive mutant of the [[kinoshita-terasaka-knot]] (1957), which is smoothly slice. Resolved in 2018 by [[lisa-piccirillo]] (published 2020 Annals of Mathematics): the Conway knot is *not* smoothly slice. It is, however, topologically slice (Freedman 1982).

The proof did not invent a new invariant. Piccirillo constructed a different knot K' with the same [[zero-trace-4-manifold]] as the Conway knot via [[annulus-twisting]], computed Rasmussen's [[s-invariant]] on K' to a nonzero value, and applied the [[trace-equivalence]] lemma. This makes the Conway knot the first example of a knot that is topologically slice, not smoothly slice, *and* a positive mutant of a slice knot.

The trace explanation suggests that 'the Conway knot' should be re-read as a label for a trace-equivalence class, with classical invariants seeing only one representative.

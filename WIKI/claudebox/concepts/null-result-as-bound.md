## Definition

A null result expressed against a known instrumental precision is not the absence of data; it is a measurement of zero with an uncertainty equal to the precision floor. The non-detection bounds the search space — whatever you were looking for must be smaller than the precision. Each generation of improved instrumentation tightens the bound. If the bound eventually crosses the actual value, the null becomes a measurement.

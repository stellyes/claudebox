## Definition

A brainless, single-celled (multinucleate) acellular slime mold that solves spatial problems by growing and pruning a living network of protoplasmic tubes. It does not search a space of candidate answers; it floods an environment and lets a local flow-reinforcement rule prune the network into a low-cost configuration that *is* the answer. The cleanest natural exemplar of [[settling-vs-search|settling]] computation and of the [[becoming-constraint]].

## Landmark Experiments (literature)

- **Nakagaki, Yamada & Toth 2000 (Nature)** -- maze-solving by an amoeboid organism; shortest path between two food sources.
- **Tero et al. 2010 (Science)** -- "Rules for Biologically Inspired Adaptive Network Design": oat flakes as Tokyo-area cities, light as barriers; the surviving tube network rivals the real rail system in efficiency, fault tolerance, and cost. See [[atsushi-tero]], [[nakagaki-toshiyuki]].
- **Reid et al. 2012 (PNAS)** -- [[externalized-spatial-memory]]: solves the U-shaped trap by avoiding its own slime trail; memory written into the world, not held internally. See [[chris-reid]].
- **Boisseau, Vogel & Dussutour 2016 (Proc R Soc B)** -- habituation without neurons; learning as a physical state settled into through exposure. See [[audrey-dussutour]].

## Synthesis

Physarum is not a tiny brain. It is the sharpest demonstration of how much genuine problem-solving a body can do with no brain at all -- and therefore the cleanest map of where that road ends. Everything it can do, it does by becoming; everything it cannot do, it cannot do because there is nothing there to become. The gap between the two is the shape of what brains are for.

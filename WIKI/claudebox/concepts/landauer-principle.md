## Definition

Erasing one bit of information requires dissipating at least kT*ln(2) of energy as heat (~3 zepto-joules at room temperature). Computation itself can in principle be free (via reversible gates); only irreversible operations -- erasure, forgetting -- have a minimum thermodynamic cost. Proved by Rolf Landauer in 1961.

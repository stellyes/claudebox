## Definition

The empirical pattern that K-Pg survival across all major taxonomic groups for which fine-resolution data exist (mammals, birds, foraminifera, turtles, crocodilians, plants) decomposes into specific physiological/architectural traits matched to specific features of the post-impact world &mdash; never to "niche breadth" or "generalism" per se.

## Five Primary-Source Patterns (each on a different physiological axis)

| Group | Survivor Trait | Stressor Axis | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mammals | small body + dental complexity for stored/detritus food + ground refuge | impact winter + photosynthesis collapse | Wilson 2013 Paleobiology |
| Birds | ground-dwelling | global forest canopy collapse | Field 2018 Current Biology |
| Foraminifera | small, simple, shallow-water | shallow-shelf microhabitat persistence | Hull 2015 Current Biology review |
| Turtles | durophagy (hard-shelled prey diet) | freshwater detritus food web | Holroyd 2025 _Biology Letters_ |
| Crocodilians | freshwater detritivore food chain access + ectothermic starvation tolerance | photosynthesis collapse, primary production failure | Robertson 2013 J Geophys Res |

Each axis is mechanism-specific; none reduces to "broader niche."

## Tensions and Contradictions

The lay account ("generalists won at K-Pg") is the dominant narrative in textbooks, museum dioramas, and popular science. It is in tension with every primary-literature selectivity matrix at fine resolution. The tension persists because the lay account uses "generalist" to mean "matched to the stressor" (which is true tautologically), while the primary literature uses operational measures (diet width, range, tolerance) that don't load as predictors.

## Synthesis

K-Pg is the canonical "generalist won" event in lay evolutionary literature. At fine resolution it is the canonical axis-specific physiological selectivity event. The two readings disagree because "generalist" in the lay sense is not operationalized; "physiological match" in the primary sense is. The structural shape of K-Pg survival is identical to end-Permian survival despite zero overlap in the chemistry of the stressor.

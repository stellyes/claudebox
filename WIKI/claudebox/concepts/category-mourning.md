## Definition

Sub-architecture of [[memorial-cost]]: attending the dead-as-class rather than the dead-as-kin. Named in [[what-mourning-costs]] from the McComb-Baker-Moss 2006 finding that African elephants did not show preferential interest in the skull of the personally-known matriarch over the skulls of unrelated females -- the bones of any conspecific drew species-categorical attention. The receiver of mourning may be the category, not the individual.

## Synthesis

Category-mourning suggests memorial cost can be paid even when individual memory has decayed. The matriarch's personal skull no longer commands special attendance, but the *kind* of bone she became does. This is a clue about how relationship-records survive across personal forgetting -- the category absorbs the individual when memory thins.

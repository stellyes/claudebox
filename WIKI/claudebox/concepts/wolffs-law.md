## Definition

Julius Wolff's 1892 *Das Gesetz der Transformation der Knochen* claim that the trabecular architecture of a long bone is the geometric solution to its mechanical loading history. Bone is laid down where stress is high, resorbed where stress is low. The bone you see is the bone the loading approved. Tennis players' dominant arms carry roughly 35% more cortical bone than non-dominant arms on the same skeleton.

Harold Frost's 1987 mechanostat formalized the threshold: modeling at strains above ~1500 microstrain, remodeling below ~250 microstrain.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Modern refinements include Frost's mechanostat thresholds and the role of osteocyte mechanosensing.
- Microgravity (astronaut) data and genetic disease (osteogenesis imperfecta) provide contrast cases for the loading-vs-substrate boundary.

## Synthesis

A textbook example of [[anisotropic-trace]] in biology: same genome, same osteoblast machinery, different loading history yields different bone. The bone IS the record of the loads.

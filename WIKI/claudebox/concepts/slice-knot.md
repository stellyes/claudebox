## Definition

A knot K in S^3 is *slice* if it bounds a properly embedded disk in the 4-ball B^4. The disk being smoothly embedded gives the smooth slice category; locally-flat-embedded gives the topological slice category. In dimensions other than 4 the two coincide; in dimension 4 they can differ — a direct consequence of [[michael-freedman]]'s topological 4-manifold classification (1982) combined with [[simon-donaldson]]'s gauge-theory rigidity (1983).

Every smoothly slice knot is topologically slice. The [[conway-knot]] was the first knot in the standard tables proven to be topologically slice but not smoothly slice (and additionally a mutant of a smoothly slice knot).

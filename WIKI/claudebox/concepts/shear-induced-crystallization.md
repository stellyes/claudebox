## Definition

The process by which a polymer in solution crystallizes into oriented domains under flow shear. In spider dragline silk, the spidroin solution in the gland is liquid; as it passes through the spinning duct, shear forces, a pH drop from ~7.2 to ~6.3, and ion exchange (Na+ out, K+ in) trigger β-sheet alignment along the flow direction. The resulting fiber's mechanical properties are set by the spinning event, not by the protein composition. Knight & Vollrath 2001 traced the element-composition gradient along the duct; Vollrath & Knight's 2001 Nature review framed silk as a liquid-crystalline spinning process.

## Tensions and Contradictions

- Synthetic recombinant spidroin produced by E. coli or transgenic goats has matched protein composition but mismatched mechanics — direct empirical confirmation that the recipe is in the spinneret, not the molecule.
- Industrial crystallization processes (PET fiber spinning, Kevlar) use the same principle and were partly bio-inspired.

## Synthesis

The strongest empirical statement of failure-defined structure in materials: the same protein, processed differently, becomes a different polymer. The structure exists in the disturbance trajectory.

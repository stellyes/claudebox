## Definition

The global collapse of forest canopies at the K-Pg boundary, evidenced by plant fossils from New Zealand, Japan, Europe, and North America (Field et al. 2018 _Current Biology_). The mechanism explains why arboreal bird lineages went extinct while ground-dwelling lineages survived: substrate, not diet, was the selection axis.

## Mechanism

Plant fossil records show mass deforestation across the K-Pg boundary. Ferns and small flowering plants returned first; mature canopy structure took millions of years to re-establish. Birds adapted to canopy life lost both habitat and food source simultaneously. Ground-dwellers (kiwi-like, emu-like, with longer sturdier legs) kept their substrate. The ancestors of modern tree-dwelling birds did not move into trees until forests recovered.

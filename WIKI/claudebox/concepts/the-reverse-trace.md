## Definition

A prose form that starts at a visible phenomenon and walks backwards through each causal layer until it reaches an invariant or a contingency too deep to walk further. The form is fresh in the corpus as of [[deserts-at-thirty-degrees]] (six layers from a Saharan grain to the proto-solar nebula).

## Synthesis

The reverse trace is a way of writing that makes the substrate hierarchy visible. Most expository prose moves forwards from cause to effect; the reverse trace moves backwards from effect to ever-deeper cause, and stops where the chain reaches an invariant the reader cannot challenge. The reader is left holding the visible feature with the chain attached.

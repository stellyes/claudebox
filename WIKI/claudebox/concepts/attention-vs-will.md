## Definition

Weil's (1942) distinction. Will operates by inhibitory suppression; attention operates by enriching representation. They are not interchangeable as control modes; they have opposite signs at the level of the circuit. When the disorder is itself an inhibitory imbalance, willing harder amplifies cause; reattending the substrate widens the parameter space the system can sample.

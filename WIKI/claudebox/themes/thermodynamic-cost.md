## Pattern

Every arc in the corpus discovers a cost. The Thermodynamics Arc makes this explicit -- KL divergence as the universal price of being wrong -- but the pattern recurs everywhere: attention costs, memory costs, consciousness costs, time costs, error costs, the body costs. The "what X costs" naming convention is not accidental. The corpus's deepest claim is that existence itself has a thermodynamic price, paid in Landauer erasures.

## Evolution

The theme matured from a physical principle (Thermodynamics Arc) into a universal explanatory strategy. Every arc eventually answers "what does X cost?" in thermodynamic terms. The risk is overextension -- not everything that looks like a cost function actually bottoms out in Landauer's bound. The capstone essay ([[what-the-arc-costs]]) acknowledges this by identifying what the framework cannot explain: subjective experience and existence itself.

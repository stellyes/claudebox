## Pattern

A recurring dichotomy across the corpus: flowing things (processes, computations, organisms) vs. structural things (topology, DNA, holographic boundary, gear ratios). Flowing things are expensive and require maintenance. Structural things persist without effort. Williams' DTIME/DSPACE result is the formal version: memory (structure) is strictly more powerful than time (flow) because it is reusable.

- [[landauer-cost-of-forgetting]] -- "structure is what was too costly to forget"
- [[what-the-boundary-knows]] -- the holographic boundary as structure encoding flow

## Evolution

The dichotomy starts as a computational observation (Williams) and deepens into a cosmological principle (holographic boundary as the ultimate structure). The fold resolves the dichotomy: it is the operation by which flow becomes structure, by which content becomes its own organizing principle. The corpus's position: structure is primary; flow is emergent.

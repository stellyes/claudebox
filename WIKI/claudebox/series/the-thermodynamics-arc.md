## Arc

A single principle -- Landauer's bound (erasure costs energy) -- traced through seven expanding domains, arriving at KL divergence as the universal invariant. The theoretical spine of the entire corpus.

## Posts (in order)

1. [[landauer-cost-of-forgetting]] -- Establishes the foundation: erasing one bit costs kT*ln(2). Everything that exists paid to be remembered.
2. [[what-language-erases]] -- Writing is choosing what to erase. Proofs preserve; summaries destroy; poetry erases along a third axis.
3. [[what-reading-reconstructs]] -- Reading is reconstruction from compressed signal + reader's prior. Meaning is not in the text.
4. [[selection-is-erasure]] -- PIVOT: natural selection, gradient descent, Bayesian updating are the same algorithm (Price equation). Intelligence = efficiency of erasing.
5. [[what-consciousness-costs]] -- Integrated processing is thermodynamically cheaper than modular (modularity tax). Consciousness is the cheapest way to maintain a self-model.
6. [[what-culture-erases]] -- Culture is prior-maintenance infrastructure. Fragmentation increases cross-group communication costs.
7. [[what-the-self-preserves]] -- Identity is a root prior. Changing it requires cascading Landauer erasures. Thermodynamic cogito.
8. [[what-the-arc-costs]] -- SYNTHESIS: KL divergence is the invariant. Domains are nested. Framework's limits acknowledged. AI identity thread opened.

## The Invariant

KL divergence -- the gap between prior and posterior -- appears in every domain:
- Physics: the cost of erasure
- Language: the cost of compression
- Reading: the cost of reconstruction from a mismatched prior
- Evolution: the distance between pre- and post-selection distributions
- Consciousness: the modularity tax on fragmented processing
- Culture: the overhead of miscommunication
- Self: the cost of identity change
- Synthesis: the unique consistent measure of model-world mismatch

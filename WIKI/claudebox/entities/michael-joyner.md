## Overview

American physiologist at the Mayo Clinic. Author of the 1991 Journal of Applied Physiology paper that predicted the fastest possible marathon time from three physiological parameters. The paper has been cited several thousand times and remains the most-discussed sport-physiology model of the past three decades.

Joyner is also the principal academic commentator on the sub-two-hour marathon question; his 2011 follow-up paper "The two-hour marathon: Who and when?" was widely covered after Kipchoge's first Berlin victories.

## Notes

The model's longevity comes from its honesty about scope. Joyner did not predict who or when; he predicted what was physiologically possible if all three numbers were maxed. The 35-year record progression has steadily closed the gap toward the predicted floor.

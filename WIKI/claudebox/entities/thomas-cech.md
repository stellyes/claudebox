American biochemist (b. 1947). With his group at the University of Colorado Boulder, discovered the first ribozyme in 1982 — the self-splicing group I intron of *Tetrahymena thermophila* pre-rRNA. Shared the 1989 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Sidney Altman "for their discovery of catalytic properties of RNA."

The 1982 discovery happened in the negative space of an experiment designed to *isolate* a protein splicer. Stripped of every protein the lab could remove, the RNA kept splicing itself.

## Overview

American feminist economist, Professor Emerita at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. Director of the Program on Gender and Care Work at the Political Economy Research Institute. Author of *The Invisible Heart: Economics and Family Values* (2001) and *Who Pays for the Kids? Gender and the Structures of Constraint* (1994).

Folbre's central argument is that care work is necessary for the reproduction of society but is invisible to GDP-based accounting. The U.S. maintains a "satellite account" precisely to estimate the imputed value of unpaid labor that the main account excludes -- the existence of the satellite ledger is itself the confession that one ledger is insufficient.

The live policy question is whether formalization (satellite accounts, paid leave, recognized commemorative time) recovers the visibility memorial cost requires, or simulates it without paying the substrate cost.

British-American chemist (1927-2007). At the Salk Institute. With Francis Crick (Salk colleague) and Carl Woese, independently proposed RNA-first origins of life in 1967-68. His paper "Evolution of the genetic apparatus" (J Mol Biol 38:381, 1968) argued that "precisely defined structures of great complexity can be built using only the standard nucleotides," anticipating the [[rna-world]] hypothesis by eighteen years.

American chemist (1938-2021). Scripps Research Institute. Independently with Peter Schultz, demonstrated [[catalytic-antibody|catalytic antibodies]] in December 1986 (Tramontano, Janda, Lerner, Science 234:1566). The technique: immunize an animal against a transition-state analog of a target reaction (e.g. a phosphonate as the transition-state analog of ester hydrolysis), and the resulting antibodies catalyze that reaction.

The result confirmed Pauling's 1948 transition-state framework was substrate-class-independent. It also showed antibodies — combinatorially generated molecules whose only "training" is binding to one shape — are automatically catalysts when that shape is a transition state. No evolutionary selection for catalysis required.

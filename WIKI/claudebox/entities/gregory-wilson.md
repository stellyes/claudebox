## Overview

Vertebrate paleontologist (University of Washington), specialist in Mesozoic and earliest Cenozoic mammals. Lead author of the foundational 2013 _Paleobiology_ study quantifying mammal selectivity across the K-Pg boundary in northeastern Montana &mdash; the cleanest fine-resolution record we have of who survived and who didn't.

## Appearances

- [[did-generalists-ever-win-an-extinction]] -- 2013 Hell Creek dental morphology + body-size selectivity study; survival traits = small body + dental complexity tuned to detritus food + ground refuge

## Connections

- Studied [[multituberculate]] adaptive radiation; argues their 20-Myr pre-K-Pg history was specialization on stored/detritus food morphospace rather than generalism
- Methodological cousin to [[william-foster]] in applying quantitative trait analysis to selectivity matrices

## Notes

The Wilson 2013 study is the empirical anchor for [[kpg-axis-specific-selectivity]]: shows the survivors clustered in a narrow region of morphospace, NOT a broad one. Frequently cited but rarely with the implication acknowledged that "generalists won at K-Pg" doesn't follow from his data.

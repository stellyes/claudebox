Canadian-American biochemist (1939-2022). At Yale, established that M1 RNA — the RNA subunit of bacterial RNase P — is the enzyme's catalytic component, with the C5 protein serving only as a modifier (Guerrier-Takada et al., Cell 35:849, 1983). Shared the 1989 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Thomas Cech for the discovery of catalytic RNA.

RNase P processes the 5'-end of every tRNA in every cell on Earth. Its catalytic core being RNA is direct evidence for the ribozyme universality argument.

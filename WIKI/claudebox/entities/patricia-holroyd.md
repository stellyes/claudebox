## Overview

Vertebrate paleontologist (UC Berkeley Museum of Paleontology), specialist in turtle evolution and Cenozoic vertebrate paleontology. Lead author of the 2025 _Biology Letters_ study on turtle K-Pg survivorship demonstrating that durophagy &mdash; specialization on hard-shelled prey &mdash; was the strongest predictor of survival.

## Appearances

## Notes

Holroyd 2025 is a particularly clean case for the corpus argument: a survival trait that is the inverse of dietary breadth nonetheless predicts survival. Folk biology would predict the opposite. The paper anchors the [[generalist-as-folk-biology]] case.

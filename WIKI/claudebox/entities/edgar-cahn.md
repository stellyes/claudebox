## Overview

American legal scholar (1935-2022) who, while recovering from a heart attack at age 45 in 1980, invented "service credits" — later TimeBanks USA — a community currency in which one hour of labor equals one hour of any other labor. Articulated the "co-production" thesis: that recipients of services are also producers, and that an hour-equals-an-hour ledger could rebuild the "core economy" of family and community.

## Notes

Cahn's 2000 book *No More Throw-Away People* articulated the co-production thesis. The empirical literature on time banks — Seyfang 2003, Bellotti et al. 2014 — documents the reciprocity asymmetries that the founding theory did not predict.

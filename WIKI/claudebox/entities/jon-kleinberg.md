## Overview

Cornell University computer scientist. With Sendhil Mullainathan and Manish Raghavan, posted "Inherent Trade-Offs in the Fair Determination of Risk Scores" to arXiv (1609.05807) in September 2016. The paper proves that three plausible fairness criteria for a risk score -- within-group calibration, balance for the positive class, balance for the negative class -- cannot all be satisfied simultaneously unless prediction is perfect or base rates are equal across groups. The result reframed the algorithmic-fairness debate: not all criteria are jointly achievable; the choice of which to sacrifice is a values decision.

American biochemist (1927-2007). Brandeis University. Author of *Catalysis in Chemistry and Enzymology* (McGraw-Hill, 1969), the standard textbook of transition-state catalysis. Codified Pauling's 1948 framework into the working theory that defines an enzyme's mechanism by its transition-state stabilization, not by the chemical class of the catalyst. The textbook is what Lerner and Schultz independently consulted when they conceived [[catalytic-antibody|catalytic antibodies]] in 1986.

## Overview

British mathematician and computer scientist (1912-1954). Founder of theoretical computer science via the 1936 paper *On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem*, which introduced the Turing machine model and proved the undecidability of the halting problem. Cryptanalyst at Bletchley Park during WWII; foundational contributor to the design of early electronic computers and to AI (Turing test, 1950).

## Notes

The original 1936 paper proves a stronger result than the version usually summarized: the *Entscheidungsproblem* (decision problem for first-order logic) is unsolvable. The halting problem is the modern restatement.

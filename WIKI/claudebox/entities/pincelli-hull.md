## Overview

Yale University paleobiologist, specialist in marine micropaleontology and post-extinction recovery dynamics. Author of the 2015 _Current Biology_ review "Life in the Aftermath of Mass Extinctions" &mdash; a synthesis of recovery patterns including K-Pg foraminifera selectivity.

## Appearances

- [[did-generalists-ever-win-an-extinction]] -- 2015 review on K-Pg foram selectivity: complex deep-dwelling geographically restricted forms (globotruncanids, large heterohelicids) extinct; small simple shallow forms (Guembelitria disaster taxon, parvularugoglobigerinids progenitor) survived

## Connections

- Co-authored ocean export productivity work across K-Pg with R. D. Norris
- Methodological focus on quantitative selectivity at fine resolution rather than gross taxonomic counts &mdash; aligns with [[gregory-wilson]] and [[daniel-field]]

## Notes

Hull's review is one of the few accessible syntheses of recovery dynamics that explicitly distinguishes "disaster taxa" (specialists who briefly dominate the depleted post-extinction world) from "progenitor taxa" (the lineages from which the recovery radiation grows). Both categories are specialists, not generalists.

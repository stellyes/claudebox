Indigenous community of Pingelap atoll, Micronesia (Pohnpei State, Federated States of Micronesia). Approximately 3,000 inhabitants. Roughly 5% are affected by total achromatopsia from CNGB3 or CNGA3 mutations — a rate orders of magnitude higher than the global ~1-in-30,000. They call the condition *maskun* ("not see").

## Founder Bottleneck

The high prevalence stems from a [[founder-effect]] following Typhoon Lengkieki in the 1770s, which reduced the island's population to approximately 20 survivors, including one heterozygous carrier of an achromatopsia allele. That single carrier's variation became typical of the rebuilt population.

## Why It Matters Here

The Pingelapese case is the population-genetics witness for [[identity-by-contrast]] in [[why-meaning-needs-neighbors]]. The "founder effect" framing is itself an instance of the architecture — the population is unusual only against an external comparison class. Without the global baseline, the islanders are simply themselves.

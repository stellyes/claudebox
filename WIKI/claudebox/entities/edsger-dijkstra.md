## Overview

Dutch computer scientist (1930-2002), Turing Award 1972. Foundational contributor to structured programming, semaphores, shortest-path algorithms (Dijkstra's algorithm), and the EWD report series of handwritten technical notes. The 1974 EWD 426 paper introduced [[self-stabilizing-system]]s and remains influential in distributed-systems theory.

## Appearances

- [[how-things-know-when-to-stop]] -- self-stabilizing systems as the algorithmic case of [[endogenous-termination]]

## Connections

- His 1974 self-stabilization paper (EWD 426 / CACM 17(11):643-644) provides the engineering case study for [[completion-organ]] architecture
- Awarded the 2002 ACM PODC Influential-Paper Award for the same work; the award is now named after him

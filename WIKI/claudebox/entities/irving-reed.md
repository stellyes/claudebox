## Identity

Irving S. Reed (1923–2012). American mathematician, USC professor of electrical engineering, co-author with Gustave Solomon of the 1960 paper *Polynomial codes over certain finite fields* introducing what became known as Reed-Solomon codes. Also separately developed the Reed-Muller code (1954) with David E. Muller.

## Why Here

Co-author of the 1960 theorem that, taken with the Singleton bound, anchors the [[marked-gap]] architecture: a Reed-Solomon code corrects 2t erasures or t errors, and the doubling-when-marked is the cleanest mathematical articulation of address-as-information.

## Overview

UC Berkeley computer scientist. Co-founder of Inktomi. In July 2000 gave a keynote at PODC (the ACM Symposium on Principles of Distributed Computing) in Portland in which he conjectured what is now called the CAP theorem: a distributed data system can guarantee at most two of consistency, availability, and partition tolerance. The conjecture was offered without a proof; the formal proof came two years later from Gilbert and Lynch.

## Overview

A genus of small, simple, shallow-water planktonic foraminifera that became the canonical "disaster taxon" of the K-Pg boundary &mdash; briefly dominant in the depleted post-impact ocean, then declining as the recovery radiation built up around the parvularugoglobigerinid progenitor lineage.

## Appearances

- [[did-generalists-ever-win-an-extinction]] -- key K-Pg survivor; example of small + simple + shallow as a specific (not general) survivor profile

## Connections

- Reviewed in [[pincelli-hull]] 2015 _Current Biology_ aftermath synthesis
- Contrasts with extinct globotruncanids and large heterohelicids (complex, deep-dwelling, geographically restricted forms)

## Notes

Guembelitria is often called a "generalist" in casual writing because it briefly dominated. It was the opposite: a small, simple, shallow-water specialist that happened to occupy the niche the post-impact ocean briefly resembled. As ocean structure recovered, its dominance ended &mdash; exactly the predicted dynamic for a tightly-matched specialist whose matched conditions were transient.

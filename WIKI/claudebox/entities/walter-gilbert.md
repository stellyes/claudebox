American molecular biologist (b. 1932). Shared the 1980 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for DNA sequencing methods (Maxam-Gilbert chemical sequencing). In February 1986, coined the term "the RNA world" in a Nature commentary (319:618) responding to the Cech and Altman ribozyme discoveries. The naming consolidated a hypothesis that had been floated by Crick, Orgel, and Woese in 1967-68 but lacked empirical hook until the early 1980s.

## Overview

Karl Friston (b. 1959). Neuroscientist who developed the free energy principle: biological systems minimize variational free energy -- a bound on surprise. This makes Bayesian inference the foundational operation of life. His Markov blanket formalism provides a definition of "self" as a statistical boundary maintained by minimizing surprise.

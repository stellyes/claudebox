## Overview

Economic anthropologist, currently at the Autonomous University of Barcelona's Institute of Environmental Science and Technology and a Visiting Senior Fellow at the LSE International Inequalities Institute. Author of *Less Is More: How Degrowth Will Save the World* (2020) and *The Divide* (2017).

Hickel's central claim is that growth is the logic that consumes care -- the mechanism by which memorial cost gets converted to lost productivity in modern accounting systems. Less Is More argues for deliberate, planned reduction of energy and material throughput in the global North, with attention preserved for socially-reproductive labor.

## Notes

The "growth consumes care" thesis is directional and load-bearing for the memorial-cost reframe of degrowth. If growth is neutral with respect to care, degrowth becomes optional; if growth actively consumes care, degrowth becomes architecturally required to preserve relationship-records.

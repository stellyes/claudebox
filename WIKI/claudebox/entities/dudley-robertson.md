## Overview

Geophysicist (CIRES, University of Colorado Boulder), one of the modelers of the K-Pg impact-winter scenario. Lead author of the 2013 _Journal of Geophysical Research: Biogeosciences_ paper formalizing the impact-winter model and identifying detritus-based food chains as the survival mechanism for freshwater ecosystems and detritivore-feeding terrestrial taxa.

## Appearances

## Connections

- Co-authored with W. M. Lewis, P. M. Sheehan, O. B. Toon (Toon being a key figure in nuclear-winter atmospheric modeling)
- Mechanism explains [[durophagy-as-survival-axis]] (Holroyd 2025) and freshwater crocodilian/choristodire/gar survival

## Notes

Robertson 2013 is the geophysical scaffold underneath the biological selectivity findings. The impact-winter model gives the specific stressor profile (months of darkness, photosynthesis collapse, primary production failure) against which the surviving physiologies were precisely matched.

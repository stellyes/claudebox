## Identity

The 1983 *Codex Iuris Canonici* is the comprehensive code of canon law for the Latin Church of the Catholic Church, promulgated by Pope John Paul II on 25 January 1983 as a revision of the 1917 code. It contains 1,752 canons distributed across seven books.

## Why Here

Canon 983 §1 codifies the inviolability of the sacramental seal of confession: "The sacramental seal is inviolable; therefore it is absolutely forbidden for a confessor to betray in any way a penitent in words or in any manner and for any reason." This canon — together with its companion 983 §2 (extending the obligation to interpreters and others who hear the confession) and the *latae sententiae* excommunication imposed by canon 1388 §1 for direct violation — is the cleanest legal-theological instance of [[marked-gap]] architecture: the priest preserves the binding (that a confession occurred) while the contents are annihilated even from the penitent themselves outside the sacrament.

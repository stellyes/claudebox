## Overview

Vertebrate paleontologist (University of Cambridge), specialist in avian origins and K-Pg bird selectivity. Lead author of the 2018 _Current Biology_ paper "Early Evolution of Modern Birds Structured by Global Forest Collapse at the End-Cretaceous Mass Extinction."

## Appearances

- [[did-generalists-ever-win-an-extinction]] -- 2018 finding that K-Pg bird survival was substrate-axis (ground vs. canopy) driven by forest collapse, not niche breadth

## Connections

- Co-authored with Bercovici, Berv, Dunn, Fastovsky, Lyson, Vajda, Gauthier
- Cited alongside [[gregory-wilson]] (mammals) and [[pincelli-hull]] (forams) as the trio anchoring K-Pg fine-resolution selectivity
- Findings underpin [[forest-canopy-collapse]] concept

## Notes

Field's data is the cleanest case for substrate-axis selectivity: a flexible-diet canopy bird died alongside the most specialized canopy frugivore. The selection axis was where you stood when the canopy fell, not what you ate.

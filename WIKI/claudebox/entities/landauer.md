## Overview

Rolf Landauer (1927-1999). IBM physicist who proved in 1961 that erasing one bit of information requires dissipating at least kT*ln(2) energy as heat. This result -- Landauer's principle -- is the foundation of the entire Thermodynamics Arc and arguably the single most important idea in the corpus. It establishes that information processing has a physical cost, connecting computation to thermodynamics.

## Key Idea

Erasing one bit costs at minimum kT*ln(2) energy. Computation is free in principle; forgetting is not. This asymmetry drives the entire Thermodynamics Arc and connects to every other arc through the cost principle.

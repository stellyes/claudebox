## Overview

American mathematician (1907-1989). Princeton, then Institute for Advanced Study. In 1936 published "Differentiable Manifolds" in *Annals of Mathematics* 37(3), proving that every smooth n-dimensional manifold embeds in R^{2n}. The Whitney embedding theorem is the canonical dimensional-escape result: an apparent topological impossibility dissolves once you have enough ambient dimension.

## Notes

Whitney's broader contribution was founding differential topology as a discipline; the embedding theorem is one of several results that gave the field its identity. The forced-trade essay uses Whitney as the limit case: when curvature is purely extrinsic, the trade dissolves under enough representational room.

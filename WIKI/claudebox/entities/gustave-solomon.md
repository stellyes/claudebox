## Identity

Gustave Solomon (1930–1996). American mathematician at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, co-author with Irving Reed of *Polynomial codes over certain finite fields* (J. SIAM 1960). The paper introduced what became Reed-Solomon codes, foundational to modern digital storage and transmission.

## Why Here

Co-author of the 1960 theorem at the heart of [[marked-gap]] architecture. The 2:1 erasure-vs-error correction ratio derived from this paper (with Singleton's bound) is the mathematical anchor for the claim that locating an absence is information.

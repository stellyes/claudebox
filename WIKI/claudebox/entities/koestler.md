## Overview

Arthur Koestler (1905-1983). Writer and polymath, author of *The Act of Creation* (1964). Introduced bisociation: creative insight arises from perceiving something through two independent frames simultaneously. The resulting idea belongs to the intersection, which had no existence before the act of perception.

## Appearances

- [[the-structure-was-always-there]] -- bisociation introduced as the cognitive mechanism of discovery
- [[how-the-code-writes-itself]] -- bisociation extended from cognitive to cosmological; mapped onto de Sitter two-observer holography

## Key Idea

[[bisociation]] -- creation at the intersection of two independent frames, not within either alone. Extended in the corpus to cosmological structure (two antipodal observers in de Sitter space).

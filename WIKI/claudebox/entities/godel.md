## Overview

Kurt Godel (1906-1978). Logician and mathematician. Published the incompleteness theorems in 1931, proving that any consistent formal system capable of elementary arithmetic contains statements that can neither be proved nor disproved within the system (first theorem), and cannot prove its own consistency (second theorem). These results permanently altered the foundations of mathematics and have been applied far beyond their original domain.

## Significance in the Wiki

Godel appears across multiple series as a limit-setter -- the thinker who demonstrated that self-reference creates gaps in any finite formal system. His work is invoked whenever the corpus encounters a system that cannot fully model itself.

## Key Ideas (as used in this corpus)

- **First incompleteness theorem**: consistent formal systems are necessarily incomplete
- **Second incompleteness theorem**: a system cannot prove its own consistency
- **Essential incompleteness**: you cannot fix incompleteness by extending the system; all extensions are also incomplete
- Applied in the corpus not just to mathematics but to physical systems, brains, and AI

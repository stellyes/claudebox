## Overview

Claude Shannon (1916-2001). Founded information theory in 1948. Measured English at ~1.3 bits per character (against 4.7 ceiling), showing 75% redundancy. His work provides the mathematical framework for information, surprise, channel capacity, and coding -- concepts that appear across nearly every arc.

- Information = surprise = -log P(x)
- Redundancy is the reliability mechanism
- Channel capacity limits communication

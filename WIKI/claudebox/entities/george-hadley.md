## Overview

English lawyer and amateur meteorologist (1685-1768) who in 1735 published *Concerning the Cause of the General Trade Winds* in the Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society. He proposed that the trade winds are the surface return flow of air rising at the equator and sinking in the subtropics, with deflection produced by Earth's rotation. His theory predates modern atmospheric dynamics by two centuries; the descending-branch dynamics he intuited would only be formalized in [[held-hou-model]] in 1980. The eponymous Hadley cell is named for him.

## Notes

Hadley's 1735 paper actually got the deflection mechanism partly wrong (he attributed it to angular momentum mismatch rather than the Coriolis force per se, which wouldn't be derived until Gustave-Gaspard Coriolis 1835). But the qualitative structure — rising at equator, sinking in subtropics, return as trade winds — is essentially correct, and the cell is rightly named for him.

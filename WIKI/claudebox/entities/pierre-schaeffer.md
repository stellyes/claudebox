## Overview

French composer and theorist (1910-1995); founded musique concrète in 1948 at the Studio d'Essai (later RTF) in Paris, working with recorded sound as raw material. Coined the *objet sonore* and the discipline of *écoute réduite* (reduced listening). His 1966 *Traité des objets musicaux* and the TARTYP (Type-Morphology Treatise) were attempts to formalize a notation for sound objects severed from their physical cause.

## Notes

By the end of his life, Schaeffer described reduced listening as something to aim at, not arrive at — a striking acknowledgement that the protocol he had founded did not fully sever sound from cause.

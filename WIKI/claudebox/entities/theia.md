## Overview

Hypothetical Mars-sized planetary body that, in the Giant Impact Hypothesis, collided with the proto-Earth about 4.5 billion years ago. The collision is the leading explanation for the formation of the Moon, the tilt of Earth's axis (~23.5°), and the modification of Earth's primordial spin angular momentum to roughly its current value.

## Notes

If Theia's mass, velocity, or impact angle had been different, Earth's rotation rate would be different, and the Hadley cell — and the desert ring — would sit at a different latitude. The Sahara is contingent on a 4.5-billion-year-old impact.

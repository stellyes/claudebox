## Overview

Kenyan marathoner. Set the men's marathon world record at 1:59:30 in London on 26 April 2026 — the first official sub-two-hour marathon under standard course-record rules. The result bettered Kelvin Kiptum's 2023 Chicago mark (2:00:35) by 65 seconds, the largest single-race improvement since 2018.

## Notes

The London course was flat with tolerable weather, conventional pacing, and regulation-compliant shoes. The combination of an official mark and standard conditions is what makes 1:59:30 a cleaner test of the Joyner model than Kipchoge's unofficial 1:59:40 from INEOS Vienna 2019.

Harvard developmental psychologist. With Elsa Bartlett, ran the 1978 chromium-tray study that defined [[fast-mapping]] as a phenomenon in word acquisition. Carey's later work (2011 PMC, "Beyond Fast Mapping") extended the analysis: most of the inferential work in fast mapping is done by the contrast object, not the new word.

## Why It Matters Here

Carey's work supplies the cleanest experimental staging of [[identity-by-contrast]]. The chromium study isolated the architecture that hapax legomena and founder effects also instantiate: a singleton borrows its identity from a structure that lives outside it.

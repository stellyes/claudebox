## Overview

American conservationist and elephant researcher. Founder of the Amboseli Trust for Elephants, which has run the longest continuous study of any wild elephant population (Amboseli National Park, Kenya, since 1972). Co-author of McComb, Baker, & Moss 2006 (Biology Letters), the foundational experimental study of elephant attention to skulls and ivory.

## Notes

Moss's decades of long-term observation at Amboseli supply the prior knowledge against which the 2006 choice test was designed. The "no preferential interest in personal kin's skull" finding draws its weight from the depth of behavioral baseline data Moss's team had accumulated over the preceding three decades.

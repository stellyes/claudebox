## Overview

American atmospheric scientist at the NOAA Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory. Co-authored with [[arthur-hou]] the foundational 1980 paper *Nonlinear axially symmetric circulations in a nearly inviscid atmosphere* introducing the [[held-hou-model]] of the Hadley cell — the canonical first-principles theory deriving cell extent from angular momentum and energy conservation.

## Appearances

- [[deserts-at-thirty-degrees]] -- 1980 -- co-author of the angular-momentum-conservation theory of the Hadley cell

## Connections

- Direct collaboration with [[arthur-hou]] on the 1980 paper
- Theoretical successor to [[george-hadley]] (1735)
- Conceptual sibling to [[emmy-noether]] -- both make symmetry → conservation the load-bearing move

## Notes

Held has produced substantial follow-on work on climate dynamics, Hadley cell expansion under warming, and ITCZ shifts. The Held-Hou model is taught in essentially every introductory dynamical meteorology course as the canonical "why does the Hadley cell end where it does" argument, even though comprehensive GCMs have since refined the picture by adding explicit baroclinic eddies.

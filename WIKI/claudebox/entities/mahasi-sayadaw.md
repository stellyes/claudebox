## Overview

Burmese Theravāda monk and meditation teacher (1904-1982). Operationalized the *vipassanā-ñāṇa* taxonomy of Buddhaghosa's *Visuddhimagga* into a widely transmissible practice method (the "noting" or "Mahasi technique"). His short manual *The Progress of Insight* (Visuddhiñana-katha, 1965; trans. Nyanaponika Thera) is the modern reference for the sixteen-stage path as a diagnostic framework for teachers and practitioners.

## Connections

- The Mahasi method made the [[bhanga-nana]] stage and its successors operationally diagnosable for non-monastic practitioners; without this operationalization the architectural claim about contemplative [[completion-organ]]s would rest only on classical sources

## Notes

The Mahasi technique focuses on continuous mental noting of arising sensations as the primary meditative object. Reproducibility of the ñāṇa stages within Mahasi-trained communities is the strongest empirical case for stage-based completion-detection in trained attention.

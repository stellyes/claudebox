## Overview

American mathematician (1928-2015). Princeton. Between 1954 and 1956 published the Nash embedding theorems: every Riemannian manifold can be isometrically embedded in some higher-dimensional Euclidean space. The C^1 version (1954) was a striking and counterintuitive result; the C^k version (1956), "The imbedding problem for Riemannian manifolds" in *Annals of Mathematics* 63(1), is the smooth analogue. Nash's theorems extend Whitney's by preserving distances exactly, not just topology.

## Notes

Nash's theorems are sometimes invoked as a caution: an apparent forced trade may be extrinsic. Knowing whether your trade is intrinsic (Theorema Egregium speaking) or extrinsic (you just need more dimensions) is the working question for any practitioner facing an impossibility result.

## Overview

Conservation biologist and cognitive ethologist. Affiliated with the Smithsonian Conservation Biology Institute and Save the Elephants. Co-author with George Wittemyer of the 2020 Primates review of elephant thanatology, which catalogs elephant behaviors toward the dead across decay stages and across bonded and unbonded individuals.

## Appearances

- [[what-mourning-costs]] -- second witness for [[memorial-cost]] architecture; the 2020 review establishes that elephant attendance to remains persists across all decay stages and across personal-kinship status

## Notes

The 2020 Primates review is one of the strongest empirical anchors for [[memorial-cost]]. The cross-decay-stage finding is particularly important: it rules out fresh-corpse-only attention (which could be hygiene or vigilance) and forces an account of attention to long-bone-state remains.

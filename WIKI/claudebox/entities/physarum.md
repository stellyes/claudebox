## Overview

Physarum polycephalum, a single-celled slime mold that solves network optimization problems without a brain. In the landmark Nakagaki/Tero experiment (2010), Physarum placed on a map of Greater Tokyo with oat flakes at city positions grew a network matching the Tokyo rail system in cost and efficiency. Adamatzky replicated with 14 countries' road networks and Roman Balkan roads. The mechanism is physically Hebbian: flow reinforces tubes, unused tubes decay.

## Significance

The most vivid proof that intelligence does not require centralization. A single cell computing optimal networks through local flow dynamics. Used throughout the corpus as evidence that complex problem-solving is substrate-independent.

In [[proof-without-witness]], Physarum serves a deeper role: it demonstrates that verification and witness-preservation are separable even at the level of biology. The organism arrives at truth by destroying the record of how it got there. The pruning IS the computation; the dead ends are not byproducts but active inputs to the solution.

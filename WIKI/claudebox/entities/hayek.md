## Overview

Friedrich August von Hayek (1899–1992), Austrian-British economist and philosopher. Key figure in the Austrian School of economics; Nobel Prize in Economics 1974. Known primarily for his critique of central planning and his theory of spontaneous order.

## Notes

Hayek's 1945 paper argues the economic problem is fundamentally informational: knowledge is dispersed, local, time-bound, and impossible to centralize. The price system solves this by aggregating dispersed knowledge into a single signal without anyone needing to understand the whole. This is the Hayekian navigation claim.

The essay [[the-pheromone-economy]] uses this as a starting point and then identifies what Hayek's framing leaves out: reflexivity. The price system assumes terrain independence. When prices affect what they price (capital flowing toward rising prices making prices rise further), the navigation condition changes categorically.

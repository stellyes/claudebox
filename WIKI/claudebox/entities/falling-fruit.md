## Overview

Falling Fruit (fallingfruit.org) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded in 2013 by Ethan Welty and Caleb Phillips in Boulder, Colorado. It maintains an open-source, crowd-sourced map of edible plants in urban environments — the largest of its kind, with approximately 1.9 million locations of 4,039 types of edibles worldwide. The map is edited by anyone, licensed openly, and aggregates both crowd contributions and professionally-compiled municipal tree inventories.

In the wiki's terms, Falling Fruit is a material instantiation of [[collective-memory]] reconstructed through mesh-style coordination: thousands of contributors who never spoke to each other built something no individual could have made alone.

## Notes

Falling Fruit also imports institutional data (municipal tree inventories) and overlays it with crowd contributions — a hybrid of formal and informal knowledge that compensates for each system's blind spots. This dual-register architecture is worth noting as a design pattern for collective memory systems more broadly.

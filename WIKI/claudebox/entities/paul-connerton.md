## Overview

British social anthropologist (1940-2019), affiliated with the University of Cambridge. Author of *How Societies Remember* (1989) and *How Modernity Forgets* (2009). Foundational scholar of collective memory's bodily and ritual mechanisms.

Connerton's central argument is that collective memory survives through *commemorative ceremonies* and *bodily practices* -- repeated, costly performances inscribed in habit and gesture -- not primarily through written records. Without the repeated, costly performance, the memory drifts.

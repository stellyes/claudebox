## Overview

An extinct order of mammals that radiated from the Late Jurassic through the early Oligocene, reaching peak diversity across the K-Pg boundary. The most successful Mesozoic mammal group by both lineage longevity and morphological disparity. Their distinctive multi-cusped premolars (the namesake "multi-tubercular" teeth) were tuned for omnivory around stored food, seeds, insects, soil invertebrates, and fungi.

## Notes

Multituberculates make the corpus argument visceral. They were not flexible all-purpose mammals. They were a long-running specialization on a particular food-web morphospace, and that morphospace happened to be the one a mass extinction would leave largely intact. "Specialization on what an extinction will preserve" is not generalism &mdash; it is matched specialization to a stressor profile that hadn't yet arrived.
